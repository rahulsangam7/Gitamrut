# Bhagavad Gita - Sanskrit With Simple English Translation

Style: simple meaning-focused English, paired with the Sanskrit source.
Verse-count note: This follows the Sanskrit source file and includes BG 13.1, so it has 701 verse entries.

## Chapter 1 - Arjuna Visada Yoga

### 1.1

**Sanskrit**

धृतराष्ट्र उवाच ।
धर्मक्षेत्रे कुरुक्षेत्रे समवेता युयुत्सवः ।
मामकाः पाण्डवाश्चैव किमकुर्वत सञ्जय ॥१-१॥

**Simple English**

Dhritarashtra said: Sanjaya, my sons and the sons of Pandu have gathered at Kurukshetra, the field of dharma, ready for war. What did they do?

### 1.2

**Sanskrit**

सञ्जय उवाच ।
दृष्ट्वा तु पाण्डवानीकं व्यूढं दुर्योधनस्तदा ।
आचार्यमुपसंगम्य राजा वचनमब्रवीत् ॥१-२॥

**Simple English**

Sanjaya said: Seeing the Pandava army arranged for battle, King Duryodhana went to his teacher Drona and spoke these words.

### 1.3

**Sanskrit**

पश्यैतां पाण्डुपुत्राणामाचार्य महतीं चमूम् ।
व्यूढां द्रुपदपुत्रेण तव शिष्येण धीमता ॥१-३॥

**Simple English**

Duryodhana said: Teacher, look at this mighty army of the sons of Pandu, arranged by your wise student, the son of Drupada.

### 1.4

**Sanskrit**

अत्र शूरा महेष्वासा भीमार्जुनसमा युधि ।
युयुधानो विराटश्च द्रुपदश्च महारथः ॥१-४॥

**Simple English**

Here are heroic archers, equal in battle to Bhima and Arjuna: Yuyudhana, Virata, and Drupada, all great warriors.

### 1.5

**Sanskrit**

धृष्टकेतुश्चेकितानः काशिराजश्च वीर्यवान् ।
पुरुजित्कुन्तिभोजश्च शैब्यश्च नरपुंगवः ॥१-५॥

**Simple English**

There are also Dhrishtaketu, Chekitana, the brave king of Kashi, Purujit, Kuntibhoja, and Shaibya, all excellent men.

### 1.6

**Sanskrit**

युधामन्युश्च विक्रान्त उत्तमौजाश्च वीर्यवान् ।
सौभद्रो द्रौपदेयाश्च सर्व एव महारथाः ॥१-६॥

**Simple English**

There are the strong Yudhamanyu, the brave Uttamauja, Abhimanyu the son of Subhadra, and the sons of Draupadi, all great warriors.

### 1.7

**Sanskrit**

अस्माकं तु विशिष्टा ये तान्निबोध द्विजोत्तम ।
नायका मम सैन्यस्य संज्ञार्थं तान्ब्रवीमि ते ॥१-७॥

**Simple English**

Now, best of teachers, hear also about the important leaders on our side. I will name the commanders of my army for you.

### 1.8

**Sanskrit**

भवान्भीष्मश्च कर्णश्च कृपश्च समितिञ्जयः ।
अश्वत्थामा विकर्णश्च सौमदत्तिस्तथैव च ॥१-८॥

**Simple English**

There are you yourself, Bhishma, Karna, victorious Kripa, Ashvatthama, Vikarna, and Bhurishrava, the son of Somadatta.

### 1.9

**Sanskrit**

अन्ये च बहवः शूरा मदर्थे त्यक्तजीविताः ।
नानाशस्त्रप्रहरणाः सर्वे युद्धविशारदाः ॥१-९॥

**Simple English**

Many other heroes are also ready to give their lives for me. They are armed with many weapons and skilled in battle.

### 1.10

**Sanskrit**

अपर्याप्तं तदस्माकं बलं भीष्माभिरक्षितम् ।
पर्याप्तं त्विदमेतेषां बलं भीमाभिरक्षितम् ॥१-१०॥

**Simple English**

Our army, protected by Bhishma, is called aparyaptam; their army, protected by Bhima, is called paryaptam. These words can be read as insufficient/sufficient or vast/limited, so the verse is kept neutral.

### 1.11

**Sanskrit**

अयनेषु च सर्वेषु यथाभागमवस्थिताः ।
भीष्ममेवाभिरक्षन्तु भवन्तः सर्व एव हि ॥१-११॥

**Simple English**

So all of you, standing in your proper places in every division of the army, must especially protect Bhishma.

### 1.12

**Sanskrit**

तस्य सञ्जनयन्हर्षं कुरुवृद्धः पितामहः ।
सिंहनादं विनद्योच्चैः शङ्खं दध्मौ प्रतापवान् ॥१-१२॥

**Simple English**

Then Bhishma, the great elder of the Kauravas, roared like a lion and blew his conch to cheer Duryodhana.

### 1.13

**Sanskrit**

ततः शङ्खाश्च भेर्यश्च पणवानकगोमुखाः ।
सहसैवाभ्यहन्यन्त स शब्दस्तुमुलोऽभवत् ॥१-१३॥

**Simple English**

At once conches, drums, kettledrums, trumpets, and horns sounded together. The noise was tremendous.

### 1.14

**Sanskrit**

ततः श्वेतैर्हयैर्युक्ते महति स्यन्दने स्थितौ ।
माधवः पाण्डवश्चैव दिव्यौ शङ्खौ प्रदध्मतुः ॥१-१४॥

**Simple English**

Then Krishna and Arjuna, seated in a grand chariot drawn by white horses, blew their divine conches.

### 1.15

**Sanskrit**

पाञ्चजन्यं हृषीकेशो देवदत्तं धनञ्जयः ।
पौण्ड्रं दध्मौ महाशङ्खं भीमकर्मा वृकोदरः ॥१-१५॥

**Simple English**

Krishna blew Panchajanya, Arjuna blew Devadatta, and mighty Bhima blew the great conch Paundra.

### 1.16

**Sanskrit**

अनन्तविजयं राजा कुन्तीपुत्रो युधिष्ठिरः ।
नकुलः सहदेवश्च सुघोषमणिपुष्पकौ ॥१-१६॥

**Simple English**

King Yudhishthira, son of Kunti, blew Anantavijaya; Nakula and Sahadeva blew Sughosha and Manipushpaka.

### 1.17

**Sanskrit**

काश्यश्च परमेष्वासः शिखण्डी च महारथः ।
धृष्टद्युम्नो विराटश्च सात्यकिश्चापराजितः ॥१-१७॥

**Simple English**

The king of Kashi, the great archer Shikhandi, Dhrishtadyumna, Virata, and undefeated Satyaki also blew their conches.

### 1.18

**Sanskrit**

द्रुपदो द्रौपदेयाश्च सर्वशः पृथिवीपते ।
सौभद्रश्च महाबाहुः शङ्खान्दध्मुः पृथक्पृथक् ॥१-१८॥

**Simple English**

Drupada, the sons of Draupadi, and mighty Abhimanyu, son of Subhadra, each blew their own conch.

### 1.19

**Sanskrit**

स घोषो धार्तराष्ट्राणां हृदयानि व्यदारयत् ।
नभश्च पृथिवीं चैव तुमुलोऽभ्यनुनादयन् (or लोव्यनु) ॥१-१९॥

**Simple English**

That huge sound shook the hearts of Dhritarashtra’s sons and echoed through heaven and earth.

### 1.20

**Sanskrit**

अथ व्यवस्थितान्दृष्ट्वा धार्तराष्ट्रान् कपिध्वजः ।
प्रवृत्ते शस्त्रसम्पाते धनुरुद्यम्य पाण्डवः ।
हृषीकेशं तदा वाक्यमिदमाह महीपते ॥१-२०॥

**Simple English**

Then Arjuna, whose flag bore Hanuman, saw Dhritarashtra’s sons ready for battle. As the fighting was about to begin, he lifted his bow and spoke to Krishna.

### 1.21

**Sanskrit**

अर्जुन उवाच ।
सेनयोरुभयोर्मध्ये रथं स्थापय मेऽच्युत ॥१-२१॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: O Krishna, please place my chariot between the two armies.

### 1.22

**Sanskrit**

यावदेतान्निरीक्षेऽहं योद्धुकामानवस्थितान् ।
कैर्मया सह योद्धव्यमस्मिन् रणसमुद्यमे ॥१-२२॥

**Simple English**

Let me look at the warriors standing here, eager to fight, and see whom I must face in this battle.

### 1.23

**Sanskrit**

योत्स्यमानानवेक्षेऽहं य एतेऽत्र समागताः ।
धार्तराष्ट्रस्य दुर्बुद्धेर्युद्धे प्रियचिकीर्षवः ॥१-२३॥

**Simple English**

I want to see those who have gathered here to fight, wishing to please the wrong-minded Duryodhana.

### 1.24

**Sanskrit**

सञ्जय उवाच ।
एवमुक्तो हृषीकेशो गुडाकेशेन भारत ।
सेनयोरुभयोर्मध्ये स्थापयित्वा रथोत्तमम् ॥१-२४॥

**Simple English**

Sanjaya said: O Dhritarashtra, after Arjuna said this, Krishna placed the best of chariots between the two armies.

### 1.25

**Sanskrit**

भीष्मद्रोणप्रमुखतः सर्वेषां च महीक्षिताम् ।
उवाच पार्थ पश्यैतान्समवेतान्कुरूनिति ॥१-२५॥

**Simple English**

In front of Bhishma, Drona, and all the kings, Krishna said, “Arjuna, see these Kurus gathered here.”

### 1.26

**Sanskrit**

तत्रापश्यत्स्थितान्पार्थः पितॄनथ पितामहान् ।
आचार्यान्मातुलान्भ्रातॄन्पुत्रान्पौत्रान्सखींस्तथा ॥१-२६॥

**Simple English**

There Arjuna saw fathers, grandfathers, teachers, uncles, brothers, sons, grandsons, and friends standing in both armies.

### 1.27

**Sanskrit**

श्वशुरान्सुहृदश्चैव सेनयोरुभयोरपि ।
तान्समीक्ष्य स कौन्तेयः सर्वान्बन्धूनवस्थितान् ॥१-२७॥

**Simple English**

He also saw fathers-in-law and companions on both sides. Seeing all his relatives lined up, Arjuna was filled with deep pity and sorrow.

### 1.28

**Sanskrit**

कृपया परयाविष्टो विषीदन्निदमब्रवीत् ।
अर्जुन उवाच ।
दृष्ट्वेमं स्वजनं कृष्ण युयुत्सुं समुपस्थितम् ॥१-२८॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: O Krishna, seeing my own relatives gathered here and eager to fight,

### 1.29

**Sanskrit**

सीदन्ति मम गात्राणि मुखं च परिशुष्यति ।
वेपथुश्च शरीरे मे रोमहर्षश्च जायते ॥१-२९॥

**Simple English**

my limbs become weak, my mouth dries up, my body trembles, and my hair stands on end.

### 1.30

**Sanskrit**

गाण्डीवं स्रंसते हस्तात्त्वक्चैव परिदह्यते ।
न च शक्नोम्यवस्थातुं भ्रमतीव च मे मनः ॥१-३०॥

**Simple English**

My bow Gandiva slips from my hand, my skin burns, I cannot stand steady, and my mind seems to spin.

### 1.31

**Sanskrit**

निमित्तानि च पश्यामि विपरीतानि केशव ।
न च श्रेयोऽनुपश्यामि हत्वा स्वजनमाहवे ॥१-३१॥

**Simple English**

I see bad signs, O Krishna. I do not see any good in killing my own relatives in battle.

### 1.32

**Sanskrit**

न काङ्क्षे विजयं कृष्ण न च राज्यं सुखानि च ।
किं नो राज्येन गोविन्द किं भोगैर्जीवितेन वा ॥१-३२॥

**Simple English**

I do not want victory, kingdom, or pleasures, O Krishna. What use are kingdom, enjoyment, or even life to us now?

### 1.33

**Sanskrit**

येषामर्थे काङ्क्षितं नो राज्यं भोगाः सुखानि च ।
त इमेऽवस्थिता युद्धे प्राणांस्त्यक्त्वा धनानि च ॥१-३३॥

**Simple English**

Those for whose sake we desire kingdom, enjoyments and pleasures, stand here in battle, having renounced life and wealth.

### 1.34

**Sanskrit**

आचार्याः पितरः पुत्रास्तथैव च पितामहाः ।
मातुलाः श्वशुराः पौत्राः श्यालाः सम्बन्धिनस्तथा ॥१-३४॥

**Simple English**

Teachers, fathers, sons and also grandfathers, maternal uncles, fathers-in-law, grandsons, brothers-in-law and other relatives,-.

### 1.35

**Sanskrit**

एतान्न हन्तुमिच्छामि घ्नतोऽपि मधुसूदन ।
अपि त्रैलोक्यराज्यस्य हेतोः किं नु महीकृते ॥१-३५॥

**Simple English**

These I do not wish to kill, though they kill me, O Krishna, even for the sake of dominion over the three worlds; leave alone killing them for the sake of the earth.

### 1.36

**Sanskrit**

निहत्य धार्तराष्ट्रान्नः का प्रीतिः स्याज्जनार्दन ।
पापमेवाश्रयेदस्मान्हत्वैतानाततायिनः ॥१-३६॥

**Simple English**

By killing these sons of Dhritarashtra, what pleasure can be ours, O Janardana? Only wrongdoing will accrue to us from killing these felons.

### 1.37

**Sanskrit**

तस्मान्नार्हा वयं हन्तुं धार्तराष्ट्रान्स्वबान्धवान् ।
स्वजनं हि कथं हत्वा सुखिनः स्याम माधव ॥१-३७॥

**Simple English**

Therefore, we should not kill the sons of Dhritarashtra, our relatives; for how can we be happy by killing our own people, O Madhava (Krishna)?

### 1.38

**Sanskrit**

यद्यप्येते न पश्यन्ति लोभोपहतचेतसः ।
कुलक्षयकृतं दोषं मित्रद्रोहे च पातकम् ॥१-३८॥

**Simple English**

Though they, with intelligence overpowered by greed, see no evil in the destruction of families, and no wrongdoing in hostility to friends,.

### 1.39

**Sanskrit**

कथं न ज्ञेयमस्माभिः पापादस्मान्निवर्तितुम् ।
कुलक्षयकृतं दोषं प्रपश्यद्भिर्जनार्दन ॥१-३९॥

**Simple English**

Why should not we who clearly see evil in the destruction of families, learn to turn away from this wrongdoing, O Janardana (Krishna)?

### 1.40

**Sanskrit**

कुलक्षये प्रणश्यन्ति कुलधर्माः सनातनाः ।
धर्मे नष्टे कुलं कृत्स्नमधर्मोऽभिभवत्युत ॥१-४०॥

**Simple English**

In the destruction of a family, the immemorial religious rites of that family perish; on the destruction of spirituality, impiety, indeed, overcomes the whole family.

### 1.41

**Sanskrit**

अधर्माभिभवात्कृष्ण प्रदुष्यन्ति कुलस्त्रियः ।
स्त्रीषु दुष्टासु वार्ष्णेय जायते वर्णसङ्करः ॥१-४१॥

**Simple English**

By the prevalence of impiety, O Krishna, the women of the family become corrupt; and, women being corrupted, O Varshenya (descendant of Vrishni), there arises intermingling of castes.

### 1.42

**Sanskrit**

सङ्करो नरकायैव कुलघ्नानां कुलस्य च ।
पतन्ति पितरो ह्येषां लुप्तपिण्डोदकक्रियाः ॥१-४२॥

**Simple English**

Confusion of castes leads to hell the slayers of the family, for their forefathers fall, deprived of the offerings of rice-ball and water (libations).

### 1.43

**Sanskrit**

दोषैरेतैः कुलघ्नानां वर्णसङ्करकारकैः ।
उत्साद्यन्ते जातिधर्माः कुलधर्माश्च शाश्वताः ॥१-४३॥

**Simple English**

By these evil deeds of the destroyers of the family, which cause confusion of castes, the eternal religious rites of the caste and the family are destroyed.

### 1.44

**Sanskrit**

उत्सन्नकुलधर्माणां मनुष्याणां जनार्दन ।
नरके नियतं वासो भवतीत्यनुशुश्रुम (or नरकेऽनियतं) ॥१-४४॥

**Simple English**

We have heard, O Janardana, that inevitable is the dwelling for an unknown period in hell for those men in whose families the religious practices have been destroyed.

### 1.45

**Sanskrit**

अहो बत महत्पापं कर्तुं व्यवसिता वयम् ।
यद्राज्यसुखलोभेन हन्तुं स्वजनमुद्यताः ॥१-४५॥

**Simple English**

Alas! We are involved in a great wrongdoing, in that we are prepared to kill our kinsmen, through greed for the pleasures of a kingdom.

### 1.46

**Sanskrit**

यदि मामप्रतीकारमशस्त्रं शस्त्रपाणयः ।
धार्तराष्ट्रा रणे हन्युस्तन्मे क्षेमतरं भवेत् ॥१-४६॥

**Simple English**

If the sons of Dhritarashtra with weapons in hand should kill me in battle, unresisting and unarmed, that would be better for me.

### 1.47

**Sanskrit**

सञ्जय उवाच ।
एवमुक्त्वार्जुनः सङ्ख्ये रथोपस्थ उपाविशत् ।
विसृज्य सशरं चापं शोकसंविग्नमानसः ॥१-४७॥

**Simple English**

Sanjaya said: Having thus spoken in the midst of the battlefield, Arjuna, casting away his bow and arrow, sat down on the seat of the chariot with his mind overwhelmed with sorrow.

## Chapter 2 - Sankhya Yoga

### 2.1

**Sanskrit**

सञ्जय उवाच ।
तं तथा कृपयाविष्टमश्रुपूर्णाकुलेक्षणम् ।
विषीदन्तमिदं वाक्यमुवाच मधुसूदनः ॥२-१॥

**Simple English**

Sanjaya said: To him who was thus overcome with pity and who was despondent, with eyes full of tears and agitated, Madhusudana (the destroyer of Madhu) or Krishna spoke these words.

### 2.2

**Sanskrit**

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
कुतस्त्वा कश्मलमिदं विषमे समुपस्थितम् ।
अनार्यजुष्टमस्वर्ग्यमकीर्तिकरमर्जुन ॥२-२॥

**Simple English**

Sri Krishna said: Arjuna, how has this weakness come to you at such a serious moment? It is not noble, it does not bring honor, and it does not lead to heaven.

### 2.3

**Sanskrit**

क्लैब्यं मा स्म गमः पार्थ नैतत्त्वय्युपपद्यते ।

### 2.6

### 2.7

**Simple English**

### 2.8

### 2.9

### 2.12

### 2.13

### 2.16

### 2.17

### 2.19

### 2.51

**Sanskrit**

कर्मजं बुद्धियुक्ता हि फलं त्यक्त्वा मनीषिणः ।
जन्मबन्धविनिर्मुक्ताः पदं गच्छन्त्यनामयम् ॥२-५१॥

**Simple English**

The wise, possessed of knowledge, having abandoned the fruits of their actions, and being freed from the fetters of birth, go to the place which is beyond all evil.

### 2.52

**Sanskrit**

यदा ते मोहकलिलं बुद्धिर्व्यतितरिष्यति ।
तदा गन्तासि निर्वेदं श्रोतव्यस्य श्रुतस्य च ॥२-५२॥

**Simple English**

When your intellect crosses beyond the mire of confusion, then you will attain to indifference as to what has been heard and what has yet to be heard.

### 2.53

**Sanskrit**

श्रुतिविप्रतिपन्ना ते यदा स्थास्यति निश्चला ।
समाधावचला बुद्धिस्तदा योगमवाप्स्यसि ॥२-५३॥

**Simple English**

When your intellect, which is perplexed by the Veda text, which you have read, shall stand immovable and steady in the Self, then you will attain Self-realisation.

### 2.54

**Sanskrit**

अर्जुन उवाच ।
स्थितप्रज्ञस्य का भाषा समाधिस्थस्य केशव ।
स्थितधीः किं प्रभाषेत किमासीत व्रजेत किम् ॥२-५४॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: What, O Krishna, is the description of him who has steady wisdom, and is merged in the superconscious state? How does one of steady wisdom speak, how does he sit, how does he walk?

### 2.55

**Sanskrit**

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
प्रजहाति यदा कामान्सर्वान्पार्थ मनोगतान् ।
आत्मन्येवात्मना तुष्टः स्थितप्रज्ञस्तदोच्यते ॥२-५५॥

**Simple English**

Sri Krishna said: When a man completely casts off, O Arjuna, all the desires of the mind and is satisfied in the Self by the Self, then is he said to be one of steady wisdom.

### 2.56

**Sanskrit**

दुःखेष्वनुद्विग्नमनाः सुखेषु विगतस्पृहः ।
वीतरागभयक्रोधः स्थितधीर्मुनिरुच्यते ॥२-५६॥

**Simple English**

He whose mind is not shaken by adversity, who does not hanker after pleasures, and is free from attachment, fear and anger, is called a sage of steady wisdom.

### 2.57

**Sanskrit**

यः सर्वत्रानभिस्नेहस्तत्तत्प्राप्य शुभाशुभम् ।
नाभिनन्दति न द्वेष्टि तस्य प्रज्ञा प्रतिष्ठिता ॥२-५७॥

**Simple English**

He who is everywhere without attachment, on meeting with anything good or bad, who neither rejoices not hastes, his wisdom is fixed.

### 2.58

**Sanskrit**

यदा संहरते चायं कूर्मोऽङ्गानीव सर्वशः ।
इन्द्रियाणीन्द्रियार्थेभ्यस्तस्य प्रज्ञा प्रतिष्ठिता ॥२-५८॥

**Simple English**

When, like the tortoise which withdraws on all sides its limbs, he withdraws his senses from the sense-objects, then his wisdom becomes steady.

### 2.59

**Sanskrit**

विषया विनिवर्तन्ते निराहारस्य देहिनः ।
रसवर्जं रसोऽप्यस्य परं दृष्ट्वा निवर्तते ॥२-५९॥

**Simple English**

The objects of the senses turn away from the abstinent man leaving the longing (behind); but his longing also turns away on seeing the Supreme.

### 2.60

**Sanskrit**

यततो ह्यपि कौन्तेय पुरुषस्य विपश्चितः ।
इन्द्रियाणि प्रमाथीनि हरन्ति प्रसभं मनः ॥२-६०॥

**Simple English**

The turbulent senses, O Arjuna, do violently carry away the mind of a wise man though he be striving (to control them).

### 2.61

**Sanskrit**

तानि सर्वाणि संयम्य युक्त आसीत मत्परः ।
वशे हि यस्येन्द्रियाणि तस्य प्रज्ञा प्रतिष्ठिता ॥२-६१॥

**Simple English**

Having restrained them all he should sit steady, intent on Me; his wisdom is steady whose senses are under control.

### 2.62

**Sanskrit**

ध्यायतो विषयान्पुंसः सङ्गस्तेषूपजायते ।
सङ्गात्सञ्जायते कामः कामात्क्रोधोऽभिजायते ॥२-६२॥

**Simple English**

When a man thinks of the objects, attachment for them arises; from attachment desire is born; from desire anger arises.

### 2.63

**Sanskrit**

क्रोधाद्भवति सम्मोहः सम्मोहात्स्मृतिविभ्रमः ।
स्मृतिभ्रंशाद् बुद्धिनाशो बुद्धिनाशात्प्रणश्यति ॥२-६३॥

**Simple English**

From anger comes confusion; from confusion loss of memory; from loss of memory the destruction of clear understanding; from the destruction of clear understanding he perishes.

### 2.64

**Sanskrit**

रागद्वेषविमुक्तैस्तु विषयानिन्द्रियैश्चरन् । (or वियुक्तैस्तु)
आत्मवश्यैर्विधेयात्मा प्रसादमधिगच्छति ॥२-६४॥

**Simple English**

But the self-controlled man, moving among the objects with the senses under restraint and free from attraction and repulsion, attains to peace.

### 2.65

**Sanskrit**

प्रसादे सर्वदुःखानां हानिरस्योपजायते ।
प्रसन्नचेतसो ह्याशु बुद्धिः पर्यवतिष्ठते ॥२-६५॥

**Simple English**

In that peace all pains are destroyed; for the intellect of the tranil-minded soon becomes steady.

### 2.66

**Sanskrit**

नास्ति बुद्धिरयुक्तस्य न चायुक्तस्य भावना ।
न चाभावयतः शान्तिरशान्तस्य कुतः सुखम् ॥२-६६॥

**Simple English**

There is no knowledge of the Self to the unsteady and to the unsteady no meditation is possible, and to the unmeditative there can be no peace, and to the man who has no peace, how can there be happiness?

### 2.67

**Sanskrit**

इन्द्रियाणां हि चरतां यन्मनोऽनुविधीयते ।
तदस्य हरति प्रज्ञां वायुर्नावमिवाम्भसि ॥२-६७॥

**Simple English**

For the mind, which follows in the wake of the wandering senses, carries away his clear understanding, as the wind (carries away) a boat on the waters.

### 2.68

**Sanskrit**

तस्माद्यस्य महाबाहो निगृहीतानि सर्वशः ।
इन्द्रियाणीन्द्रियार्थेभ्यस्तस्य प्रज्ञा प्रतिष्ठिता ॥२-६८॥

**Simple English**

Therefore, O mighty-armed Arjuna, his knowledge is steady whose senses are completely restrained from sense-objects.

### 2.69

**Sanskrit**

या निशा सर्वभूतानां तस्यां जागर्ति संयमी ।
यस्यां जाग्रति भूतानि सा निशा पश्यतो मुनेः ॥२-६९॥

**Simple English**

That which is night to all beings, in that the self-controlled man is awake; when all beings are awake, that is night for the Muni (sage) who sees.

### 2.70

**Sanskrit**

आपूर्यमाणमचलप्रतिष्ठं
समुद्रमापः प्रविशन्ति यद्वत् ।
तद्वत्कामा यं प्रविशन्ति सर्वे
स शान्तिमाप्नोति न कामकामी ॥२-७०॥

**Simple English**

He attains peace into whom all desires enter as waters enter the ocean which, filled from all sides, remains unmoved; but not the man who is full of desires.

### 2.71

**Sanskrit**

विहाय कामान्यः सर्वान्पुमांश्चरति निःस्पृहः ।
निर्ममो निरहङ्कारः स शान्तिमधिगच्छति ॥२-७१॥

**Simple English**

That man attains peace who, abandoning all desires, moves about without longing, without the sense of mine and without ego.

### 2.72

**Sanskrit**

एषा ब्राह्मी स्थितिः पार्थ नैनां प्राप्य विमुह्यति ।
स्थित्वास्यामन्तकालेऽपि ब्रह्मनिर्वाणमृच्छति ॥२-७२॥

**Simple English**

This is the Brahmic seat (eternal state), O son of Pritha. Attaining to this, none is confused. Being established in it, even at the end of life, one attains to oneness with the Supreme Reality.

## Chapter 3 - Karma Yoga

### 3.1

**Sanskrit**

अर्जुन उवाच ।
ज्यायसी चेत्कर्मणस्ते मता बुद्धिर्जनार्दन ।
तत्किं कर्मणि घोरे मां नियोजयसि केशव ॥३-१॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: If You think that knowledge is superior to action, O Krishna, why then, O Keshava, do You ask me to engage in this terrible action?

### 3.2

**Sanskrit**

व्यामिश्रेणेव वाक्येन बुद्धिं मोहयसीव मे ।
तदेकं वद निश्चित्य येन श्रेयोऽहमाप्नुयाम् ॥३-२॥

**Simple English**

With this apparently perplexing speech, You confuse, as it were, my understanding; therefore tell me that one way for certain by which I may attain bliss.

### 3.3

**Sanskrit**

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
लोकेऽस्मिन् द्विविधा निष्ठा पुरा प्रोक्ता मयानघ ।
ज्ञानयोगेन साङ्ख्यानां कर्मयोगेन योगिनाम् ॥३-३॥

**Simple English**

Sri Krishna said: In this world there is a twofold path, as I said before, O pure one; the path of knowledge of the Sankhyas and the path of action of the Yogis.

### 3.4

**Sanskrit**

न कर्मणामनारम्भान्नैष्कर्म्यं पुरुषोऽश्नुते ।
न च संन्यसनादेव सिद्धिं समधिगच्छति ॥३-४॥

**Simple English**

Not by non-performance of actions does man reach actionlessness; nor by mere renunciation does he attain to perfection.

### 3.5

**Sanskrit**

न हि कश्चित्क्षणमपि जातु तिष्ठत्यकर्मकृत् ।
कार्यते ह्यवशः कर्म सर्वः प्रकृतिजैर्गुणैः ॥३-५॥

**Simple English**

Truly none can ever remain for even a moment without performing action; for everyone is made to act helplessly indeed by the qualities born of Nature.

### 3.6

**Sanskrit**

कर्मेन्द्रियाणि संयम्य य आस्ते मनसा स्मरन् ।
इन्द्रियार्थान्विमूढात्मा मिथ्याचारः स उच्यते ॥३-६॥

**Simple English**

He who, restraining the organs of action, sits thinking of the sense-objects in mind, he of confused understanding is called a hypocrite.

### 3.7

**Sanskrit**

यस्त्विन्द्रियाणि मनसा नियम्यारभतेऽर्जुन ।
कर्मेन्द्रियैः कर्मयोगमसक्तः स विशिष्यते ॥३-७॥

**Simple English**

But whoever, controlling the senses by the mind, O Arjuna, engages himself in Karma Yoga with the organs of action, without attachment, he excels.

### 3.8

**Sanskrit**

नियतं कुरु कर्म त्वं कर्म ज्यायो ह्यकर्मणः ।
शरीरयात्रापि च ते न प्रसिद्ध्येदकर्मणः ॥३-८॥

**Simple English**

Do you perform (your) proper duty, for action is superior to inaction and even the maintenance of the body would not be possible for you by inaction.

### 3.9

**Sanskrit**

यज्ञार्थात्कर्मणोऽन्यत्र लोकोऽयं कर्मबन्धनः ।
तदर्थं कर्म कौन्तेय मुक्तसङ्गः समाचर ॥३-९॥

**Simple English**

The world is bound by actions other than those performed for the sake of sacrifice; do you, therefore, O son of Kunti (Arjuna), perform action for that sake (for sacrifice alone), free from attachment.

### 3.10

**Sanskrit**

सहयज्ञाः प्रजाः सृष्ट्वा पुरोवाच प्रजापतिः ।
अनेन प्रसविष्यध्वमेष वोऽस्त्विष्टकामधुक् ॥३-१०॥

**Simple English**

The Creator, having in the beginning (of creation) created mankind together with sacrifice, said, "By this shall you propagate; let this be the milch cow of your desires (the cow which yields all the desired objects).".

### 3.11

**Sanskrit**

देवान्भावयतानेन ते देवा भावयन्तु वः ।
परस्परं भावयन्तः श्रेयः परमवाप्स्यथ ॥३-११॥

**Simple English**

With this do you nourish the gods and may those gods nourish you; thus nourishing one another, you shall attain to the highest good.

### 3.12

**Sanskrit**

इष्टान्भोगान्हि वो देवा दास्यन्ते यज्ञभाविताः ।
तैर्दत्तानप्रदायैभ्यो यो भुङ्क्ते स्तेन एव सः ॥३-१२॥

**Simple English**

The gods, nourished by the sacrifice, will give you the desired objects. So, he who enjoys the objects given by the gods without offering (in return) to them, is truly a thief.

### 3.13

**Sanskrit**

यज्ञशिष्टाशिनः सन्तो मुच्यन्ते सर्वकिल्बिषैः ।
भुञ्जते ते त्वघं पापा ये पचन्त्यात्मकारणात् ॥३-१३॥

**Simple English**

The righteous who eat the remnants of the sacrifice are freed from all wrongs; but those wrong ones who cook food (only) for their own sake truly eat wrongdoing.

### 3.14

**Sanskrit**

अन्नाद्भवन्ति भूतानि पर्जन्यादन्नसम्भवः ।
यज्ञाद्भवति पर्जन्यो यज्ञः कर्मसमुद्भवः ॥३-१४॥

**Simple English**

From food come forth beings; from rain food is produced; from sacrifice arises rain and sacrifice is born of action.

### 3.15

**Sanskrit**

कर्म ब्रह्मोद्भवं विद्धि ब्रह्माक्षरसमुद्भवम् ।
तस्मात्सर्वगतं ब्रह्म नित्यं यज्ञे प्रतिष्ठितम् ॥३-१५॥

**Simple English**

Know you that action comes from Brahma and Brahma comes from the Imperishable. Therefore, the all-pervading (Brahma) ever rests in sacrifice.

### 3.16

**Sanskrit**

एवं प्रवर्तितं चक्रं नानुवर्तयतीह यः ।
अघायुरिन्द्रियारामो मोघं पार्थ स जीवति ॥३-१६॥

**Simple English**

He who does not follow here the wheel thus set revolving, who is of wrong life, rejoicing in the senses, he lives in vain, O Arjuna.

### 3.17

**Sanskrit**

यस्त्वात्मरतिरेव स्यादात्मतृप्तश्च मानवः ।
आत्मन्येव च सन्तुष्टस्तस्य कार्यं न विद्यते ॥३-१७॥

**Simple English**

But for that man who rejoices only in the Self, who is satisfied with the Self and who is content in the Self alone, truly there is nothing to do.

### 3.18

**Sanskrit**

नैव तस्य कृतेनार्थो नाकृतेनेह कश्चन ।
न चास्य सर्वभूतेषु कश्चिदर्थव्यपाश्रयः ॥३-१८॥

**Simple English**

For him there is no interest whatever in what is done or what is not done; nor does he depend on any being for any object.

### 3.19

**Sanskrit**

तस्मादसक्तः सततं कार्यं कर्म समाचर ।
असक्तो ह्याचरन्कर्म परमाप्नोति पूरुषः ॥३-१९॥

**Simple English**

Therefore without attachment, do you always perform action which should be done; for by performing action without attachment man reaches the Supreme.

### 3.20

**Sanskrit**

कर्मणैव हि संसिद्धिमास्थिता जनकादयः ।
लोकसंग्रहमेवापि सम्पश्यन्कर्तुमर्हसि ॥३-२०॥

**Simple English**

Janaka and others attained perfection truly by action only; even with a view to the protection of the masses you should perform action.

### 3.21

**Sanskrit**

यद्यदाचरति श्रेष्ठस्तत्तदेवेतरो जनः ।
स यत्प्रमाणं कुरुते लोकस्तदनुवर्तते ॥३-२१॥

**Simple English**

Whatsoever a great man does, that the other men also do; whatever he sets up as the standard, that the world (mankind) follows.

### 3.22

**Sanskrit**

न मे पार्थास्ति कर्तव्यं त्रिषु लोकेषु किञ्चन ।
नानवाप्तमवाप्तव्यं वर्त एव च कर्मणि ॥३-२२॥

**Simple English**

There is nothing in the three worlds, O Arjuna, that should be done by Me, nor is there anything unattained that should be attained; yet I engage Myself in action.

### 3.23

**Sanskrit**

यदि ह्यहं न वर्तेयं जातु कर्मण्यतन्द्रितः ।
मम वर्त्मानुवर्तन्ते मनुष्याः पार्थ सर्वशः ॥३-२३॥

**Simple English**

For, should I not ever engage Myself in action, unwearied, men would in every way follow My path, O Arjuna.

### 3.24

**Sanskrit**

उत्सीदेयुरिमे लोका न कुर्यां कर्म चेदहम् ।
सङ्करस्य च कर्ता स्यामुपहन्यामिमाः प्रजाः ॥३-२४॥

**Simple English**

These worlds would perish if I did not perform action; I should be the author of confusion of castes and destruction of these beings.

### 3.25

**Sanskrit**

सक्ताः कर्मण्यविद्वांसो यथा कुर्वन्ति भारत ।
कुर्याद्विद्वांस्तथासक्तश्चिकीर्षुर्लोकसंग्रहम् ॥३-२५॥

**Simple English**

As the ignorant men act from attachment to action, O Bharata (Arjuna), so should the wise act without attachment, wishing the welfare of the world.

### 3.26

**Sanskrit**

न बुद्धिभेदं जनयेदज्ञानां कर्मसङ्गिनाम् ।
जोषयेत्सर्वकर्माणि विद्वान्युक्तः समाचरन् ॥३-२६॥

**Simple English**

Let no wise man unsettle the mind of ignorant people who are attached to action; he should engage them in all actions, himself fulfilling them with devotion.

### 3.27

**Sanskrit**

प्रकृतेः क्रियमाणानि गुणैः कर्माणि सर्वशः ।
अहङ्कारविमूढात्मा कर्ताहमिति मन्यते ॥३-२७॥

**Simple English**

All actions are wrought in all cases by the qualities of Nature only. He whose mind is confused by ego thinks, "I am the doer.".

### 3.28

**Sanskrit**

तत्त्ववित्तु महाबाहो गुणकर्मविभागयोः ।
गुणा गुणेषु वर्तन्त इति मत्वा न सज्जते ॥३-२८॥

**Simple English**

But he who knows the Truth, O mighty-armed (Arjuna), about the divisions of the qualities and (their) functions, knowing that the Gunas as senses move among the Gunas as the sense-objects, is not attached.

### 3.29

**Sanskrit**

प्रकृतेर्गुणसम्मूढाः सज्जन्ते गुणकर्मसु ।
तानकृत्स्नविदो मन्दान्कृत्स्नविन्न विचालयेत् ॥३-२९॥

**Simple English**

Those confused by the qualities of Nature are attached to the functions of the qualities. The man of perfect knowledge should not unsettle the foolish one who is of imperfect knowledge.

### 3.30

**Sanskrit**

मयि सर्वाणि कर्माणि संन्यस्याध्यात्मचेतसा ।
निराशीर्निर्ममो भूत्वा युध्यस्व विगतज्वरः ॥३-३०॥

**Simple English**

Renouncing all actions in Me, with the mind centred in the Self, free from hope and ego, and from (mental) fever, do you fight.

### 3.31

**Sanskrit**

ये मे मतमिदं नित्यमनुतिष्ठन्ति मानवाः ।
श्रद्धावन्तोऽनसूयन्तो मुच्यन्ते तेऽपि कर्मभिः ॥३-३१॥

**Simple English**

Those men who constantly practise this teaching of Mine with faith and without cavilling, they too are freed from actions.

### 3.32

**Sanskrit**

ये त्वेतदभ्यसूयन्तो नानुतिष्ठन्ति मे मतम् ।
सर्वज्ञानविमूढांस्तान्विद्धि नष्टानचेतसः ॥३-३२॥

**Simple English**

But those who carp at My teaching and do not practise it, confused of all knowledge, and devoid of clear understanding, know them to be doomed to destruction.

### 3.33

**Sanskrit**

सदृशं चेष्टते स्वस्याः प्रकृतेर्ज्ञानवानपि ।
प्रकृतिं यान्ति भूतानि निग्रहः किं करिष्यति ॥३-३३॥

**Simple English**

Even a wise man acts in accordance with his own nature; beings will follow Nature; what can restraint do?

### 3.34

**Sanskrit**

इन्द्रियस्येन्द्रियस्यार्थे रागद्वेषौ व्यवस्थितौ ।
तयोर्न वशमागच्छेत्तौ ह्यस्य परिपन्थिनौ ॥३-३४॥

**Simple English**

Attachment and aversion for the objects of the senses abide in the senses; let none come under their sway; for, they are his foes.

### 3.35

**Sanskrit**

श्रेयान्स्वधर्मो विगुणः परधर्मात्स्वनुष्ठितात् ।
स्वधर्मे निधनं श्रेयः परधर्मो भयावहः ॥३-३५॥

**Simple English**

It is better to do your own duty, even imperfectly, than to do someone else’s duty perfectly. It is better to die doing your own duty, because another person’s path brings danger.

### 3.36

**Sanskrit**

अर्जुन उवाच ।
अथ केन प्रयुक्तोऽयं पापं चरति पूरुषः ।
अनिच्छन्नपि वार्ष्णेय बलादिव नियोजितः ॥३-३६॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: But impelled by what does man commit wrongdoing, though against his wishes, O Varshneya (Krishna), constrained as it were, by force?

### 3.37

**Sanskrit**

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
काम एष क्रोध एष रजोगुणसमुद्भवः ।
महाशनो महापाप्मा विद्ध्येनमिह वैरिणम् ॥३-३७॥

**Simple English**

Sri Krishna said: It is desire, it is anger both of the quality of passion, all-devouring, all-wrong; know this as the foe here (in this world).

### 3.38

**Sanskrit**

धूमेनाव्रियते वह्निर्यथादर्शो मलेन च ।
यथोल्बेनावृतो गर्भस्तथा तेनेदमावृतम् ॥३-३८॥

**Simple English**

As fire is enveloped by smoke, as a mirror by dust, and as an embryo by the amnion, so is this enveloped by that.

### 3.39

**Sanskrit**

आवृतं ज्ञानमेतेन ज्ञानिनो नित्यवैरिणा ।
कामरूपेण कौन्तेय दुष्पूरेणानलेन च ॥३-३९॥

**Simple English**

O Arjuna, wisdom is enveloped by this constant enemy of the wise in the form of desire, which is unappeasable as fire.

### 3.40

**Sanskrit**

इन्द्रियाणि मनो बुद्धिरस्याधिष्ठानमुच्यते ।
एतैर्विमोहयत्येष ज्ञानमावृत्य देहिनम् ॥३-४०॥

**Simple English**

The senses, the mind and the intellect are said to be its seat; through these it deludes the embodied by veiling his wisdom.

### 3.41

**Sanskrit**

तस्मात्त्वमिन्द्रियाण्यादौ नियम्य भरतर्षभ ।
पाप्मानं प्रजहि ह्येनं ज्ञानविज्ञाननाशनम् ॥३-४१॥

**Simple English**

Therefore, O best of the Bharatas (Arjuna), controlling the senses first, do you kill this wrong thing, the destroyer of knowledge and realisation.

### 3.42

**Sanskrit**

इन्द्रियाणि पराण्याहुरिन्द्रियेभ्यः परं मनः ।
मनसस्तु परा बुद्धिर्यो बुद्धेः परतस्तु सः ॥३-४२॥

**Simple English**

They say that the senses are superior (to the body); superior to the senses is the mind; superior to the mind is the intellect; one who is superior even to the intellect is He (the Self).

### 3.43

**Sanskrit**

एवं बुद्धेः परं बुद्ध्वा संस्तभ्यात्मानमात्मना ।
जहि शत्रुं महाबाहो कामरूपं दुरासदम् ॥३-४३॥

**Simple English**

Thus knowing Him Who is superior to the intellect and restraining the self by the Self, kill you, O mighty-armed Arjuna, the enemy in the form of desire, hard to coner.

## Chapter 4 - Jnana Karma Sanyasa Yoga

### 4.1

**Sanskrit**

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
इमं विवस्वते योगं प्रोक्तवानहमव्ययम् ।
विवस्वान्मनवे प्राह मनुरिक्ष्वाकवेऽब्रवीत् ॥४-१॥

**Simple English**

Sri Krishna said: I taught this eternal Yoga to Vivasvan; he told it to Manu; Manu proclaimed it to Ikshvaku.

### 4.2

**Sanskrit**

एवं परम्पराप्राप्तमिमं राजर्षयो विदुः ।
स कालेनेह महता योगो नष्टः परन्तप ॥४-२॥

**Simple English**

This, handed down thus in regular succession, the royal sages knew. This Yoga, by long lapse of time, has been lost here, O Parantapa (burner of the foes).

### 4.3

**Sanskrit**

स एवायं मया तेऽद्य योगः प्रोक्तः पुरातनः ।
भक्तोऽसि मे सखा चेति रहस्यं ह्येतदुत्तमम् ॥४-३॥

**Simple English**

That same ancient Yoga has been today taught to you by Me, for you are My devotee and My friend; it is the supreme secret.

### 4.4

**Sanskrit**

अर्जुन उवाच ।
अपरं भवतो जन्म परं जन्म विवस्वतः ।
कथमेतद्विजानीयां त्वमादौ प्रोक्तवानिति ॥४-४॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: Later on was Your birth, and prior to it was the birth of Vivasvan (the Sun); how am I to understand that You taught this Yoga in the beginning?

### 4.5

**Sanskrit**

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
बहूनि मे व्यतीतानि जन्मानि तव चार्जुन ।
तान्यहं वेद सर्वाणि न त्वं वेत्थ परन्तप ॥४-५॥

**Simple English**

Sri Krishna said: Many births of Mine have passed as well as of yours, O Arjuna; I know them all but you know not, O Parantapa (scorcher of foes).

### 4.6

**Sanskrit**

अजोऽपि सन्नव्ययात्मा भूतानामीश्वरोऽपि सन् ।
प्रकृतिं स्वामधिष्ठाय सम्भवाम्यात्ममायया ॥४-६॥

**Simple English**

Though I am unborn, of eternal nature, and though I am the Lord of all beings, yet, governing My own Nature, I am born by My own divine illusion.

### 4.7

**Sanskrit**

यदा यदा हि धर्मस्य ग्लानिर्भवति भारत ।
अभ्युत्थानमधर्मस्य तदात्मानं सृजाम्यहम् ॥४-७॥

**Simple English**

Whenever dharma becomes weak and adharma grows strong, I appear in the world.

### 4.8

**Sanskrit**

परित्राणाय साधूनां विनाशाय च दुष्कृताम् ।
धर्मसंस्थापनार्थाय सम्भवामि युगे युगे ॥४-८॥

**Simple English**

I come age after age to protect good people, remove harmful forces, and firmly restore dharma.

### 4.9

**Sanskrit**

जन्म कर्म च मे दिव्यमेवं यो वेत्ति तत्त्वतः ।
त्यक्त्वा देहं पुनर्जन्म नैति मामेति सोऽर्जुन ॥४-९॥

**Simple English**

He who thus know, in their true light, My divine birth and action, having abandoned the body, is not born again, he comes to Me, O Arjuna.

### 4.10

**Sanskrit**

वीतरागभयक्रोधा मन्मया मामुपाश्रिताः ।
बहवो ज्ञानतपसा पूता मद्भावमागताः ॥४-१०॥

**Simple English**

Freed from attachment, fear and anger, absorbed in Me, taking refuge in Me, purified by the fire of knowledge, many have attained to My Being.

### 4.11

**Sanskrit**

ये यथा मां प्रपद्यन्ते तांस्तथैव भजाम्यहम् ।
मम वर्त्मानुवर्तन्ते मनुष्याः पार्थ सर्वशः ॥४-११॥

**Simple English**

In whatever way men approach Me even so do I reward them; My path do men tread in all ways, O Arjuna.

### 4.12

**Sanskrit**

काङ्क्षन्तः कर्मणां सिद्धिं यजन्त इह देवताः ।
क्षिप्रं हि मानुषे लोके सिद्धिर्भवति कर्मजा ॥४-१२॥

**Simple English**

Those who long for success in action in this world sacrifice to the gods; because success is quickly attained by men through action.

### 4.13

**Sanskrit**

चातुर्वर्ण्यं मया सृष्टं गुणकर्मविभागशः ।
तस्य कर्तारमपि मां विद्ध्यकर्तारमव्ययम् ॥४-१३॥

**Simple English**

The fourfold varna order was created by me according to guna and karma. Though I am its source, know me as the non-doer and unchanging.

### 4.14

**Sanskrit**

न मां कर्माणि लिम्पन्ति न मे कर्मफले स्पृहा ।
इति मां योऽभिजानाति कर्मभिर्न स बध्यते ॥४-१४॥

**Simple English**

Actions do not taint Me, nor have I a desire for the fruit of actions. He who knows Me thus is not bound by actions.

### 4.15

**Sanskrit**

एवं ज्ञात्वा कृतं कर्म पूर्वैरपि मुमुक्षुभिः ।
कुरु कर्मैव तस्मात्त्वं पूर्वैः पूर्वतरं कृतम् ॥४-१५॥

**Simple English**

Having known this, the ancient seekers after freedom also performed action; therefore do you also perform action, as did the ancients in days of yore.

### 4.16

**Sanskrit**

किं कर्म किमकर्मेति कवयोऽप्यत्र मोहिताः ।
तत्ते कर्म प्रवक्ष्यामि यज्ज्ञात्वा मोक्ष्यसेऽशुभात् ॥४-१६॥

**Simple English**

What is action? What is inaction? As to this even the wise are confused. Therefore I shall teach you such action (the nature of action and inaction) by knowing which you will be free from the evil (of worldly cycle, the wheel of birth and death).

### 4.17

**Sanskrit**

कर्मणो ह्यपि बोद्धव्यं बोद्धव्यं च विकर्मणः ।
अकर्मणश्च बोद्धव्यं गहना कर्मणो गतिः ॥४-१७॥

**Simple English**

For truly (the true nature) of action (enjoined by the scriptures) should be known, also (that) of forbidden (or unlawful) action, and of inaction; hard to understand is the nature (path) of action.

### 4.18

**Sanskrit**

कर्मण्यकर्म यः पश्येदकर्मणि च कर्म यः ।
स बुद्धिमान्मनुष्येषु स युक्तः कृत्स्नकर्मकृत् ॥४-१८॥

**Simple English**

He who sees inaction in action and action in inaction, he is wise among men; he is a Yogi and performer of all actions.

### 4.19

**Sanskrit**

यस्य सर्वे समारम्भाः कामसङ्कल्पवर्जिताः ।
ज्ञानाग्निदग्धकर्माणं तमाहुः पण्डितं बुधाः ॥४-१९॥

**Simple English**

He whose undertakings are all devoid of desires and (selfish) purposes and whose actions have been burnt by the fire of knowledge, him the wise call a sage.

### 4.20

**Sanskrit**

त्यक्त्वा कर्मफलासङ्गं नित्यतृप्तो निराश्रयः ।
कर्मण्यभिप्रवृत्तोऽपि नैव किञ्चित्करोति सः ॥४-२०॥

**Simple English**

Having abandoned attachment to the fruits of the action, ever content, depending on nothing, he does not do anything though engaged in activity.

### 4.21

**Sanskrit**

निराशीर्यतचित्तात्मा त्यक्तसर्वपरिग्रहः ।
शारीरं केवलं कर्म कुर्वन्नाप्नोति किल्बिषम् ॥४-२१॥

**Simple English**

Without hope and with the mind and the self controlled, having abandoned all greed, doing mere bodily action, he incurs no wrongdoing.

### 4.22

**Sanskrit**

यदृच्छालाभसन्तुष्टो द्वन्द्वातीतो विमत्सरः ।
समः सिद्धावसिद्धौ च कृत्वापि न निबध्यते ॥४-२२॥

**Simple English**

Content with what comes to him without effort, free from the pairs of opposites and envy, even-minded in success and failure, though acting, he is not bound.

### 4.23

**Sanskrit**

गतसङ्गस्य मुक्तस्य ज्ञानावस्थितचेतसः ।
यज्ञायाचरतः कर्म समग्रं प्रविलीयते ॥४-२३॥

**Simple English**

To one who is devoid of attchment, who is free, whose mind is established in knowledge, who works for the sake of sacrifice (for the sake of God), the whole action is dissolved.

### 4.24

**Sanskrit**

ब्रह्मार्पणं ब्रह्म हविर्ब्रह्माग्नौ ब्रह्मणा हुतम् ।
ब्रह्मैव तेन गन्तव्यं ब्रह्मकर्मसमाधिना ॥४-२४॥

**Simple English**

the Supreme Reality is the oblation; the Supreme Reality is the melted butter (ghee); by the Supreme Reality is the oblation poured into the fire of the Supreme Reality; the Supreme Reality truly shall be reached by him who always sees the Supreme Reality in action.

### 4.25

**Sanskrit**

दैवमेवापरे यज्ञं योगिनः पर्युपासते ।
ब्रह्माग्नावपरे यज्ञं यज्ञेनैवोपजुह्वति ॥४-२५॥

**Simple English**

Some Yogis perform sacrifice to the gods alone; while others (who have realised the Self) offer the self as sacrifice by the Self in the fire of the Supreme Reality alone.

### 4.26

**Sanskrit**

श्रोत्रादीनीन्द्रियाण्यन्ये संयमाग्निषु जुह्वति ।
शब्दादीन्विषयानन्य इन्द्रियाग्निषु जुह्वति ॥४-२६॥

**Simple English**

Some again offer the organ of hearing and other senses as sacrifice in the fire of restraint; others offer sound and other objects of the senses as sacrifice in the fire of the senses.

### 4.27

**Sanskrit**

सर्वाणीन्द्रियकर्माणि प्राणकर्माणि चापरे ।
आत्मसंयमयोगाग्नौ जुह्वति ज्ञानदीपिते ॥४-२७॥

**Simple English**

Others again sacrifice all the functions of the senses and those of the breath (vital energy or Prana) in the fire of the Yoga of self-restraint kindled by knowledge.

### 4.28

**Sanskrit**

द्रव्ययज्ञास्तपोयज्ञा योगयज्ञास्तथापरे ।
स्वाध्यायज्ञानयज्ञाश्च यतयः संशितव्रताः ॥४-२८॥

**Simple English**

Others again offer wealth, self-discipline and Yoga as sacrifice, while the disciplined seekers of self-restraint and rigid vows offer study of scriptures and knowledge as sacrifice.

### 4.29

**Sanskrit**

अपाने जुह्वति प्राणं प्राणेऽपानं तथापरे ।
प्राणापानगती रुद्ध्वा प्राणायामपरायणाः ॥४-२९॥

**Simple English**

Others offer as sacrifice the outgoing breath in the incoming, and the incoming in the outgoing, restraining the course of the outgoing and the incoming breaths, solely absorbed in the restraint of the breath.

### 4.30

**Sanskrit**

अपरे नियताहाराः प्राणान्प्राणेषु जुह्वति ।
सर्वेऽप्येते यज्ञविदो यज्ञक्षपितकल्मषाः ॥४-३०॥

**Simple English**

Others who regulate their diet offer life-breaths in life-breaths. All these are knowers of sacrifice, whose wrongs are destroyed by sacrifice.

### 4.31

**Sanskrit**

यज्ञशिष्टामृतभुजो यान्ति ब्रह्म सनातनम् ।
नायं लोकोऽस्त्ययज्ञस्य कुतोऽन्यः कुरुसत्तम ॥४-३१॥

**Simple English**

Those who eat the remnants of the sacrifice, which are like nectar, go to the eternal the Supreme Reality. This world is not for the man who does not perform sacrifice; how then can he have the other, O Arjuna?

### 4.32

**Sanskrit**

एवं बहुविधा यज्ञा वितता ब्रह्मणो मुखे ।
कर्मजान्विद्धि तान्सर्वानेवं ज्ञात्वा विमोक्ष्यसे ॥४-३२॥

**Simple English**

Thus, manifold sacrifices are spread out before the Supreme Reality (literally) at the mouth or face of the Supreme Reality). Know them all as born of action and thus knowing, you will be free.

### 4.33

**Sanskrit**

श्रेयान्द्रव्यमयाद्यज्ञाज्ज्ञानयज्ञः परन्तप ।
सर्वं कर्माखिलं पार्थ ज्ञाने परिसमाप्यते ॥४-३३॥

**Simple English**

Swami Sivananda did not comment on this sloka.

### 4.34

**Sanskrit**

तद्विद्धि प्रणिपातेन परिप्रश्नेन सेवया ।
उपदेक्ष्यन्ति ते ज्ञानं ज्ञानिनस्तत्त्वदर्शिनः ॥४-३४॥

**Simple English**

Know That by long prostration, by estion and by service; the wise who have realised the Truth will instruct you in (that) knowledge.

### 4.35

**Sanskrit**

यज्ज्ञात्वा न पुनर्मोहमेवं यास्यसि पाण्डव ।
येन भूतान्यशेषेण द्रक्ष्यस्यात्मन्यथो मयि (var अशेषाणि) ॥४-३५॥

**Simple English**

Knowing that you will not, O Arjuna, again get confused like this; and by that you will see all beings in your Self and also in Me.

### 4.36

**Sanskrit**

अपि चेदसि पापेभ्यः सर्वेभ्यः पापकृत्तमः ।
सर्वं ज्ञानप्लवेनैव वृजिनं सन्तरिष्यसि ॥४-३६॥

**Simple English**

Even if you are the most wrong of all sinners, yet you will truly cross all wrongs by the raft of knowledge.

### 4.37

**Sanskrit**

यथैधांसि समिद्धोऽग्निर्भस्मसात्कुरुतेऽर्जुन ।
ज्ञानाग्निः सर्वकर्माणि भस्मसात्कुरुते तथा ॥४-३७॥

**Simple English**

As the blazing fire reduces fuel to ashes, O Arjuna, so does the fire of knowledge reduce all actions to ashes.

### 4.38

**Sanskrit**

न हि ज्ञानेन सदृशं पवित्रमिह विद्यते ।
तत्स्वयं योगसंसिद्धः कालेनात्मनि विन्दति ॥४-३८॥

**Simple English**

Truly, there is no purifier in this world like knowledge. He who is perfected in Yoga finds it in the Self in time.

### 4.39

**Sanskrit**

श्रद्धावाँल्लभते ज्ञानं तत्परः संयतेन्द्रियः ।
ज्ञानं लब्ध्वा परां शान्तिमचिरेणाधिगच्छति ॥४-३९॥

**Simple English**

The man who is full of faith, who is devoted to it, and who has subdued the senses obtains (this) knowledge; and having obtained the knowledge he attains at once to the supreme peace.

### 4.40

**Sanskrit**

अज्ञश्चाश्रद्दधानश्च संशयात्मा विनश्यति ।
नायं लोकोऽस्ति न परो न सुखं संशयात्मनः ॥४-४०॥

**Simple English**

The ignorant the faithless, the doubting self goes to destruction; there is neither this world nor the other, nor happiness for the doubting.

### 4.41

**Sanskrit**

योगसंन्यस्तकर्माणं ज्ञानसञ्छिन्नसंशयम् ।
आत्मवन्तं न कर्माणि निबध्नन्ति धनञ्जय ॥४-४१॥

**Simple English**

He who has renounced actions by Yoga, whose doubts are rent asunder by knowledge, and who is self-possessed actions do not bind him, O Arjuna.

### 4.42

**Sanskrit**

तस्मादज्ञानसम्भूतं हृत्स्थं ज्ञानासिनात्मनः ।
छित्त्वैनं संशयं योगमातिष्ठोत्तिष्ठ भारत ॥४-४२॥

**Simple English**

Therefore with the sword of the knowledge (of the Self) cut asunder the doubt of the self born of ignorance, residing in your heart, and take refuge in Yoga. Arise, O Arjuna.

## Chapter 5 - Karma Sanyasa Yoga

### 5.1

**Sanskrit**

अर्जुन उवाच ।
संन्यासं कर्मणां कृष्ण पुनर्योगं च शंससि ।
यच्छ्रेय एतयोरेकं तन्मे ब्रूहि सुनिश्चितम् ॥५-१॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: Renunciation of actions, O Krishna, You praise, and again Yoga. Tell me conclusively that which is the better of the two.

### 5.2

**Sanskrit**

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
संन्यासः कर्मयोगश्च निःश्रेयसकरावुभौ ।
तयोस्तु कर्मसंन्यासात्कर्मयोगो विशिष्यते ॥५-२॥

**Simple English**

Sri Krishna said: Renunciation and the Yoga of action both lead to the highest bliss; but of the two, the Yoga of action is superior to the renunciation of action.

### 5.3

**Sanskrit**

ज्ञेयः स नित्यसंन्यासी यो न द्वेष्टि न काङ्क्षति ।
निर्द्वन्द्वो हि महाबाहो सुखं बन्धात्प्रमुच्यते ॥५-३॥

**Simple English**

He should be known as a perpetual Sannyasi who neither hates nor desires; for, free from the pairs of opposites, O mighty-armed Arjuna, he is easily set free from bondage.

### 5.4

**Sanskrit**

साङ्ख्ययोगौ पृथग्बालाः प्रवदन्ति न पण्डिताः ।
एकमप्यास्थितः सम्यगुभयोर्विन्दते फलम् ॥५-४॥

**Simple English**

Children, not the wise, speak of knowledge and the Yoga of action or the performance of action as though they are distinct and different; he who is truly established in one obtains the fruits of both.

### 5.5

**Sanskrit**

यत्साङ्ख्यैः प्राप्यते स्थानं तद्योगैरपि गम्यते ।
एकं साङ्ख्यं च योगं च यः पश्यति स पश्यति ॥५-५॥

**Simple English**

That place which is reached by the Sankhyas or the Jnanis is reached by the Yogis (Karma Yogis). He sees, who sees knowledge and the performance of action (Karma Yoga) as one.

### 5.6

**Sanskrit**

संन्यासस्तु महाबाहो दुःखमाप्तुमयोगतः ।
योगयुक्तो मुनिर्ब्रह्म नचिरेणाधिगच्छति ॥५-६॥

**Simple English**

But renunciation, O mighty-armed Arjuna, is hard to attain without Yoga; the Yoga-harmonised sage quickly goes to the Supreme Reality.

### 5.7

**Sanskrit**

योगयुक्तो विशुद्धात्मा विजितात्मा जितेन्द्रियः ।
सर्वभूतात्मभूतात्मा कुर्वन्नपि न लिप्यते ॥५-७॥

**Simple English**

He who is devoted to the path of action, whose mind is quite pure, who has conquered the self, who has subdued his senses and who realises his Self as the Self in all beings, though acting, is not tainted.

### 5.8

**Sanskrit**

नैव किञ्चित्करोमीति युक्तो मन्येत तत्त्ववित् ।
पश्यञ्शृण्वन्स्पृशञ्जिघ्रन्नश्नन्गच्छन्स्वपञ्श्वसन् ॥५-८॥

**Simple English**

"I do nothing at all," thus would the harmonised knower of Truth think seeing, hearing, touching, smelling, eating, going, sleeping, breathing.

### 5.9

**Sanskrit**

प्रलपन्विसृजन्गृह्णन्नुन्मिषन्निमिषन्नपि ।
इन्द्रियाणीन्द्रियार्थेषु वर्तन्त इति धारयन् ॥५-९॥

**Simple English**

Speaking, letting go, seizing, opening and closing the eyes convinced that the senses move among the sense-objects.

### 5.10

**Sanskrit**

ब्रह्मण्याधाय कर्माणि सङ्गं त्यक्त्वा करोति यः ।
लिप्यते न स पापेन पद्मपत्रमिवाम्भसा ॥५-१०॥

**Simple English**

He who does actions, offering them to the Supreme Reality, and abandoning attachment, is not tainted by wrongdoing, just as a lotus-leaf is not tainted by water.

### 5.11

**Sanskrit**

कायेन मनसा बुद्ध्या केवलैरिन्द्रियैरपि ।
योगिनः कर्म कुर्वन्ति सङ्गं त्यक्त्वात्मशुद्धये ॥५-११॥

**Simple English**

Yogis, having abandoned attachment, perform actions only by the body, mind, intellect, and even by the senses, for the purification of the self.

### 5.12

**Sanskrit**

युक्तः कर्मफलं त्यक्त्वा शान्तिमाप्नोति नैष्ठिकीम् ।
अयुक्तः कामकारेण फले सक्तो निबध्यते ॥५-१२॥

**Simple English**

The united one (the well poised or the harmonised) having abandoned the result of action attains to the eternal peace: the non-united only (the unsteady or the unbalanced) impelled by desire, attached to the fruit, is bound.

### 5.13

**Sanskrit**

सर्वकर्माणि मनसा संन्यस्यास्ते सुखं वशी ।
नवद्वारे पुरे देही नैव कुर्वन्न कारयन् ॥५-१३॥

**Simple English**

Mentally renouncing all actions and self-controlled, the embodied one rests happily in the nine-gated city, neither acting nor causing others (body and senses) to act.

### 5.14

**Sanskrit**

न कर्तृत्वं न कर्माणि लोकस्य सृजति प्रभुः ।
न कर्मफलसंयोगं स्वभावस्तु प्रवर्तते ॥५-१४॥

**Simple English**

Neither agency nor actions does the Lord create for the world, nor union with the fruits of actions. But it is Nature that acts.

### 5.15

**Sanskrit**

नादत्ते कस्यचित्पापं न चैव सुकृतं विभुः ।
अज्ञानेनावृतं ज्ञानं तेन मुह्यन्ति जन्तवः ॥५-१५॥

**Simple English**

The Lord takes neither the demerit nor even the merit of any; knowledge is enveloped by ignorance, thery beings are confused.

### 5.16

**Sanskrit**

ज्ञानेन तु तदज्ञानं येषां नाशितमात्मनः ।
तेषामादित्यवज्ज्ञानं प्रकाशयति तत्परम् ॥५-१६॥

**Simple English**

But to those whose ignorance is destroyed by the knowledge of the Self, like the sun, knowledge reveals the Supreme (the Supreme Reality).

### 5.17

**Sanskrit**

तद्बुद्धयस्तदात्मानस्तन्निष्ठास्तत्परायणाः ।
गच्छन्त्यपुनरावृत्तिं ज्ञाननिर्धूतकल्मषाः ॥५-१७॥

**Simple English**

Their intellect absorbed in That, their self being That, established in That, with That for their supreme goal, they go from where there is no return, their wrongs dispelled by knowledge.

### 5.18

**Sanskrit**

विद्याविनयसम्पन्ने ब्राह्मणे गवि हस्तिनि ।
शुनि चैव श्वपाके च पण्डिताः समदर्शिनः ॥५-१८॥

**Simple English**

Sages look with an equal eye on a Brahmana endowed with learning and humility, on a cow, on an elephant, and even on a dog and an outcaste.

### 5.19

**Sanskrit**

इहैव तैर्जितः सर्गो येषां साम्ये स्थितं मनः ।
निर्दोषं हि समं ब्रह्म तस्माद् ब्रह्मणि ते स्थिताः ॥५-१९॥

**Simple English**

Even here (in this world) birth (everything) is overcome by those whose minds rest in equality; the Supreme Reality is spotless indeed and equal; therefore they are established in the Supreme Reality.

### 5.20

**Sanskrit**

न प्रहृष्येत्प्रियं प्राप्य नोद्विजेत्प्राप्य चाप्रियम् ।
स्थिरबुद्धिरसम्मूढो ब्रह्मविद् ब्रह्मणि स्थितः ॥५-२०॥

**Simple English**

Resting in the Supreme Reality, with steady intellect and undeluded, the knower of the Supreme Reality neither rejoices on obtaining what is pleasant nor grieves on obtaining what is unpleasant.

### 5.21

**Sanskrit**

बाह्यस्पर्शेष्वसक्तात्मा विन्दत्यात्मनि यत्सुखम् ।
स ब्रह्मयोगयुक्तात्मा सुखमक्षयमश्नुते ॥५-२१॥

**Simple English**

With the self unattached to external contacts he finds happiness in the Self; with the self engaged in the meditation of the Supreme Reality he attains to the endless happiness.

### 5.22

**Sanskrit**

ये हि संस्पर्शजा भोगा दुःखयोनय एव ते ।
आद्यन्तवन्तः कौन्तेय न तेषु रमते बुधः ॥५-२२॥

**Simple English**

The enjoyments that are born of contacts are only generators of pain, for they have a beginning and an end, O Arjuna; the wise man does not rejoice in them.

### 5.23

**Sanskrit**

शक्नोतीहैव यः सोढुं प्राक्शरीरविमोक्षणात् ।
कामक्रोधोद्भवं वेगं स युक्तः स सुखी नरः ॥५-२३॥

**Simple English**

He who is able, while still here (in this world) to withstand, before the freedom from the body, the impulse born out of desire and anger he is a Yogi, he is a happy man.

### 5.24

**Sanskrit**

योऽन्तःसुखोऽन्तरारामस्तथान्तर्ज्योतिरेव यः ।
स योगी ब्रह्मनिर्वाणं ब्रह्मभूतोऽधिगच्छति ॥५-२४॥

**Simple English**

He who is happy within, who rejoices within, and who is illuminated within, that Yogi attains absolute freedom or spiritual freedom, himself becoming the Supreme Reality.

### 5.25

**Sanskrit**

लभन्ते ब्रह्मनिर्वाणमृषयः क्षीणकल्मषाः ।
छिन्नद्वैधा यतात्मानः सर्वभूतहिते रताः ॥५-२५॥

**Simple English**

The sages (Rishis) obtain absolute freedom or spiritual freedom they whose wrongs have been destroyed, whose dualities (perception of dualities or experience of the pairs of opposites) are torn asunder, who are self-controlled, and intent on the welfare of all beings.

### 5.26

**Sanskrit**

कामक्रोधवियुक्तानां यतीनां यतचेतसाम् ।
अभितो ब्रह्मनिर्वाणं वर्तते विदितात्मनाम् ॥५-२६॥

**Simple English**

Absolute freedom (or Brahmic bliss) exists on all sides for those self-controlled disciplined seekers who are free from desire and anger, who have controlled their thoughts and who have realised the Self.

### 5.27

**Sanskrit**

स्पर्शान्कृत्वा बहिर्बाह्यांश्चक्षुश्चैवान्तरे भ्रुवोः ।
प्राणापानौ समौ कृत्वा नासाभ्यन्तरचारिणौ ॥५-२७॥

**Simple English**

Shutting out (all) external contacts and fixing the gaze between the eyebrow, ealising the outgoing and incoming breaths moving within the nostrils.

### 5.28

**Sanskrit**

यतेन्द्रियमनोबुद्धिर्मुनिर्मोक्षपरायणः ।
विगतेच्छाभयक्रोधो यः सदा मुक्त एव सः ॥५-२८॥

**Simple English**

With the senses, the mind and the intellect (ever) controlled, having freedom as his supreme goal, free from desire, fear and anger the sage is truly free for ever.

### 5.29

**Sanskrit**

भोक्तारं यज्ञतपसां सर्वलोकमहेश्वरम् ।
सुहृदं सर्वभूतानां ज्ञात्वा मां शान्तिमृच्छति ॥५-२९॥

**Simple English**

He who knows Me as the enjoyer of sacrifices and self-discipline, the great Lord of all the worlds and the friend of all beings, attains to peace.

## Chapter 6 - Dhyana Yoga

### 6.1

**Sanskrit**

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
अनाश्रितः कर्मफलं कार्यं कर्म करोति यः ।
स संन्यासी च योगी च न निरग्निर्न चाक्रियः ॥६-१॥

**Simple English**

Sri Krishna said: He who performs his proper duty without depending on the fruits of his actions he is a Sannyasi and a Yogi; not he who is without fire and without action.

### 6.2

**Sanskrit**

यं संन्यासमिति प्राहुर्योगं तं विद्धि पाण्डव ।
न ह्यसंन्यस्तसङ्कल्पो योगी भवति कश्चन ॥६-२॥

**Simple English**

Do you, O Arjuna, know Yoga to be that which they call renunciation; no one truly becomes a Yogi who has not renounced thoughts.

### 6.3

**Sanskrit**

आरुरुक्षोर्मुनेर्योगं कर्म कारणमुच्यते ।
योगारूढस्य तस्यैव शमः कारणमुच्यते ॥६-३॥

**Simple English**

For a sage who wishes to attain to Yoga, action is said to be the means; for the same sage who has attained to Yoga, inaction (iescence) is said to be the means.

### 6.4

**Sanskrit**

यदा हि नेन्द्रियार्थेषु न कर्मस्वनुषज्जते ।
सर्वसङ्कल्पसंन्यासी योगारूढस्तदोच्यते ॥६-४॥

**Simple English**

When a man is not attached to the sense-objects or to actions, having renounced all thoughts, then he is said to have attained to Yoga.

### 6.5

**Sanskrit**

उद्धरेदात्मनात्मानं नात्मानमवसादयेत् ।
आत्मैव ह्यात्मनो बन्धुरात्मैव रिपुरात्मनः ॥६-५॥

**Simple English**

Lift yourself by your own mind; do not pull yourself down. Your mind can be your friend, and it can also become your enemy.

### 6.6

**Sanskrit**

बन्धुरात्मात्मनस्तस्य येनात्मैवात्मना जितः ।
अनात्मनस्तु शत्रुत्वे वर्तेतात्मैव शत्रुवत् ॥६-६॥

**Simple English**

The Self is the friend of the self of him by whom the self has been conquered by the Self, but to the unconquered self, this Self stands in the position of an enemy, like an (external) foe.

### 6.7

**Sanskrit**

जितात्मनः प्रशान्तस्य परमात्मा समाहितः ।
शीतोष्णसुखदुःखेषु तथा मानापमानयोः ॥६-७॥

**Simple English**

The Supreme Self of him who is self-controlled and peaceful is balanced in cold and heat, pleasure and pain, as also in honour and dishonour.

### 6.8

**Sanskrit**

ज्ञानविज्ञानतृप्तात्मा कूटस्थो विजितेन्द्रियः ।
युक्त इत्युच्यते योगी समलोष्टाश्मकाञ्चनः ॥६-८॥

**Simple English**

The Yogi who is satisfied with the knowledge and the wisdom (of the Self), who has conquered the senses, and to whom a clod of earth, a piece of stone and gold are the same, is said to be harmonized (i.e., is said to have attained Nirvikalpa Samadhi).

### 6.9

**Sanskrit**

सुहृन्मित्रार्युदासीनमध्यस्थद्वेष्यबन्धुषु ।
साधुष्वपि च पापेषु समबुद्धिर्विशिष्यते ॥६-९॥

**Simple English**

He who is of the same mind to the good-hearted, friends, enemies, the indifferent, the neutral, the hateful, the relatives, the righteous and the unrighteous, excels.

### 6.10

**Sanskrit**

योगी युञ्जीत सततमात्मानं रहसि स्थितः ।
एकाकी यतचित्तात्मा निराशीरपरिग्रहः ॥६-१०॥

**Simple English**

Let the Yogi try constantly to keep the mind steady, remaining in solitude, alone, with the mind and the body controlled, and free from hope and greed.

### 6.11

**Sanskrit**

शुचौ देशे प्रतिष्ठाप्य स्थिरमासनमात्मनः ।
नात्युच्छ्रितं नातिनीचं चैलाजिनकुशोत्तरम् ॥६-११॥

**Simple English**

In a clean spot, having established a firm seat of his own, neither too high nor too low, made of a cloth, a skin and Kusa-grass, one over the other.

### 6.12

**Sanskrit**

तत्रैकाग्रं मनः कृत्वा यतचित्तेन्द्रियक्रियः ।
उपविश्यासने युञ्ज्याद्योगमात्मविशुद्धये ॥६-१२॥

**Simple English**

There, having made the mind one-pointed, with the actions of the mind and the senses controlled, let him, seated on the seat, practise Yoga for the purification of the self.

### 6.13

**Sanskrit**

समं कायशिरोग्रीवं धारयन्नचलं स्थिरः ।
सम्प्रेक्ष्य नासिकाग्रं स्वं दिशश्चानवलोकयन् ॥६-१३॥

**Simple English**

Let him firmly hold his body, head and neck erect and still, gazing at the tip of his nose, without looking around.

### 6.14

**Sanskrit**

प्रशान्तात्मा विगतभीर्ब्रह्मचारिव्रते स्थितः ।
मनः संयम्य मच्चित्तो युक्त आसीत मत्परः ॥६-१४॥

**Simple English**

Serene-minded, fearless, firm in the vow of a Brahmachari, having controlled the mind, thinking of Me and balanced in mind, let him sit, having Me as his supreme goal.

### 6.15

**Sanskrit**

युञ्जन्नेवं सदात्मानं योगी नियतमानसः ।
शान्तिं निर्वाणपरमां मत्संस्थामधिगच्छति ॥६-१५॥

**Simple English**

Thus always keeping the mind balanced, the Yogi, with the mind controlled, attains to the peace abiding in Me, which culminates in freedom.

### 6.16

**Sanskrit**

नात्यश्नतस्तु योगोऽस्ति न चैकान्तमनश्नतः ।
न चातिस्वप्नशीलस्य जाग्रतो नैव चार्जुन ॥६-१६॥

**Simple English**

Truly Yoga is not possible for him who eats too much, nor for him who does not eat at all, nor for him who sleeps too much, nor for him who is (always) awake, O Arjuna.

### 6.17

**Sanskrit**

युक्ताहारविहारस्य युक्तचेष्टस्य कर्मसु ।
युक्तस्वप्नावबोधस्य योगो भवति दुःखहा ॥६-१७॥

**Simple English**

Yoga becomes the destroyer of pain for him who is moderate in eating and recreation (such as walking, etc.), who is moderate in exertion in actions, who is moderate in sleep and wakefulness.

### 6.18

**Sanskrit**

यदा विनियतं चित्तमात्मन्येवावतिष्ठते ।
निःस्पृहः सर्वकामेभ्यो युक्त इत्युच्यते तदा ॥६-१८॥

**Simple English**

When the perfectly controlled mind rests in the Self only, free from longing for all the objects of desires, then it is said, 'He is united'.

### 6.19

**Sanskrit**

यथा दीपो निवातस्थो नेङ्गते सोपमा स्मृता ।
योगिनो यतचित्तस्य युञ्जतो योगमात्मनः ॥६-१९॥

**Simple English**

As a lamp placed in a windless spot does not flicker to such is compared the Yogi of controlled mind, practising Yoga in the Self (or absorbed in the Yoga of the Self).

### 6.20

**Sanskrit**

यत्रोपरमते चित्तं निरुद्धं योगसेवया ।
यत्र चैवात्मनात्मानं पश्यन्नात्मनि तुष्यति ॥६-२०॥

**Simple English**

When the mind, restrained by the practice of Yoga attains to quietude and when seeing the Self by the self, he is satisfied in his own Self.

### 6.21

**Sanskrit**

सुखमात्यन्तिकं यत्तद् बुद्धिग्राह्यमतीन्द्रियम् ।
वेत्ति यत्र न चैवायं स्थितश्चलति तत्त्वतः ॥६-२१॥

**Simple English**

When he (the Yogi) feels that Infinite Bliss which can be grasped by the (pure) intellect and which transcends the senses, and established wherein he never moves from the Reality.

### 6.22

**Sanskrit**

यं लब्ध्वा चापरं लाभं मन्यते नाधिकं ततः ।
यस्मिन्स्थितो न दुःखेन गुरुणापि विचाल्यते ॥६-२२॥

**Simple English**

Which, having obtained, he thinks there is no other gain superior to it; wherein established, he is not moved even by heavy sorrow.

### 6.23

**Sanskrit**

तं विद्याद् दुःखसंयोगवियोगं योगसंज्ञितम् ।
स निश्चयेन योक्तव्यो योगोऽनिर्विण्णचेतसा ॥६-२३॥

**Simple English**

Let that be known by the name of Yoga, the severance from union with pain. This Yoga should be practised with determination and with an undesponding mind.

### 6.24

**Sanskrit**

सङ्कल्पप्रभवान्कामांस्त्यक्त्वा सर्वानशेषतः ।
मनसैवेन्द्रियग्रामं विनियम्य समन्ततः ॥६-२४॥

**Simple English**

Abandoning without reserve all desires born of Sankalpa (thought and imagination) and completely restraining the whole group of the senses by the mind from all sides.

### 6.25

**Sanskrit**

शनैः शनैरुपरमेद् बुद्ध्या धृतिगृहीतया ।
आत्मसंस्थं मनः कृत्वा न किञ्चिदपि चिन्तयेत् ॥६-२५॥

**Simple English**

Little by little let him attain to ietude by the intellect held firmly; having made the mind establish itself in the Self, let him not think of anything.

### 6.26

**Sanskrit**

यतो यतो निश्चरति मनश्चञ्चलमस्थिरम् ।
ततस्ततो नियम्यैतदात्मन्येव वशं नयेत् ॥६-२६॥

**Simple English**

From whatever cause the restless and unsteady mind wanders away, from that let him restrain it and bring it under the control of the Self alone.

### 6.27

**Sanskrit**

प्रशान्तमनसं ह्येनं योगिनं सुखमुत्तमम् ।
उपैति शान्तरजसं ब्रह्मभूतमकल्मषम् ॥६-२७॥

**Simple English**

Supreme Bliss truly comes to this Yogi whose mind is quite peaceful, whose passion is quieted, who has become the Supreme Reality and who is free from wrongdoing.

### 6.28

**Sanskrit**

युञ्जन्नेवं सदात्मानं योगी विगतकल्मषः ।
सुखेन ब्रह्मसंस्पर्शमत्यन्तं सुखमश्नुते ॥६-२८॥

**Simple English**

The Yogi, always engaging the mind thus (in the practice of Yoga), freed from wrongs, easily enjoys the Infinite Bliss of contact with the Supreme Reality (the Eternal).

### 6.29

**Sanskrit**

सर्वभूतस्थमात्मानं सर्वभूतानि चात्मनि ।
ईक्षते योगयुक्तात्मा सर्वत्र समदर्शनः ॥६-२९॥

**Simple English**

With the mind harmonised by Yoga he sees the Self abiding in all beings and all beings in the Self; he sees the same everywhere.

### 6.30

**Sanskrit**

यो मां पश्यति सर्वत्र सर्वं च मयि पश्यति ।
तस्याहं न प्रणश्यामि स च मे न प्रणश्यति ॥६-३०॥

**Simple English**

He who sees Me everywhere and sees everything in Me, he never becomes separated from Me, nor do I become separated from him.

### 6.31

**Sanskrit**

सर्वभूतस्थितं यो मां भजत्येकत्वमास्थितः ।
सर्वथा वर्तमानोऽपि स योगी मयि वर्तते ॥६-३१॥

**Simple English**

He who, being established in unity, worships Me Who dwells in all beings, that Yogi abides in Me, whatever may be his mode of living.

### 6.32

**Sanskrit**

आत्मौपम्येन सर्वत्र समं पश्यति योऽर्जुन ।
सुखं वा यदि वा दुःखं स योगी परमो मतः ॥६-३२॥

**Simple English**

He who, through the likeness of the Self, O Arjuna, sees equality everywhere, be it pleasure or pain, he is regarded as the highest Yogi.

### 6.33

**Sanskrit**

अर्जुन उवाच ।
योऽयं योगस्त्वया प्रोक्तः साम्येन मधुसूदन ।
एतस्याहं न पश्यामि चञ्चलत्वात्स्थितिं स्थिराम् ॥६-३३॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: This Yoga of equal-mindedness taught by You, O Krishna, I do not see its steady continuance, because of the restlessness (of the mind).

### 6.34

**Sanskrit**

चञ्चलं हि मनः कृष्ण प्रमाथि बलवद् दृढम् ।
तस्याहं निग्रहं मन्ये वायोरिव सुदुष्करम् ॥६-३४॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: The mind is restless, stormy, strong, and stubborn, O Krishna. I think controlling it is as hard as controlling the wind.

### 6.35

**Sanskrit**

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
असंशयं महाबाहो मनो दुर्निग्रहं चलम् ।
अभ्यासेन तु कौन्तेय वैराग्येण च गृह्यते ॥६-३५॥

**Simple English**

Sri Krishna said: Undoubtedly, O mighty-armed Arjuna, the mind is difficult to control and restless; but by practice and by dispassion it may be restrained.

### 6.36

**Sanskrit**

असंयतात्मना योगो दुष्प्राप इति मे मतिः ।
वश्यात्मना तु यतता शक्योऽवाप्तुमुपायतः ॥६-३६॥

**Simple English**

I think Yoga is hard to be attained by one of uncontrolled self, but the self-controlled and striving one can attain to it by the (proper) means.

### 6.37

**Sanskrit**

अर्जुन उवाच ।
अयतिः श्रद्धयोपेतो योगाच्चलितमानसः ।
अप्राप्य योगसंसिद्धिं कां गतिं कृष्ण गच्छति ॥६-३७॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: He who is unable to control himself though he has the faith, and whose mind wanders away from Yoga, what end does he, having failed to attain perfection in Yoga, meet O Krishna?

### 6.38

**Sanskrit**

कच्चिन्नोभयविभ्रष्टश्छिन्नाभ्रमिव नश्यति ।
अप्रतिष्ठो महाबाहो विमूढो ब्रह्मणः पथि ॥६-३८॥

**Simple English**

Swami Sivananda did not comment on this sloka.

### 6.39

**Sanskrit**

एतन्मे संशयं कृष्ण छेत्तुमर्हस्यशेषतः ।
त्वदन्यः संशयस्यास्य छेत्ता न ह्युपपद्यते ॥६-३९॥

**Simple English**

This doubt of mine, O Krishna, do You dispel completely; because it is not possible for any but You to dispel this doubt.

### 6.40

**Sanskrit**

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
पार्थ नैवेह नामुत्र विनाशस्तस्य विद्यते ।
न हि कल्याणकृत्कश्चिद् दुर्गतिं तात गच्छति ॥६-४०॥

**Simple English**

Sri Krishna said: O Arjuna, neither in this world, nor in the next world is there destruction for him; none, truly, who does good, O My son, ever comes to grief.

### 6.41

**Sanskrit**

प्राप्य पुण्यकृतां लोकानुषित्वा शाश्वतीः समाः ।
शुचीनां श्रीमतां गेहे योगभ्रष्टोऽभिजायते ॥६-४१॥

**Simple English**

Having attained to the worlds of the righteous and having dwelt there for everlasting years, he who fell from Yoga is rorn in a house of the pure and wealthy.

### 6.42

**Sanskrit**

अथवा योगिनामेव कुले भवति धीमताम् ।
एतद्धि दुर्लभतरं लोके जन्म यदीदृशम् ॥६-४२॥

**Simple English**

Or he is born in a family of even the wise Yogis; truly a birth like this is very difficult to obtain in this world.

### 6.43

**Sanskrit**

तत्र तं बुद्धिसंयोगं लभते पौर्वदेहिकम् ।
यतते च ततो भूयः संसिद्धौ कुरुनन्दन ॥६-४३॥

**Simple English**

You he comes in touch with the knowledge acired in his former body and strives more than before for perfection, O Arjuna.

### 6.44

**Sanskrit**

पूर्वाभ्यासेन तेनैव ह्रियते ह्यवशोऽपि सः ।
जिज्ञासुरपि योगस्य शब्दब्रह्मातिवर्तते ॥६-४४॥

**Simple English**

By that very former practice he is borne on in spite of himself. Even he who merely wishes to know Yoga goes beyond the Brahmic word.

### 6.45

**Sanskrit**

प्रयत्नाद्यतमानस्तु योगी संशुद्धकिल्बिषः ।
अनेकजन्मसंसिद्धस्ततो याति परां गतिम् ॥६-४५॥

**Simple English**

But the Yogi who strives with assiduity, purified of wrongs and perfected gradually through many births, reaches the highest goal.

### 6.46

**Sanskrit**

तपस्विभ्योऽधिको योगी ज्ञानिभ्योऽपि मतोऽधिकः ।
कर्मिभ्यश्चाधिको योगी तस्माद्योगी भवार्जुन ॥६-४६॥

**Simple English**

The Yogi is thought to be superior to the disciplined seekers and even superior to men of knowledge (obtained through the study of scriptures); he is also superior to men of action; therefore be you a Yogi, O Arjuna.

### 6.47

**Sanskrit**

योगिनामपि सर्वेषां मद्गतेनान्तरात्मना ।
श्रद्धावान्भजते यो मां स मे युक्ततमो मतः ॥६-४७॥

**Simple English**

And among all the Yogis he who, full of faith and with his inner self merged in Me, worships Me is deemed by Me to be the most devout.

## Chapter 7 - Gyaan Vigyana Yoga

### 7.1

**Sanskrit**

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
मय्यासक्तमनाः पार्थ योगं युञ्जन्मदाश्रयः ।
असंशयं समग्रं मां यथा ज्ञास्यसि तच्छृणु ॥७-१॥

**Simple English**

Sri Krishna said: O Arjuna, hear how you shall without doubt know Me fully, with the mind intent on Me, practising Yoga and taking refuge in Me.

### 7.2

**Sanskrit**

ज्ञानं तेऽहं सविज्ञानमिदं वक्ष्याम्यशेषतः ।
यज्ज्ञात्वा नेह भूयोऽन्यज्ज्ञातव्यमवशिष्यते ॥७-२॥

**Simple English**

I shall declare to you in full this knowledge combined with realisation, after knowing which nothing more here remains to be known.

### 7.3

**Sanskrit**

मनुष्याणां सहस्रेषु कश्चिद्यतति सिद्धये ।
यततामपि सिद्धानां कश्चिन्मां वेत्ति तत्त्वतः ॥७-३॥

**Simple English**

Among thousands of men, one perhaps strives for perfection; even among those successful strivers, only one perhaps knows Me in essence.

### 7.4

**Sanskrit**

भूमिरापोऽनलो वायुः खं मनो बुद्धिरेव च ।
अहंकार इतीयं मे भिन्ना प्रकृतिरष्टधा ॥७-४॥

**Simple English**

Earth, water, fire, air, ether, mind, intellect and ego thus is My Nature divided eightfold.

### 7.5

**Sanskrit**

अपरेयमितस्त्वन्यां प्रकृतिं विद्धि मे पराम् ।
जीवभूतां महाबाहो ययेदं धार्यते जगत् ॥७-५॥

**Simple English**

This is the inferior nature, O mighty-armed (Arjuna); know you as different from it My higher nature (Nature), the very life-element, by which this world is upheld.

### 7.6

**Sanskrit**

एतद्योनीनि भूतानि सर्वाणीत्युपधारय ।
अहं कृत्स्नस्य जगतः प्रभवः प्रलयस्तथा ॥७-६॥

**Simple English**

Know that these two (Natures) are the womb of all beings. So I am the source and dissolution of the whole universe.

### 7.7

**Sanskrit**

मत्तः परतरं नान्यत्किञ्चिदस्ति धनञ्जय ।
मयि सर्वमिदं प्रोतं सूत्रे मणिगणा इव ॥७-७॥

**Simple English**

There is nothing whatsoever higher than Me, O Arjuna. All this is strung on Me, as clusters of gems on a string.

### 7.8

**Sanskrit**

रसोऽहमप्सु कौन्तेय प्रभास्मि शशिसूर्ययोः ।
प्रणवः सर्ववेदेषु शब्दः खे पौरुषं नृषु ॥७-८॥

**Simple English**

I am the sapidity in water, O Arjuna; I am the light in the moon and the sun; I am the syllable Om in all the Vedas, sound in ether and virility in men.

### 7.9

**Sanskrit**

पुण्यो गन्धः पृथिव्यां च तेजश्चास्मि विभावसौ ।
जीवनं सर्वभूतेषु तपश्चास्मि तपस्विषु ॥७-९॥

**Simple English**

I am the sweet fragrance in the earth and the brilliance in the fire, the life in all beings, and I am the self-discipline in disciplined seekers.

### 7.10

**Sanskrit**

बीजं मां सर्वभूतानां विद्धि पार्थ सनातनम् ।
बुद्धिर्बुद्धिमतामस्मि तेजस्तेजस्विनामहम् ॥७-१०॥

**Simple English**

Know Me, O Arjuna, as the eternal seed of all beings; I am the intelligence of the intelligent; the splendour of the splendid objects am I.

### 7.11

**Sanskrit**

बलं बलवतां चाहं कामरागविवर्जितम् ।
धर्माविरुद्धो भूतेषु कामोऽस्मि भरतर्षभ ॥७-११॥

**Simple English**

Of the strong, I am the strength devoid of desire and attachment, and in (all) beings, I am the desire unopposed to Dharma, O Arjuna.

### 7.12

**Sanskrit**

ये चैव सात्त्विका भावा राजसास्तामसाश्च ये ।
मत्त एवेति तान्विद्धि न त्वहं तेषु ते मयि ॥७-१२॥

**Simple English**

Whatever beings (and objects) that are pure, active and inert, know that they proceed from Me. They are in Me, yet I am not in them.

### 7.13

**Sanskrit**

त्रिभिर्गुणमयैर्भावैरेभिः सर्वमिदं जगत् ।
मोहितं नाभिजानाति मामेभ्यः परमव्ययम् ॥७-१३॥

**Simple English**

Deluded by these Natures (states or things) composed of the three qualities of Nature all this world does not know Me as distinct from them and unchanging.

### 7.14

**Sanskrit**

दैवी ह्येषा गुणमयी मम माया दुरत्यया ।
मामेव ये प्रपद्यन्ते मायामेतां तरन्ति ते ॥७-१४॥

**Simple English**

This divine maya of mine, made of the gunas, is difficult to cross. Those who take refuge in me cross over this maya.

### 7.15

**Sanskrit**

न मां दुष्कृतिनो मूढाः प्रपद्यन्ते नराधमाः ।
माययापहृतज्ञाना आसुरं भावमाश्रिताः ॥७-१५॥

**Simple English**

The evil-doers and the confused who are the lowest of men do not seek Me; they whose knowledge is destroyed by illusion follow the ways of demons.

### 7.16

**Sanskrit**

चतुर्विधा भजन्ते मां जनाः सुकृतिनोऽर्जुन ।
आर्तो जिज्ञासुरर्थार्थी ज्ञानी च भरतर्षभ ॥७-१६॥

**Simple English**

Four kinds of virtuous men worship Me, O Arjuna, and they are the distressed, the seeker of knowledge, the seeker of wealth and the wise, O lord of the Bharatas.

### 7.17

**Sanskrit**

तेषां ज्ञानी नित्ययुक्त एकभक्तिर्विशिष्यते ।
प्रियो हि ज्ञानिनोऽत्यर्थमहं स च मम प्रियः ॥७-१७॥

**Simple English**

Of them the wise, ever steady and devoted to the One, excels (is the best); for I am exceedingly dear to the wise and he is dear to Me.

### 7.18

**Sanskrit**

उदाराः सर्व एवैते ज्ञानी त्वात्मैव मे मतम् ।
आस्थितः स हि युक्तात्मा मामेवानुत्तमां गतिम् ॥७-१८॥

**Simple English**

Noble indeed are all these; but I deem the wise man as My very Self; for, steady in mind he is established in Me alone as the supreme goal.

### 7.19

**Sanskrit**

बहूनां जन्मनामन्ते ज्ञानवान्मां प्रपद्यते ।
वासुदेवः सर्वमिति स महात्मा सुदुर्लभः ॥७-१९॥

**Simple English**

At the end of many births the wise man comes to Me, realising that all this is Vaasudeva (the innermost Self); such a great soul (Mahatma) is very hard to find.

### 7.20

**Sanskrit**

कामैस्तैस्तैर्हृतज्ञानाः प्रपद्यन्तेऽन्यदेवताः ।
तं तं नियममास्थाय प्रकृत्या नियताः स्वया ॥७-२०॥

**Simple English**

Those whose wisdom has been rent away by this or that desire, go to other gods, following this or that rite, led by their own nature.

### 7.21

**Sanskrit**

यो यो यां यां तनुं भक्तः श्रद्धयार्चितुमिच्छति ।
तस्य तस्याचलां श्रद्धां तामेव विदधाम्यहम् ॥७-२१॥

**Simple English**

Whatsoever form any devotee desires to worship with faith that (same) faith of his I make firm and unflinching.

### 7.22

**Sanskrit**

स तया श्रद्धया युक्तस्तस्याराधनमीहते ।
लभते च ततः कामान्मयैव विहितान्हि तान् ॥७-२२॥

**Simple English**

Endowed with that faith, he engages in the worship of that (form) and from it he obtains his desire, these being truly ordained by Me (alone).

### 7.23

**Sanskrit**

अन्तवत्तु फलं तेषां तद्भवत्यल्पमेधसाम् ।
देवान्देवयजो यान्ति मद्भक्ता यान्ति मामपि ॥७-२३॥

**Simple English**

Truly the reward (fruit) that accrues to those men of small intelligence is finite. The worshippers of the gods go to them, but My devotees come to Me.

### 7.24

**Sanskrit**

अव्यक्तं व्यक्तिमापन्नं मन्यन्ते मामबुद्धयः ।
परं भावमजानन्तो ममाव्ययमनुत्तमम् ॥७-२४॥

**Simple English**

The foolish think of Me, the Unseen, as having manifestation, knowing not My higher, unchanging and most excellent nature.

### 7.25

**Sanskrit**

नाहं प्रकाशः सर्वस्य योगमायासमावृतः ।
मूढोऽयं नाभिजानाति लोको मामजमव्ययम् ॥७-२५॥

**Simple English**

I am not manifest to all (as I am) veiled by the Yoga-divine illusion. This confused world does not know Me, the unborn and eternal.

### 7.26

**Sanskrit**

वेदाहं समतीतानि वर्तमानानि चार्जुन ।
भविष्याणि च भूतानि मां तु वेद न कश्चन ॥७-२६॥

**Simple English**

I know, O Arjuna, the beings of the past, the present and the future, but no one knows Me.

### 7.27

**Sanskrit**

इच्छाद्वेषसमुत्थेन द्वन्द्वमोहेन भारत ।
सर्वभूतानि सम्मोहं सर्गे यान्ति परन्तप ॥७-२७॥

**Simple English**

By the confusion of the pairs of opposites arising from desire and aversion, O Bharata, all beings are subject to confusion at birth, O Parantapa.

### 7.28

**Sanskrit**

येषां त्वन्तगतं पापं जनानां पुण्यकर्मणाम् ।
ते द्वन्द्वमोहनिर्मुक्ता भजन्ते मां दृढव्रताः ॥७-२८॥

**Simple English**

But those men of virtuous deeds whose wrongs have come to an end, and who are freed from the confusion of the pairs of opposites, worship Me, steady in their vows.

### 7.29

**Sanskrit**

जरामरणमोक्षाय मामाश्रित्य यतन्ति ये ।
ते ब्रह्म तद्विदुः कृत्स्नमध्यात्मं कर्म चाखिलम् ॥७-२९॥

**Simple English**

Those who strive for freedom from old age and death, taking refuge in Me, realise in full that the Supreme Reality, the whole knowledge of the Self and all action.

### 7.30

**Sanskrit**

साधिभूताधिदैवं मां साधियज्ञं च ये विदुः ।
प्रयाणकालेऽपि च मां ते विदुर्युक्तचेतसः ॥७-३०॥

**Simple English**

Those who know Me with the Adhibhuta (pertaining to the elements), Adhidaiva (pertaining to the gods) and the Adhiyajna (pertaining to the sacrifice) know Me even at the time of death, steady in mind.

## Chapter 8 - Akshara Brahma Yoga

### 8.1

**Sanskrit**

अर्जुन उवाच ।
किं तद् ब्रह्म किमध्यात्मं किं कर्म पुरुषोत्तम ।
अधिभूतं च किं प्रोक्तमधिदैवं किमुच्यते ॥८-१॥

**Simple English**

Swami Sivananda did not comment on this sloka.

### 8.2

**Sanskrit**

अधियज्ञः कथं कोऽत्र देहेऽस्मिन्मधुसूदन ।
प्रयाणकाले च कथं ज्ञेयोऽसि नियतात्मभिः ॥८-२॥

**Simple English**

Who and how is Adhiyajna here in this body, O destroyer of Madhu (Krishna)? And how at the time of death, are You to be known by the self-controlled?

### 8.3

**Sanskrit**

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
अक्षरं ब्रह्म परमं स्वभावोऽध्यात्ममुच्यते ।
भूतभावोद्भवकरो विसर्गः कर्मसंज्ञितः ॥८-३॥

**Simple English**

Sri Krishna said: the Supreme Reality is the Imperishable, the Supreme; Its essential nature is called Self-knowledge; the offering (to the gods) which causes existence and manifestation of beings and which also sustains them is called action.

### 8.4

**Sanskrit**

अधिभूतं क्षरो भावः पुरुषश्चाधिदैवतम् ।
अधियज्ञोऽहमेवात्र देहे देहभृतां वर ॥८-४॥

**Simple English**

Adhibhuta (knowledge of the elements) pertains to My temporary Nature and the conscious Self or the Soul is the Adhidaiva; I alone am the Adhiyajna here in this body, O best among the embodied (men).

### 8.5

**Sanskrit**

अन्तकाले च मामेव स्मरन्मुक्त्वा कलेवरम् ।
यः प्रयाति स मद्भावं याति नास्त्यत्र संशयः ॥८-५॥

**Simple English**

And whoever, leaving the body, goes forth remembering Me alone, at the time of death, he attains My Being: there is no doubt about this.

### 8.6

**Sanskrit**

यं यं वापि स्मरन्भावं त्यजत्यन्ते कलेवरम् ।
तं तमेवैति कौन्तेय सदा तद्भावभावितः ॥८-६॥

**Simple English**

Whosoever at the end leaves the body, thinking of any being, to that being only does he go, O son of Kunti (Arjuna), because of his constant thought of that being.

### 8.7

**Sanskrit**

तस्मात्सर्वेषु कालेषु मामनुस्मर युध्य च ।
मय्यर्पितमनोबुद्धिर्मामेवैष्यस्यसंशयः (orसंशयम्) ॥८-७॥

**Simple English**

Therefore at all times remember Me only and fight. With mind and intellect fixed (or absorbed) in Me, you will doubtessly come to Me alone.

### 8.8

**Sanskrit**

अभ्यासयोगयुक्तेन चेतसा नान्यगामिना ।
परमं पुरुषं दिव्यं याति पार्थानुचिन्तयन् ॥८-८॥

**Simple English**

With the mind not moving towards any other thing, made steady by the method of habitual meditation, and constantly meditating, one goes to the Supreme Person, the Resplendent, O Arjuna.

### 8.9

**Sanskrit**

कविं पुराणमनुशासितार-
मणोरणीयंसमनुस्मरेद्यः ।
सर्वस्य धातारमचिन्त्यरूप-
मादित्यवर्णं तमसः परस्तात् ॥८-९॥

**Simple English**

Whosoever meditates on the Omniscient, the Ancient, the Ruler (of the whole world), minuter than an atom, the supporter of all, of inconceivable form, effulgent like the sun and beyond the darkness of ignorance.

### 8.10

**Sanskrit**

प्रयाणकाले मनसाऽचलेन
भक्त्या युक्तो योगबलेन चैव ।
भ्रुवोर्मध्ये प्राणमावेश्य सम्यक्
स तं परं पुरुषमुपैति दिव्यम् ॥८-१०॥

**Simple English**

At the time of death, with unshaken mind, endowed with devotion, by the power of Yoga, fixing the whole life-breath in the middle of the two eyebrows, he reaches that resplendent Supreme Person.

### 8.11

**Sanskrit**

यदक्षरं वेदविदो वदन्ति
विशन्ति यद्यतयो वीतरागाः ।
यदिच्छन्तो ब्रह्मचर्यं चरन्ति
तत्ते पदं संग्रहेण प्रवक्ष्ये ॥८-११॥

**Simple English**

That which is declared Imperishable by those who know the Vedas, that which the self-controlled (disciplined seekers or Sannyasins) and passion-free enter, that desiring which celibacy is practised that goal I will declare to you in brief.

### 8.12

**Sanskrit**

सर्वद्वाराणि संयम्य मनो हृदि निरुध्य च ।
मूध्न्यार्धायात्मनः प्राणमास्थितो योगधारणाम् ॥८-१२॥

**Simple English**

Having closed all the gates, confined the mind in the heart and fixed the life-breath in the head, engaged in the practice of concentration.

### 8.13

**Sanskrit**

ओमित्येकाक्षरं ब्रह्म व्याहरन्मामनुस्मरन् ।
यः प्रयाति त्यजन्देहं स याति परमां गतिम् ॥८-१३॥

**Simple English**

Uttering the one-syllabled Om the Supreme Reality and remembering Me, he who departs, leaving the body, attains to the Supreme Goal.

### 8.14

**Sanskrit**

अनन्यचेताः सततं यो मां स्मरति नित्यशः ।
तस्याहं सुलभः पार्थ नित्ययुक्तस्य योगिनः ॥८-१४॥

**Simple English**

I am easily attainable by that ever-steady Yogi who constantly and daily remembers Me (for a long time), not thinking of anything else (with a single mind or one-pointed mind), O Partha (Arjuna).

### 8.15

**Sanskrit**

मामुपेत्य पुनर्जन्म दुःखालयमशाश्वतम् ।
नाप्नुवन्ति महात्मानः संसिद्धिं परमां गताः ॥८-१५॥

**Simple English**

Having attained Me these great souls do not again take birth (here) which is the place of pain and is non-eternal: they have reached the highest perfection (freedom).

### 8.16

**Sanskrit**

आब्रह्मभुवनाल्लोकाः पुनरावर्तिनोऽर्जुन ।
मामुपेत्य तु कौन्तेय पुनर्जन्म न विद्यते ॥८-१६॥

**Simple English**

(All) the worlds including the world of Brahma are subject to return again, O Arjuna; but he who reaches Me, O son of Kunti, has no rirth.

### 8.17

**Sanskrit**

सहस्रयुगपर्यन्तमहर्यद् ब्रह्मणो विदुः ।
रात्रिं युगसहस्रान्तां तेऽहोरात्रविदो जनाः ॥८-१७॥

**Simple English**

Those people who know the day of Brahma which is of a duration of a thousand Yugas (ages) and the night which is also of a thousand Yugas duration, they know day and night.

### 8.18

**Sanskrit**

अव्यक्ताद् व्यक्तयः सर्वाः प्रभवन्त्यहरागमे ।
रात्र्यागमे प्रलीयन्ते तत्रैवाव्यक्तसंज्ञके ॥८-१८॥

**Simple English**

From the Unseen all the manifested (worlds) proceed at the coming of the 'day'; at the coming of the 'night' they dissolve truly into that alone which is called the Unseen.

### 8.19

**Sanskrit**

भूतग्रामः स एवायं भूत्वा भूत्वा प्रलीयते ।
रात्र्यागमेऽवशः पार्थ प्रभवत्यहरागमे ॥८-१९॥

**Simple English**

This same multitude of beings, being born again and again, is dissolved, helplessly, O Arjuna (into the Unseen) at the coming of the night and comes forth at the coming of the day.

### 8.20

**Sanskrit**

परस्तस्मात्तु भावोऽन्योऽव्यक्तोऽव्यक्तात्सनातनः ।
यः स सर्वेषु भूतेषु नश्यत्सु न विनश्यति ॥८-२०॥

**Simple English**

But truly there exists, higher than this Unseen, another unseen Eternal, which is not destroyed when all beings are destroyed.

### 8.21

**Sanskrit**

अव्यक्तोऽक्षर इत्युक्तस्तमाहुः परमां गतिम् ।
यं प्राप्य न निवर्तन्ते तद्धाम परमं मम ॥८-२१॥

**Simple English**

What is called the Unseen and the Imperishable, That they say is the highest goal. They who reach It do not return (to this worldly cycle). That is My highest abode (place or state).

### 8.22

**Sanskrit**

पुरुषः स परः पार्थ भक्त्या लभ्यस्त्वनन्यया ।
यस्यान्तःस्थानि भूतानि येन सर्वमिदं ततम् ॥८-२२॥

**Simple English**

That highest the conscious Self, O Arjuna, is attainable by unswerving devotion to Him alone within Whom all beings dwell and by Whom all this is pervaded.

### 8.23

**Sanskrit**

यत्र काले त्वनावृत्तिमावृत्तिं चैव योगिनः ।
प्रयाता यान्ति तं कालं वक्ष्यामि भरतर्षभ ॥८-२३॥

**Simple English**

Now I will tell you, O chief of Bharatas, the times departing at which the Yogis will return or not return.

### 8.24

**Sanskrit**

अग्निर्जोतिरहः शुक्लः षण्मासा उत्तरायणम् ।
तत्र प्रयाता गच्छन्ति ब्रह्म ब्रह्मविदो जनाः ॥८-२४॥

**Simple English**

Fire, light daytime, the bright fortnight, the six months of the northern path of the sun (the northern solstice) departing then (by these) men who know the Supreme Reality go to the Supreme Reality.

### 8.25

**Sanskrit**

धूमो रात्रिस्तथा कृष्णः षण्मासा दक्षिणायनम् ।
तत्र चान्द्रमसं ज्योतिर्योगी प्राप्य निवर्तते ॥८-२५॥

**Simple English**

Attaining to the lunar light by smoke, night time, the dark fortnight also, the six months of the southern path of the sun (the southern solstice), the Yogi returns.

### 8.26

**Sanskrit**

शुक्लकृष्णे गती ह्येते जगतः शाश्वते मते ।
एकया यात्यनावृत्तिमन्ययावर्तते पुनः ॥८-२६॥

**Simple English**

The bright and the dark paths of the world are truly thought to be eternal; by the one (the bright path) a man goes not to return and by the other (the dark path) he returns.

### 8.27

**Sanskrit**

नैते सृती पार्थ जानन्योगी मुह्यति कश्चन ।
तस्मात्सर्वेषु कालेषु योगयुक्तो भवार्जुन ॥८-२७॥

**Simple English**

Knowing these paths, O Arjuna, no Yogi is confused; therefore at all times be steady in Yoga.

### 8.28

**Sanskrit**

वेदेषु यज्ञेषु तपःसु चैव
दानेषु यत्पुण्यफलं प्रदिष्टम् ।
अत्येति तत्सर्वमिदं विदित्वा
योगी परं स्थानमुपैति चाद्यम् ॥८-२८॥

**Simple English**

Whatever fruit of merit is declared (in the scriptures) to accrue from (the study of) the Vedas, (the performance of) sacrifices, (the practice of) self-discipline, and gifts beyond all this goes the Yogi, having known this; and he attains to the Supreme Primeval (first or ancient) Abode.

## Chapter 9 - Raja Vidya Yoga

### 9.1

**Sanskrit**

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
इदं तु ते गुह्यतमं प्रवक्ष्याम्यनसूयवे ।
ज्ञानं विज्ञानसहितं यज्ज्ञात्वा मोक्ष्यसेऽशुभात् ॥९-१॥

**Simple English**

Sri Krishna said: I shall now declare to you who does not cavil, the greatest secret, the knowledge combined with experience (Self-realisation). Having known this you will be free from evil.

### 9.2

**Sanskrit**

राजविद्या राजगुह्यं पवित्रमिदमुत्तमम् ।
प्रत्यक्षावगमं धर्म्यं सुसुखं कर्तुमव्ययम् ॥९-२॥

**Simple English**

This is the kingl science, the kingly secret, the supreme purifier, realisable by direct intuitional knowledge, according to righteousness, very easy to perform and eternal.

### 9.3

**Sanskrit**

अश्रद्दधानाः पुरुषा धर्मस्यास्य परन्तप ।
अप्राप्य मां निवर्तन्ते मृत्युसंसारवर्त्मनि ॥९-३॥

**Simple English**

Those who have no faith in this Dharma (knowledge of the Self), O Parantapa (Arjuna), return to the path of this world of death without attaining Me.

### 9.4

**Sanskrit**

मया ततमिदं सर्वं जगदव्यक्तमूर्तिना ।
मत्स्थानि सर्वभूतानि न चाहं तेष्ववस्थितः ॥९-४॥

**Simple English**

All this world is pervaded by Me in My unseen aspect; all beings exist in Me, but I do not dwell in them.

### 9.5

**Sanskrit**

न च मत्स्थानि भूतानि पश्य मे योगमैश्वरम् ।
भूतभृन्न च भूतस्थो ममात्मा भूतभावनः ॥९-५॥

**Simple English**

Nor do beings exist in Me (in reality); see My divine Yoga, supporting all beings, but not dwelling in them, is My Self, the efficient cause of beings.

### 9.6

**Sanskrit**

यथाकाशस्थितो नित्यं वायुः सर्वत्रगो महान् ।
तथा सर्वाणि भूतानि मत्स्थानीत्युपधारय ॥९-६॥

**Simple English**

As the mighty wind, moving everywhere, rests always in the ether, even so, know you that all beings rest in Me.

### 9.7

**Sanskrit**

सर्वभूतानि कौन्तेय प्रकृतिं यान्ति मामिकाम् ।
कल्पक्षये पुनस्तानि कल्पादौ विसृजाम्यहम् ॥९-७॥

**Simple English**

All beings, O Arjuna, go into My Nature at the end of a Kalpa; I send them forth again at the beginning of (the next) Kalpa.

### 9.8

**Sanskrit**

प्रकृतिं स्वामवष्टभ्य विसृजामि पुनः पुनः ।
भूतग्राममिमं कृत्स्नमवशं प्रकृतेर्वशात् ॥९-८॥

**Simple English**

Animating My Nature, I again and again send forth all this multitude of beings, helpless by the force of the Nature.

### 9.9

**Sanskrit**

न च मां तानि कर्माणि निबध्नन्ति धनञ्जय ।
उदासीनवदासीनमसक्तं तेषु कर्मसु ॥९-९॥

**Simple English**

These acts do not bind Me, O Arjuna, sitting like one indifferent, unattached to those acts.

### 9.10

**Sanskrit**

मयाध्यक्षेण प्रकृतिः सूयते सचराचरम् ।
हेतुनानेन कौन्तेय जगद्विपरिवर्तते ॥९-१०॥

**Simple English**

Under Me as supervisor, Nature produces the moving and the unmoving; because of this, O Arjuna, the world revolves.

### 9.11

**Sanskrit**

अवजानन्ति मां मूढा मानुषीं तनुमाश्रितम् ।
परं भावमजानन्तो मम भूतमहेश्वरम् ॥९-११॥

**Simple English**

Fools disregard Me, clad in human form, not knowing My higher Being as the great Lord of (all) beings.

### 9.12

**Sanskrit**

मोघाशा मोघकर्माणो मोघज्ञाना विचेतसः ।
राक्षसीमासुरीं चैव प्रकृतिं मोहिनीं श्रिताः ॥९-१२॥

**Simple English**

Of vain hopes, of vain actions, of vain knowledge and senseless, they truly are possessed of the deceitful nature of demons and undivine beings.

### 9.13

**Sanskrit**

महात्मानस्तु मां पार्थ दैवीं प्रकृतिमाश्रिताः ।
भजन्त्यनन्यमनसो ज्ञात्वा भूतादिमव्ययम् ॥९-१३॥

**Simple English**

But the great souls, O Arjuna, partaking of My divine nature, worship Me with a single mind (with the mind devoted to nothing else), knowing Me as the eternal source of beings.

### 9.14

**Sanskrit**

सततं कीर्तयन्तो मां यतन्तश्च दृढव्रताः ।
नमस्यन्तश्च मां भक्त्या नित्ययुक्ता उपासते ॥९-१४॥

**Simple English**

Always glorifying Me, striving,firm in vows, prostrating themselves before Me, they worship Me with devotion always steady.

### 9.15

**Sanskrit**

ज्ञानयज्ञेन चाप्यन्ये यजन्तो मामुपासते ।
एकत्वेन पृथक्त्वेन बहुधा विश्वतोमुखम् ॥९-१५॥

**Simple English**

Others also sacrificing with the wisdom-sacrifice worship Me, the All-faced, as one, as distinct, and as manifold.

### 9.16

**Sanskrit**

अहं क्रतुरहं यज्ञः स्वधाहमहमौषधम् ।
मन्त्रोऽहमहमेवाज्यमहमग्निरहं हुतम् ॥९-१६॥

**Simple English**

I am the Kratu; I am the Yajna; I am the offering (food) to the manes; I am the medicinal herbs and all the plants; I am the Mantra; I am also the Ghee or the melted butter; I am the fire; I am the oblation.

### 9.17

**Sanskrit**

पिताहमस्य जगतो माता धाता पितामहः ।
वेद्यं पवित्रमोंकार ऋक्साम यजुरेव च ॥९-१७॥

**Simple English**

I am the father of this world, the mother, the dispenser of the fruits of actions and the grandfather; the (one) thing to be known, the purifier, the sacred monosyllable (Om), and also the Rik-, the Sama-and the Yajur-Vedas.

### 9.18

**Sanskrit**

गतिर्भर्ता प्रभुः साक्षी निवासः शरणं सुहृत् ।
प्रभवः प्रलयः स्थानं निधानं बीजमव्ययम् ॥९-१८॥

**Simple English**

I am the goal, the supporter, the Lord, the witness, the abode, the shelter, the friend, the origin, the dissolution, the foundation, the treasure-house and the seed which is eternal.

### 9.19

**Sanskrit**

तपाम्यहमहं वर्षं निगृह्णाम्युत्सृजामि च ।
अमृतं चैव मृत्युश्च सदसच्चाहमर्जुन ॥९-१९॥

**Simple English**

(As the sun) I give heat; I withhold and send forth the rain; I am immortality and also death, existence and non-existence, O Arjuna.

### 9.20

**Sanskrit**

त्रैविद्या मां सोमपाः पूतपापा
यज्ञैरिष्ट्वा स्वर्गतिं प्रार्थयन्ते ।
ते पुण्यमासाद्य सुरेन्द्रलोक-
मश्नन्ति दिव्यान्दिवि देवभोगान् ॥९-२०॥

**Simple English**

The knowers of the three Vedas, the drinkers of Soma, purified of all wrongs, worshipping Me by sacrifices, pray for the way to heaven; they reach the holy world of the Lord of the gods and enjoy in heaven the divine pleasures of the gods.

### 9.21

**Sanskrit**

ते तं भुक्त्वा स्वर्गलोकं विशालं
क्षीणे पुण्ये मर्त्यलोकं विशन्ति ।
एवं त्रयीधर्ममनुप्रपन्ना
गतागतं कामकामा लभन्ते ॥९-२१॥

**Simple English**

They, having enjoyed the vast heaven, enter the world of mortals when their merit is exhausted; thus abiding by the injunctions of the three (Vedas) and desiring (objects of) desires, they attain to the state of going and returning.

### 9.22

**Sanskrit**

अनन्याश्चिन्तयन्तो मां ये जनाः पर्युपासते ।
तेषां नित्याभियुक्तानां योगक्षेमं वहाम्यहम् ॥९-२२॥

**Simple English**

For those who think of me with single-minded love and worship me steadily, I take care of what they need and protect what they have.

### 9.23

**Sanskrit**

येऽप्यन्यदेवता भक्ता यजन्ते श्रद्धयान्विताः ।
तेऽपि मामेव कौन्तेय यजन्त्यविधिपूर्वकम् ॥९-२३॥

**Simple English**

Even those who worship other gods with faith are, in truth, worshipping me, O Arjuna, though not according to the full understanding.

### 9.24

**Sanskrit**

अहं हि सर्वयज्ञानां भोक्ता च प्रभुरेव च ।
न तु मामभिजानन्ति तत्त्वेनातश्च्यवन्ति ते ॥९-२४॥

**Simple English**

(For) I alone am the enjoyer and also the Lord of all sacrifices; but they do not know Me in essence (in reality), and therefore they fall (return to this mortal world).

### 9.25

**Sanskrit**

यान्ति देवव्रता देवान्पितॄन्यान्ति पितृव्रताः ।
भूतानि यान्ति भूतेज्या यान्ति मद्याजिनोऽपि माम् ॥९-२५॥

**Simple English**

The worshippers of the gods go to them; to the manes go the ancestor-worshippers; to the deities who preside over the elements go their worshippers; but My devotees come to Me.

### 9.26

**Sanskrit**

पत्रं पुष्पं फलं तोयं यो मे भक्त्या प्रयच्छति ।
तदहं भक्त्युपहृतमश्नामि प्रयतात्मनः ॥९-२६॥

**Simple English**

Whoever offers Me with devotion a leaf, a flower, a fruit or a little water that, so offered devotedly by the pure-minded, I accept.

### 9.27

**Sanskrit**

यत्करोषि यदश्नासि यज्जुहोषि ददासि यत् ।
यत्तपस्यसि कौन्तेय तत्कुरुष्व मदर्पणम् ॥९-२७॥

**Simple English**

Whatever you do, whatever you eat, whatever you offer in sacrifice, whatever you give, whatever you practise as self-discipline, O Arjuna, do it as an offering to Me.

### 9.28

**Sanskrit**

शुभाशुभफलैरेवं मोक्ष्यसे कर्मबन्धनैः ।
संन्यासयोगयुक्तात्मा विमुक्तो मामुपैष्यसि ॥९-२८॥

**Simple English**

Thus will you be freed from the bonds of actions yielding good and evil fruits; with the mind steady in the Yoga of renunciation, and free, you will come to Me.

### 9.29

**Sanskrit**

समोऽहं सर्वभूतेषु न मे द्वेष्योऽस्ति न प्रियः ।
ये भजन्ति तु मां भक्त्या मयि ते तेषु चाप्यहम् ॥९-२९॥

**Simple English**

The same am I to all beings; to Me there is none hateful or dear; but those who worship Me with devotion are in Me and I am also in them.

### 9.30

**Sanskrit**

अपि चेत्सुदुराचारो भजते मामनन्यभाक् ।
साधुरेव स मन्तव्यः सम्यग्व्यवसितो हि सः ॥९-३०॥

**Simple English**

Even if the most wrong worships Me, with devotion to none else, he too should indeed by regarded as righteous for he has rightly resolved.

### 9.31

**Sanskrit**

क्षिप्रं भवति धर्मात्मा शश्वच्छान्तिं निगच्छति ।
कौन्तेय प्रतिजानीहि न मे भक्तः प्रणश्यति ॥९-३१॥

**Simple English**

Soon he becomes righteous and attains to eternal peace; O Arjuna, proclaim you for certain that My devotee never perishes.

### 9.32

**Sanskrit**

मां हि पार्थ व्यपाश्रित्य येऽपि स्युः पापयोनयः ।
स्त्रियो वैश्यास्तथा शूद्रास्तेऽपि यान्ति परां गतिम् ॥९-३२॥

**Simple English**

For, taking refuge in Me, they also who, O Arjuna, may be of a wrong birth women, Vaisyas as well as Sudras attain the Supreme Goal.

### 9.33

**Sanskrit**

किं पुनर्ब्राह्मणाः पुण्या भक्ता राजर्षयस्तथा ।
अनित्यमसुखं लोकमिमं प्राप्य भजस्व माम् ॥९-३३॥

**Simple English**

How much more (easily) then the hold Brahmins and devoted royal saints (attain the goal); having come to this impermanent and unhappy world, do you worship Me.

### 9.34

**Sanskrit**

मन्मना भव मद्भक्तो मद्याजी मां नमस्कुरु ।
मामेवैष्यसि युक्त्वैवमात्मानं मत्परायणः ॥९-३४॥

**Simple English**

Fix your mind on Me; by devoted to Me; sacrifice to Me; bow down to Me; having thus united your whole self to Me, taking Me as the supreme goal, you will come to Me.

## Chapter 10 - Vibhooti Yoga

### 10.1

**Sanskrit**

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
भूय एव महाबाहो शृणु मे परमं वचः ।
यत्तेऽहं प्रीयमाणाय वक्ष्यामि हितकाम्यया ॥१०-१॥

**Simple English**

Sri Krishna said: Again, O mighty-armed Arjuna, listen to my supreme word which I will declare to you who who are beloved, for your welfare.

### 10.2

**Sanskrit**

न मे विदुः सुरगणाः प्रभवं न महर्षयः ।
अहमादिर्हि देवानां महर्षीणां च सर्वशः ॥१०-२॥

**Simple English**

Neither the hosts of the gods nor the great sages know My origin; for in every way I am the source of all the gods and the great sages.

### 10.3

**Sanskrit**

यो मामजमनादिं च वेत्ति लोकमहेश्वरम् ।
असम्मूढः स मर्त्येषु सर्वपापैः प्रमुच्यते ॥१०-३॥

**Simple English**

He who knows Me as unborn and beginningless, as the great Lord of the worlds, he, among mortals, is undeluded and he is free from all wrongs.

### 10.4

**Sanskrit**

बुद्धिर्ज्ञानमसम्मोहः क्षमा सत्यं दमः शमः ।
सुखं दुःखं भवोऽभावो भयं चाभयमेव च ॥१०-४॥

**Simple English**

Intellect, wisdom, non-confusion, forgiveness, truth, self-restraint, calmness, happiness, pain, existence or birth, non-existence or death, fear and also fearlessness.

### 10.5

**Sanskrit**

अहिंसा समता तुष्टिस्तपो दानं यशोऽयशः ।
भवन्ति भावा भूतानां मत्त एव पृथग्विधाः ॥१०-५॥

**Simple English**

Non-injury, equal-mindedness, contentment, self-discipline, beneficence, fame, ill-fame (these) different kinds of qualities of beings arise from Me alone.

### 10.6

**Sanskrit**

महर्षयः सप्त पूर्वे चत्वारो मनवस्तथा ।
मद्भावा मानसा जाता येषां लोक इमाः प्रजाः ॥१०-६॥

**Simple English**

The seven great sages, the ancient four and also the Manus, possessed of powers like Me (on account of their minds being fixed on Me), were born of (My) mind; from them are these creatures born in this world.

### 10.7

**Sanskrit**

एतां विभूतिं योगं च मम यो वेत्ति तत्त्वतः ।
सोऽविकम्पेन योगेन युज्यते नात्र संशयः ॥१०-७॥

**Simple English**

He who in truth knows these manifold manifestations of My Being and (this) Yoga-power of Mine becomes established in the unshakable Yoga; there is no doubt about it.

### 10.8

**Sanskrit**

अहं सर्वस्य प्रभवो मत्तः सर्वं प्रवर्तते ।
इति मत्वा भजन्ते मां बुधा भावसमन्विताः ॥१०-८॥

**Simple English**

I am the source of everything. Everything comes from me. Knowing this, the wise worship me with deep devotion.

### 10.9

**Sanskrit**

मच्चित्ता मद्गतप्राणा बोधयन्तः परस्परम् ।
कथयन्तश्च मां नित्यं तुष्यन्ति च रमन्ति च ॥१०-९॥

**Simple English**

With their mind and their life wholly absorbed in Me, enlightening each other and ever speaking of Me, they are satisfied and delighted.

### 10.10

**Sanskrit**

तेषां सततयुक्तानां भजतां प्रीतिपूर्वकम् ।
ददामि बुद्धियोगं तं येन मामुपयान्ति ते ॥१०-१०॥

**Simple English**

To them who are ever steady, worshipping Me with love, I give the Yoga of clear understanding by which they come to Me.

### 10.11

**Sanskrit**

तेषामेवानुकम्पार्थमहमज्ञानजं तमः ।
नाशयाम्यात्मभावस्थो ज्ञानदीपेन भास्वता ॥१०-११॥

**Simple English**

Out of mere compassion for them, I, dwelling within their Self, destroy the darkness born of ignorance by the luminous lamp of knowledge.

### 10.12

**Sanskrit**

अर्जुन उवाच ।
परं ब्रह्म परं धाम पवित्रं परमं भवान् ।
पुरुषं शाश्वतं दिव्यमादिदेवमजं विभुम् ॥१०-१२॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: You are the Supreme the Supreme Reality, the supreme abode (or the supreme light), the supreme purifier, eternal, divine Person, the primeval God, unborn andn omnipresent.

### 10.13

**Sanskrit**

आहुस्त्वामृषयः सर्वे देवर्षिर्नारदस्तथा ।
असितो देवलो व्यासः स्वयं चैव ब्रवीषि मे ॥१०-१३॥

**Simple English**

All the sages have thus declared You, as also the divine sage Narada; so also Asita, Devala and Vyasa; and now You yourself say so to me.

### 10.14

**Sanskrit**

सर्वमेतदृतं मन्ये यन्मां वदसि केशव ।
न हि ते भगवन्व्यक्तिं विदुर्देवा न दानवाः ॥१०-१४॥

**Simple English**

I believe all this that You say to me to be true, O Krishna; truly, O blessed Lord! neither the gods nor the demons know Your manifestation (origin).

### 10.15

**Sanskrit**

स्वयमेवात्मनात्मानं वेत्थ त्वं पुरुषोत्तम ।
भूतभावन भूतेश देवदेव जगत्पते ॥१०-१५॥

**Simple English**

Swami Sivananda did not comment on this sloka.

### 10.16

**Sanskrit**

वक्तुमर्हस्यशेषेण दिव्या ह्यात्मविभूतयः ।
याभिर्विभूतिभिर्लोकानिमांस्त्वं व्याप्य तिष्ठसि ॥१०-१६॥

**Simple English**

You should indeed tell, without reserve, of Your divine glories by which You exist, pervading all these worlds. (None else can do so.).

### 10.17

**Sanskrit**

कथं विद्यामहं योगिंस्त्वां सदा परिचिन्तयन् ।
केषु केषु च भावेषु चिन्त्योऽसि भगवन्मया ॥१०-१७॥

**Simple English**

How shall I, ever meditating, know You, O Yogi? In what aspects or things, O blessed Lord, are You to be thought of by me?

### 10.18

**Sanskrit**

विस्तरेणात्मनो योगं विभूतिं च जनार्दन ।
भूयः कथय तृप्तिर्हि शृण्वतो नास्ति मेऽमृतम् ॥१०-१८॥

**Simple English**

Tell me again in detail, O Krishna, of Your Yogic power and glory; for I am not satiated with what I have heard of Your life-giving and nectar-like speech.

### 10.19

**Sanskrit**

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
हन्त ते कथयिष्यामि दिव्या ह्यात्मविभूतयः ।
प्राधान्यतः कुरुश्रेष्ठ नास्त्यन्तो विस्तरस्य मे ॥१०-१९॥

**Simple English**

Sri Krishna said: Very well! Now I will declare to you My divine glories in their prominence, O Arjuna; there is no end to their detailed description.

### 10.20

**Sanskrit**

अहमात्मा गुडाकेश सर्वभूताशयस्थितः ।
अहमादिश्च मध्यं च भूतानामन्त एव च ॥१०-२०॥

**Simple English**

I am the Self, O Gudakesa, seated in the hearts of all beings; I am the beginning, the middle and also the end of all beings.

### 10.21

**Sanskrit**

आदित्यानामहं विष्णुर्ज्योतिषां रविरंशुमान् ।
मरीचिर्मरुतामस्मि नक्षत्राणामहं शशी ॥१०-२१॥

**Simple English**

Among the (twelve) Adityas, I am Vishnu; among luminaries, the radiant sun; I am Marichi among the (seven or forty-nine) Maruts; among stars the moon am I.

### 10.22

**Sanskrit**

वेदानां सामवेदोऽस्मि देवानामस्मि वासवः ।
इन्द्रियाणां मनश्चास्मि भूतानामस्मि चेतना ॥१०-२२॥

**Simple English**

Among the Vedas I am the Sama-Veda; I am Vasava among the gods; among the senses I am the mind; and I am intelligence among living beings.

### 10.23

**Sanskrit**

रुद्राणां शङ्करश्चास्मि वित्तेशो यक्षरक्षसाम् ।
वसूनां पावकश्चास्मि मेरुः शिखरिणामहम् ॥१०-२३॥

**Simple English**

And, among the Rudras I am Sankara; among the Yakshas and Rakshasas, the Lord of wealth (Kubera); among the Vasus I am Pavaka (fire); and among the (seven) mountains I am the Meru.

### 10.24

**Sanskrit**

पुरोधसां च मुख्यं मां विद्धि पार्थ बृहस्पतिम् ।
सेनानीनामहं स्कन्दः सरसामस्मि सागरः ॥१०-२४॥

**Simple English**

And, among the household priests (of kings), O Arjuna, know Me to be the chief, Brihaspati; among the army generals I am Skana; among lakes I am the ocean. '.

### 10.25

**Sanskrit**

महर्षीणां भृगुरहं गिरामस्म्येकमक्षरम् ।
यज्ञानां जपयज्ञोऽस्मि स्थावराणां हिमालयः ॥१०-२५॥

**Simple English**

Among the great sages I am Bhrigu; among words I am the one syllable (Om); among sacrifices I am the sacrifice of silent repetition; among the immovable things I am the Himalayas.

### 10.26

**Sanskrit**

अश्वत्थः सर्ववृक्षाणां देवर्षीणां च नारदः ।
गन्धर्वाणां चित्ररथः सिद्धानां कपिलो मुनिः ॥१०-२६॥

**Simple English**

Among all the trees ( I am) the Peepul; among the divine sages, I am Narada; among Gandharvas, Chitraratha; among the perfected, the sage Kapila.

### 10.27

**Sanskrit**

उच्चैःश्रवसमश्वानां विद्धि माममृतोद्भवम् ।
ऐरावतं गजेन्द्राणां नराणां च नराधिपम् ॥१०-२७॥

**Simple English**

Know Me as Ucchaisravas born of nectar, among horses; among lordly elephants (I am) the Airavata; and, among men, the king.

### 10.28

**Sanskrit**

आयुधानामहं वज्रं धेनूनामस्मि कामधुक् ।
प्रजनश्चास्मि कन्दर्पः सर्पाणामस्मि वासुकिः ॥१०-२८॥

**Simple English**

Among weapons I am the thunderbolt; among cows I am the wish-fulfilling cow called Kamadhenu; I am the progenitor, the god of love; among serpents I am Vasuki.

### 10.29

**Sanskrit**

अनन्तश्चास्मि नागानां वरुणो यादसामहम् ।
पितॄणामर्यमा चास्मि यमः संयमतामहम् ॥१०-२९॥

**Simple English**

I am Ananta among the Nagas; I am Varuna among water-deities; Aryaman among the Manes I am; I am Yama among the governors.

### 10.30

**Sanskrit**

प्रह्लादश्चास्मि दैत्यानां कालः कलयतामहम् ।
मृगाणां च मृगेन्द्रोऽहं वैनतेयश्च पक्षिणाम् ॥१०-३०॥

**Simple English**

And, I am Prahlada among the demons, among the reckoners I am time; among beasts I am the lion, and Vainateya (Garuda) among birds.

### 10.31

**Sanskrit**

पवनः पवतामस्मि रामः शस्त्रभृतामहम् ।
झषाणां मकरश्चास्मि स्रोतसामस्मि जाह्नवी ॥१०-३१॥

**Simple English**

Among the purifiers (or the speeders) I am the wind; Rama among the warriors am I; among the fishes I am the shark; among the streams I am the Ganga.

### 10.32

**Sanskrit**

सर्गाणामादिरन्तश्च मध्यं चैवाहमर्जुन ।
अध्यात्मविद्या विद्यानां वादः प्रवदतामहम् ॥१०-३२॥

**Simple English**

Among creations I am the beginning, the middle and also the end, O Arjuna; among the sciences I am the science of the Self; and I am the logic among controversialists.

### 10.33

**Sanskrit**

अक्षराणामकारोऽस्मि द्वन्द्वः सामासिकस्य च ।
अहमेवाक्षयः कालो धाताहं विश्वतोमुखः ॥१०-३३॥

**Simple English**

Among the letters of the alphabets, the letter 'A' I am and the dual among the compounds. I am truly the inexhaustible or everlasting time; I am the dispenser (of the fruits of actions) having faces in all directions.

### 10.34

**Sanskrit**

मृत्युः सर्वहरश्चाहमुद्भवश्च भविष्यताम् ।
कीर्तिः श्रीर्वाक्च नारीणां स्मृतिर्मेधा धृतिः क्षमा ॥१०-३४॥

**Simple English**

And I am the all-devouring Death, and the prosperity of those who are to be prosperous; among the feminine qualities (I am) fame, prosperity, speech, memory, intelligence, firmness and forgiveness.

### 10.35

**Sanskrit**

बृहत्साम तथा साम्नां गायत्री छन्दसामहम् ।
मासानां मार्गशीर्षोऽहमृतूनां कुसुमाकरः ॥१०-३५॥

**Simple English**

Among the hymns also I am the Brihatsaman; among metres Gayatri am I; among the montsh I am the Margasirsha; among the seasons (I am) the flowery season.

### 10.36

**Sanskrit**

द्यूतं छलयतामस्मि तेजस्तेजस्विनामहम् ।
जयोऽस्मि व्यवसायोऽस्मि सत्त्वं सत्त्ववतामहम् ॥१०-३६॥

**Simple English**

I am the gambling of the fraudulent; I am the splendour of the splendid; I am victory; I am determination (of those who are determined); I am the goodness of the good.

### 10.37

**Sanskrit**

वृष्णीनां वासुदेवोऽस्मि पाण्डवानां धनञ्जयः ।
मुनीनामप्यहं व्यासः कवीनामुशना कविः ॥१०-३७॥

**Simple English**

Among the Vrishnis I am Vaasudeva; among the Pandavas I am Arjuna; among the sages I am Vyasa; among the Poets I am Usanas, the poet.

### 10.38

**Sanskrit**

दण्डो दमयतामस्मि नीतिरस्मि जिगीषताम् ।
मौनं चैवास्मि गुह्यानां ज्ञानं ज्ञानवतामहम् ॥१०-३८॥

**Simple English**

Of those who punish, I am the sceptre; among those who seek victory, I am statesmanship; and also among secrets, I am silence; knowledge among knowers I am.

### 10.39

**Sanskrit**

यच्चापि सर्वभूतानां बीजं तदहमर्जुन ।
न तदस्ति विना यत्स्यान्मया भूतं चराचरम् ॥१०-३९॥

**Simple English**

And whatever is the seed of all beings, that also am I, O Arjuna; there is no being, whether moving or unmoving, that can exist without Me.

### 10.40

**Sanskrit**

नान्तोऽस्ति मम दिव्यानां विभूतीनां परन्तप ।
एष तूद्देशतः प्रोक्तो विभूतेर्विस्तरो मया ॥१०-४०॥

**Simple English**

There is no end to My divine gloreis, O Arjuna, but this is a brief statement by Me of the particulars of My divine glories.

### 10.41

**Sanskrit**

यद्यद्विभूतिमत्सत्त्वं श्रीमदूर्जितमेव वा ।
तत्तदेवावगच्छ त्वं मम तेजोंऽशसम्भवम् ॥१०-४१॥

**Simple English**

Whatever being there is glorious, prosperous or powerful, that know you to be a manifestation of a part of My splendour.

### 10.42

**Sanskrit**

अथवा बहुनैतेन किं ज्ञातेन तवार्जुन ।
विष्टभ्याहमिदं कृत्स्नमेकांशेन स्थितो जगत् ॥१०-४२॥

**Simple English**

But, of what avail to you is the knowledge of all these details, O Arjuna? I exist, supporting this whole world by one part of Myself.

## Chapter 11 - Vishwaroopa Darshana Yoga

### 11.1

**Sanskrit**

अर्जुन उवाच ।
मदनुग्रहाय परमं गुह्यमध्यात्मसंज्ञितम् ।
यत्त्वयोक्तं वचस्तेन मोहोऽयं विगतो मम ॥११-१॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: By this word (explanation) of the highest secret concerning the Self which You have spoken, for the sake of blessing me, my confusion is gone.

### 11.2

**Sanskrit**

भवाप्ययौ हि भूतानां श्रुतौ विस्तरशो मया ।
त्वत्तः कमलपत्राक्ष माहात्म्यमपि चाव्ययम् ॥११-२॥

**Simple English**

The origin and the destruction of beings truly have been heard by me in detail from You, O lotus-eyed Lord, and also Your inexhaustible greatness.

### 11.3

**Sanskrit**

एवमेतद्यथात्थ त्वमात्मानं परमेश्वर ।
द्रष्टुमिच्छामि ते रूपमैश्वरं पुरुषोत्तम ॥११-३॥

**Simple English**

(Now) O Supreme Lord, as You have thus described You yourself, O Supreme Person, I wish to see Your divine form.

### 11.4

**Sanskrit**

मन्यसे यदि तच्छक्यं मया द्रष्टुमिति प्रभो ।
योगेश्वर ततो मे त्वं दर्शयात्मानमव्ययम् ॥११-४॥

**Simple English**

If You, O Lord, think it possible for me to see it, do You, then, O Lord of the Yogis, show me Your eternal Self.

### 11.5

**Sanskrit**

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
पश्य मे पार्थ रूपाणि शतशोऽथ सहस्रशः ।
नानाविधानि दिव्यानि नानावर्णाकृतीनि च ॥११-५॥

**Simple English**

Sri Krishna said: See, O Arjuna, forms of Mine, by the hundreds and thousands, of different sorts, divine, and of various colours and shapes.

### 11.6

**Sanskrit**

पश्यादित्यान्वसून्रुद्रानश्विनौ मरुतस्तथा ।
बहून्यदृष्टपूर्वाणि पश्याश्चर्याणि भारत ॥११-६॥

**Simple English**

See the Adityas, the Vasus, the Rudras, the two Asvins and also the Maruts; see many wonders never seen before, O Arjuna.

### 11.7

**Sanskrit**

इहैकस्थं जगत्कृत्स्नं पश्याद्य सचराचरम् ।
मम देहे गुडाकेश यच्चान्यद् द्रष्टुमिच्छसि ॥११-७॥

**Simple English**

Now see, O Arjuna, in this, My body, the whole universe centred in one including the moving and the unmoving and whatever else you desire to see.

### 11.8

**Sanskrit**

न तु मां शक्यसे द्रष्टुमनेनैव स्वचक्षुषा ।
दिव्यं ददामि ते चक्षुः पश्य मे योगमैश्वरम् ॥११-८॥

**Simple English**

But you are not able to see Me with these yours own eyes; I give you the divine eye; see My lordly Yoga.

### 11.9

**Sanskrit**

सञ्जय उवाच ।
एवमुक्त्वा ततो राजन्महायोगेश्वरो हरिः ।
दर्शयामास पार्थाय परमं रूपमैश्वरम् ॥११-९॥

**Simple English**

Sanjaya said: Having thus spoken, O king, the great Lord of Yoga, hari (Krishna), showed to Arjuna His supreme form as the Lord.

### 11.10

**Sanskrit**

अनेकवक्त्रनयनमनेकाद्भुतदर्शनम् ।
अनेकदिव्याभरणं दिव्यानेकोद्यतायुधम् ॥११-१०॥

**Simple English**

With numerous mouths and eyes, with numerous wonderful sights, with numerous divine ornaments, with numerous divine weapons uplifted (such a form He showed).

### 11.11

**Sanskrit**

दिव्यमाल्याम्बरधरं दिव्यगन्धानुलेपनम् ।
सर्वाश्चर्यमयं देवमनन्तं विश्वतोमुखम् ॥११-११॥

**Simple English**

Wearing divine garlands (necklaces) and apparel, anointed with divine unguents, the all-wonderful, resplendent (Being) endless with faces on all sides.

### 11.12

**Sanskrit**

दिवि सूर्यसहस्रस्य भवेद्युगपदुत्थिता ।
यदि भाः सदृशी सा स्याद्भासस्तस्य महात्मनः ॥११-१२॥

**Simple English**

If the splendour of a thousand suns were to blaze out at once (simultaneously) in the sky, that would be the splendour of that mighty Being (great Soul).

### 11.13

**Sanskrit**

तत्रैकस्थं जगत्कृत्स्नं प्रविभक्तमनेकधा ।
अपश्यद्देवदेवस्य शरीरे पाण्डवस्तदा ॥११-१३॥

**Simple English**

There, in the body of the God of gods, Arjuna then saw the whole universe resting in one, with its many groups.

### 11.14

**Sanskrit**

ततः स विस्मयाविष्टो हृष्टरोमा धनञ्जयः ।
प्रणम्य शिरसा देवं कृताञ्जलिरभाषत ॥११-१४॥

**Simple English**

Then, Arjuna, filled with wonder and with his hair standing on end, bowed down his head to the God and spoke with joined palms.

### 11.15

**Sanskrit**

अर्जुन उवाच ।
पश्यामि देवांस्तव देव देहे
सर्वांस्तथा भूतविशेषसङ्घान् ।
ब्रह्माणमीशं कमलासनस्थ-
मृषींश्च सर्वानुरगांश्च दिव्यान् ॥११-१५॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: I see all the gods, O God, in Your body, and (also) hosts of various classes of beings, Brahma, the Lord, seated on the lotus, all the sages and the celestial serpents.

### 11.16

**Sanskrit**

अनेकबाहूदरवक्त्रनेत्रं
पश्यामि त्वां सर्वतोऽनन्तरूपम् ।
नान्तं न मध्यं न पुनस्तवादिं
पश्यामि विश्वेश्वर विश्वरूप ॥११-१६॥

**Simple English**

I see You of boundless form on every side with many arms, stomachs, mouths and eyes: neither the end nor the middle nor also the beginning do I see, O Lord of the universe, O Cosmic Form.

### 11.17

**Sanskrit**

किरीटिनं गदिनं चक्रिणं च
तेजोराशिं सर्वतो दीप्तिमन्तम् ।
पश्यामि त्वां दुर्निरीक्ष्यं समन्ताद्
दीप्तानलार्कद्युतिमप्रमेयम् ॥११-१७॥

**Simple English**

I see You with the diadem, the club and the discus, a mass of radiance shining everywhere, very hard to look at, blazing all round like burning fire and the sun, and immeasurable.

### 11.18

**Sanskrit**

त्वमक्षरं परमं वेदितव्यं
त्वमस्य विश्वस्य परं निधानम् ।
त्वमव्ययः शाश्वतधर्मगोप्ता
सनातनस्त्वं पुरुषो मतो मे ॥११-१८॥

**Simple English**

You are the Imperishable, the Supreme Being, worthy to be known. You are the great treasure-house of this universe; You are the eternal protector of the eternal Dhrama; You are the Primal Person, I deem.

### 11.19

**Sanskrit**

अनादिमध्यान्तमनन्तवीर्य-
मनन्तबाहुं शशिसूर्यनेत्रम् ।
पश्यामि त्वां दीप्तहुताशवक्त्रं
स्वतेजसा विश्वमिदं तपन्तम् ॥११-१९॥

**Simple English**

I see You without beginning, middle or end, infinite in power, of endless arms, the sun and the moon being Your eyes, the burning fire Your mouth, heating the whole universe with Your radiance.

### 11.20

**Sanskrit**

द्यावापृथिव्योरिदमन्तरं हि
व्याप्तं त्वयैकेन दिशश्च सर्वाः ।
दृष्ट्वाद्भुतं रूपमुग्रं तवेदं
लोकत्रयं प्रव्यथितं महात्मन् ॥११-२०॥

**Simple English**

This space between the earth and the heaven and all the quarters are filled by You alone; having seen this, Your wonderful and terrible form, the three worlds are trembling with fear, O great-souled Being.

### 11.21

**Sanskrit**

अमी हि त्वां सुरसङ्घा विशन्ति
केचिद्भीताः प्राञ्जलयो गृणन्ति ।
स्वस्तीत्युक्त्वा महर्षिसिद्धसङ्घाः
स्तुवन्ति त्वां स्तुतिभिः पुष्कलाभिः ॥११-२१॥

**Simple English**

Truly, into You enter these hosts of gods; some extol You in fear with joined palms; saying 'may it be well', bands of great sages and perfected ones praise You with hymns complete.

### 11.22

**Sanskrit**

रुद्रादित्या वसवो ये च साध्या
विश्वेऽश्विनौ मरुतश्चोष्मपाश्च ।
गन्धर्वयक्षासुरसिद्धसङ्घा
वीक्षन्ते त्वां विस्मिताश्चैव सर्वे ॥११-२२॥

**Simple English**

The Rudras, Adityas, Vasus, Sadhyas, Visvedevas, the two Asvins, Maruts, the manes and the hosts of celestial singers, Yakshas, demons and the perfected ones, are all looking at You, in great amazement.

### 11.23

**Sanskrit**

रूपं महत्ते बहुवक्त्रनेत्रं
महाबाहो बहुबाहूरुपादम् ।
बहूदरं बहुदंष्ट्राकरालं
दृष्ट्वा लोकाः प्रव्यथितास्तथाहम् ॥११-२३॥

**Simple English**

Having seen Your immeasurable form with many mouths and eyes, O mighty-armed, with many arms, thighs and feet, with many stomachs and fearful with many teeth the worlds are terrified and so am I.

### 11.24

**Sanskrit**

नभःस्पृशं दीप्तमनेकवर्णं
व्यात्ताननं दीप्तविशालनेत्रम् ।
दृष्ट्वा हि त्वां प्रव्यथितान्तरात्मा
धृतिं न विन्दामि शमं च विष्णो ॥११-२४॥

**Simple English**

On seeing You (the Cosmic Form) touching the sky, shining in many colours, with mouths wide open, with large fiery eyes, I am terrified at heart and find neither courage nor peace, O Vishnu.

### 11.25

**Sanskrit**

दंष्ट्राकरालानि च ते मुखानि
दृष्ट्वैव कालानलसन्निभानि ।
दिशो न जाने न लभे च शर्म
प्रसीद देवेश जगन्निवास ॥११-२५॥

**Simple English**

Having seen Your mouths fearful with teeth (blazing) like the fires of cosmic dissolution, I know not the four quarters, nor do I find peace. Have mercy, O Lord of the gods, O abode of the universe.

### 11.26

**Sanskrit**

अमी च त्वां धृतराष्ट्रस्य पुत्राः
सर्वे सहैवावनिपालसङ्घैः ।
भीष्मो द्रोणः सूतपुत्रस्तथासौ
सहास्मदीयैरपि योधमुख्यैः ॥११-२६॥

**Simple English**

All the sons of Dhritarashtra, with the hosts of kings of the earth, Bhishma, Drona and Karna, with the chief among our warriors.

### 11.27

**Sanskrit**

वक्त्राणि ते त्वरमाणा विशन्ति
दंष्ट्राकरालानि भयानकानि ।
केचिद्विलग्ना दशनान्तरेषु
सन्दृश्यन्ते चूर्णितैरुत्तमाङ्गैः ॥११-२७॥

**Simple English**

Some hurriedly enter Your mouths with their terrible teeth, fearful to see. Some are found sticking in the gaps between the teeth with their heads crushed to powder.

### 11.28

**Sanskrit**

यथा नदीनां बहवोऽम्बुवेगाः
समुद्रमेवाभिमुखा द्रवन्ति ।
तथा तवामी नरलोकवीरा
विशन्ति वक्त्राण्यभिविज्वलन्ति ॥११-२८॥

**Simple English**

Truly, just as many torrents of rivers flow towards the ocean, even so these heroes in the world of men enter Your flaming mouths.

### 11.29

**Sanskrit**

यथा प्रदीप्तं ज्वलनं पतङ्गा
विशन्ति नाशाय समृद्धवेगाः ।
तथैव नाशाय विशन्ति लोकास्-
तवापि वक्त्राणि समृद्धवेगाः ॥११-२९॥

**Simple English**

As moths hurriedly rush into a blazing fire for (their own) destruction, so also these creatures hurriedly rush into Your mouths for (their own) destruction.

### 11.30

**Sanskrit**

लेलिह्यसे ग्रसमानः समन्ताल्-
लोकान्समग्रान्वदनैर्ज्वलद्भिः ।
तेजोभिरापूर्य जगत्समग्रं
भासस्तवोग्राः प्रतपन्ति विष्णो ॥११-३०॥

**Simple English**

You lick up, devouring all the worlds on every side with Your flaming mouths. Your fierce rays, filling the whole world with radiance, are burning, O Vishnu!

### 11.31

**Sanskrit**

आख्याहि मे को भवानुग्ररूपो
नमोऽस्तु ते देववर प्रसीद ।
विज्ञातुमिच्छामि भवन्तमाद्यं
न हि प्रजानामि तव प्रवृत्तिम् ॥११-३१॥

**Simple English**

Tell me, who You are, so fierce of form. Salutations to You, O God Supreme: have mercy. I desire to know You, the original Being. I know not indeed Your working.

### 11.32

**Sanskrit**

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
कालोऽस्मि लोकक्षयकृत्प्रवृद्धो
लोकान्समाहर्तुमिह प्रवृत्तः ।
ऋतेऽपि त्वां न भविष्यन्ति सर्वे
येऽवस्थिताः प्रत्यनीकेषु योधाः ॥११-३२॥

**Simple English**

Sri Krishna said: I am the full-grown world-destroying Time, now engaged in destroying the worlds. Even without you, none of the warriors arrayed in the hostile armies shall live.

### 11.33

**Sanskrit**

तस्मात्त्वमुत्तिष्ठ यशो लभस्व
जित्वा शत्रून् भुङ्क्ष्व राज्यं समृद्धम् ।
मयैवैते निहताः पूर्वमेव
निमित्तमात्रं भव सव्यसाचिन् ॥११-३३॥

**Simple English**

Therefore, stand up and obtain fame. Coner the enemies and enjoy the unrivalled kingdom. Truly by Me have they been already slain; be you a mere instrument, O Arjuna.

### 11.34

**Sanskrit**

द्रोणं च भीष्मं च जयद्रथं च
कर्णं तथान्यानपि योधवीरान् ।
मया हतांस्त्वं जहि मा व्यथिष्ठा
युध्यस्व जेतासि रणे सपत्नान् ॥११-३४॥

**Simple English**

Drona, Bhishma, Jayadratha, Karna and other brave warriors these are already slain by Me: do you kill; be not distressed with fear; fight and you will coner your enemies in battle.

### 11.35

**Sanskrit**

सञ्जय उवाच ।
एतच्छ्रुत्वा वचनं केशवस्य
कृताञ्जलिर्वेपमानः किरीटी ।
नमस्कृत्वा भूय एवाह कृष्णं
सगद्गदं भीतभीतः प्रणम्य ॥११-३५॥

**Simple English**

Sanjaya said: Having heard that speech of Lord Krishna, Arjuna, with joined palms, trembling, prostrating himself, again addressed Krishna, in a choked voice, bowing down, overwhelmed with fear.

### 11.36

**Sanskrit**

अर्जुन उवाच ।
स्थाने हृषीकेश तव प्रकीर्त्या
जगत्प्रहृष्यत्यनुरज्यते च ।
रक्षांसि भीतानि दिशो द्रवन्ति
सर्वे नमस्यन्ति च सिद्धसङ्घाः ॥११-३६॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: It is meet, O Krishna, that the world delights and rejoices in Your praise; demons fly in fear to all quarters and the hosts of the perfected ones bow to You.

### 11.37

**Sanskrit**

कस्माच्च ते न नमेरन्महात्मन्
गरीयसे ब्रह्मणोऽप्यादिकर्त्रे ।
अनन्त देवेश जगन्निवास
त्वमक्षरं सदसत्तत्परं यत् ॥११-३७॥

**Simple English**

And why should they not, O great Soul, bow to You Who are greater (than all else), the primal cause even of the Creator (Brahma), O Infinite Being, O Lord of the gods, O Abode of the universe; You are the eternal, the Being, the non-being and That which is the supreme (that which is beyond the Being and the non-being).

### 11.38

**Sanskrit**

त्वमादिदेवः पुरुषः पुराणस्-
त्वमस्य विश्वस्य परं निधानम् ।
वेत्तासि वेद्यं च परं च धाम
त्वया ततं विश्वमनन्तरूप ॥११-३८॥

**Simple English**

You are the primal God, the ancient the conscious Self, the supreme refuge of this universe, the knower, the knowable and the supreme Abode. By You is the universe pervaded, O Being of infinite forms.

### 11.39

**Sanskrit**

वायुर्यमोऽग्निर्वरुणः शशाङ्कः
प्रजापतिस्त्वं प्रपितामहश्च ।
नमो नमस्तेऽस्तु सहस्रकृत्वः
पुनश्च भूयोऽपि नमो नमस्ते ॥११-३९॥

**Simple English**

You are Vayu, Yama, Agni, Varuna, the moon, the Creator, and the great-grandfather. Salutations, salutations to You, a thousand times, and again salutations, salutations to You.

### 11.40

**Sanskrit**

नमः पुरस्तादथ पृष्ठतस्ते
नमोऽस्तु ते सर्वत एव सर्व ।
अनन्तवीर्यामितविक्रमस्त्वं
सर्वं समाप्नोषि ततोऽसि सर्वः ॥११-४०॥

**Simple English**

Salutations to You, in front and behind! Salutations to You on every side! O All!! You infinite in power and prowess, pervade all; therefore You are all.

### 11.41

**Sanskrit**

सखेति मत्वा प्रसभं यदुक्तं
हे कृष्ण हे यादव हे सखेति ।
अजानता महिमानं तवेदं
मया प्रमादात्प्रणयेन वापि ॥११-४१॥

**Simple English**

Whatever I have presumptuously said from carelessness or love, addressing You as O Krishna! O Yadava! O Friend! regarding You merely as a friend, unknowing of this, Your greatness.

### 11.42

**Sanskrit**

यच्चावहासार्थमसत्कृतोऽसि
विहारशय्यासनभोजनेषु ।
एकोऽथवाप्यच्युत तत्समक्षं
तत्क्षामये त्वामहमप्रमेयम् ॥११-४२॥

**Simple English**

In whatever way I may have insulted You for the sake of fun, while at play, reposing, sitting or at meals, when alone (with You), O Krishna, or in company that I implore You, immeasurable one, to forgive.

### 11.43

**Sanskrit**

पितासि लोकस्य चराचरस्य
त्वमस्य पूज्यश्च गुरुर्गरीयान् ।
न त्वत्समोऽस्त्यभ्यधिकः कुतोऽन्यो
लोकत्रयेऽप्यप्रतिमप्रभाव ॥११-४३॥

**Simple English**

You are the Father of this world, moving and unmoving. You are to be adored by this world, You, the greatest Guru; (for) none there exists who is equal to You; how then could there be another superior to You in the three worlds, O Being of unequalled power?

### 11.44

**Sanskrit**

तस्मात्प्रणम्य प्रणिधाय कायं
प्रसादये त्वामहमीशमीड्यम् ।
पितेव पुत्रस्य सखेव सख्युः
प्रियः प्रियायार्हसि देव सोढुम् ॥११-४४॥

**Simple English**

Therefore, bowing down, prostrating my body, I crave Your forgiveness, O adorable Lord. As a father forgives his son, a friend his (dear) friend, a lover his beloved, even so should You forgive me, O God.

### 11.45

**Sanskrit**

अदृष्टपूर्वं हृषितोऽस्मि दृष्ट्वा
भयेन च प्रव्यथितं मनो मे ।
तदेव मे दर्शय देव रूपं
प्रसीद देवेश जगन्निवास ॥११-४५॥

**Simple English**

I am delighted, having seen what has never been seen before; and yet my mind is distressed with fear. Show me that (previous) form only, O God; have mercy, O God of gods, O Abode of the universe.

### 11.46

**Sanskrit**

किरीटिनं गदिनं चक्रहस्तं
इच्छामि त्वां द्रष्टुमहं तथैव ।
तेनैव रूपेण चतुर्भुजेन
सहस्रबाहो भव विश्वमूर्ते ॥११-४६॥

**Simple English**

I desire to see You as before, crowned, bearing a mace, with the discus in hand, in Your former form only, having four arms, O thousand-armed, Cosmic Form (Being).

### 11.47

**Sanskrit**

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
मया प्रसन्नेन तवार्जुनेदं
रूपं परं दर्शितमात्मयोगात् ।
तेजोमयं विश्वमनन्तमाद्यं
यन्मे त्वदन्येन न दृष्टपूर्वम् ॥११-४७॥

**Simple English**

Sri Krishna said: O Arjuna, this Cosmic Form has graciously been shown to you by Me by My own Yogic power; full of splendour, primeval, and infinite, this Cosmic Form of Mine has never been seen before by anyone other than you yourself.

### 11.48

**Sanskrit**

न वेदयज्ञाध्ययनैर्न दानैर्-
न च क्रियाभिर्न तपोभिरुग्रैः ।
एवंरूपः शक्य अहं नृलोके
द्रष्टुं त्वदन्येन कुरुप्रवीर ॥११-४८॥

**Simple English**

Neither by the study of the Vedas and sacrifices, nor by gifts nor by rituals nor by severe self-discipline can I be seen in this form in the world of men by any other than you yourself, O great hero of the Kurus (Arjuna).

### 11.49

**Sanskrit**

मा ते व्यथा मा च विमूढभावो
दृष्ट्वा रूपं घोरमीदृङ्ममेदम् ।
व्यपेतभीः प्रीतमनाः पुनस्त्वं
तदेव मे रूपमिदं प्रपश्य ॥११-४९॥

**Simple English**

Be not afraid, nor bewildered on seeing such a terrible form of Mine as this; with your fear dispelled and with a gladdened heart, now see again this former form of Mine.

### 11.50

**Sanskrit**

सञ्जय उवाच ।
इत्यर्जुनं वासुदेवस्तथोक्त्वा
स्वकं रूपं दर्शयामास भूयः ।
आश्वासयामास च भीतमेनं
भूत्वा पुनः सौम्यवपुर्महात्मा ॥११-५०॥

**Simple English**

Sanjaya said: Having thus spoken to Arjuna, Krishna again showed His own form and the great Soul (Krishna), assuming His gentle form, consoled him (Arjuna) who was terrified.

### 11.51

**Sanskrit**

अर्जुन उवाच ।
दृष्ट्वेदं मानुषं रूपं तव सौम्यं जनार्दन ।
इदानीमस्मि संवृत्तः सचेताः प्रकृतिं गतः ॥११-५१॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: Having seen this Your gentle human form, O Krishna, now I am composed and am restored to my own nature.

### 11.52

**Sanskrit**

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
सुदुर्दर्शमिदं रूपं दृष्टवानसि यन्मम ।
देवा अप्यस्य रूपस्य नित्यं दर्शनकाङ्क्षिणः ॥११-५२॥

**Simple English**

Sri Krishna said: Very hard indeed it is to see this form of Mine which you have seen. Even the gods are ever longing to see it.

### 11.53

**Sanskrit**

नाहं वेदैर्न तपसा न दानेन न चेज्यया ।
शक्य एवंविधो द्रष्टुं दृष्टवानसि मां यथा ॥११-५३॥

**Simple English**

Neither by the Vedas nor by self-discipline, nor by gift, nor by sacrifice can I be seen in this form as you have seen Me (so easily).

### 11.54

**Sanskrit**

भक्त्या त्वनन्यया शक्य अहमेवंविधोऽर्जुन ।
ज्ञातुं द्रष्टुं च तत्त्वेन प्रवेष्टुं च परन्तप ॥११-५४॥

**Simple English**

But by single-minded devotion can I, of this Form, be known and seen in reality and also entered into, O Arjuna.

### 11.55

**Sanskrit**

मत्कर्मकृन्मत्परमो मद्भक्तः सङ्गवर्जितः ।
निर्वैरः सर्वभूतेषु यः स मामेति पाण्डव ॥११-५५॥

**Simple English**

He who does all actions for Me, who looks upon Me as the Supreme, who is devoted to Me, who is free from attachment, who bears enmity towards no creature, he comes to Me, O Arjuna.

## Chapter 12 - Bhakti Yoga

### 12.1

**Sanskrit**

अर्जुन उवाच ।
एवं सततयुक्ता ये भक्तास्त्वां पर्युपासते ।
ये चाप्यक्षरमव्यक्तं तेषां के योगवित्तमाः ॥१२-१॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: Those devotees who, ever steady, thus worship You and those also who worship the eternal and the unseen which of them are better versed in Yoga?

### 12.2

**Sanskrit**

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
मय्यावेश्य मनो ये मां नित्ययुक्ता उपासते ।
श्रद्धया परयोपेताः ते मे युक्ततमा मताः ॥१२-२॥

**Simple English**

Sri Krishna said: Those who, fixing their mind on Me, worship Me, ever steady and endowed with supreme faith, are the best in Yoga in My opinion.

### 12.3

**Sanskrit**

ये त्वक्षरमनिर्देश्यमव्यक्तं पर्युपासते ।
सर्वत्रगमचिन्त्यञ्च कूटस्थमचलन्ध्रुवम् ॥१२-३॥

**Simple English**

Swami Sivananda did not comment on this sloka.

### 12.4

**Sanskrit**

सन्नियम्येन्द्रियग्रामं सर्वत्र समबुद्धयः ।
ते प्राप्नुवन्ति मामेव सर्वभूतहिते रताः ॥१२-४॥

**Simple English**

Having restrained all the senses, even-minded everywhere, intent on the welfare of all beings truly they also come to Me.

### 12.5

**Sanskrit**

क्लेशोऽधिकतरस्तेषामव्यक्तासक्तचेतसाम् ।
अव्यक्ता हि गतिर्दुःखं देहवद्भिरवाप्यते ॥१२-५॥

**Simple English**

Greater is their trouble whose minds are set on the unseen; for the goal; the unseen, is very hard for the embodied to reach.

### 12.6

**Sanskrit**

ये तु सर्वाणि कर्माणि मयि संन्यस्य मत्परः ।
अनन्येनैव योगेन मां ध्यायन्त उपासते ॥१२-६॥

**Simple English**

But to those who worship Me, renouncing all actions in Me, regarding Me as the supreme goal, meditating on Me with single-minded Yoga.

### 12.7

**Sanskrit**

तेषामहं समुद्धर्ता मृत्युसंसारसागरात् ।
भवामि नचिरात्पार्थ मय्यावेशितचेतसाम् ॥१२-७॥

**Simple English**

To those whose minds are set on Me, O Arjuna, truly I become ere long the saviour out of the ocean of worldly cycle.

### 12.8

**Sanskrit**

मय्येव मन आधत्स्व मयि बुद्धिं निवेशय ।
निवसिष्यसि मय्येव अत ऊर्ध्वं न संशयः ॥१२-८॥

**Simple English**

Fix your mind in Me only, your intellect in Me, (then) you will no doubt live in Me alone hereafter.

### 12.9

**Sanskrit**

अथ चित्तं समाधातुं न शक्नोषि मयि स्थिरम् ।
अभ्यासयोगेन ततो मामिच्छाप्तुं धनञ्जय ॥१२-९॥

**Simple English**

If you are unable to fix your mind with steady focus on Me, then by the Yoga of constant practice do you seek to reach Me, O Arjuna.

### 12.10

**Sanskrit**

अभ्यासेऽप्यसमर्थोऽसि मत्कर्मपरमो भव ।
मदर्थमपि कर्माणि कुर्वन्सिद्धिमवाप्स्यसि ॥१२-१०॥

**Simple English**

If you are unable to practise even this Abhyasa Yoga, be you intent on doing actions for My sake; even by doing actions for My sake, you will attain perfection.

### 12.11

**Sanskrit**

अथैतदप्यशक्तोऽसि कर्तुं मद्योगमाश्रितः ।
सर्वकर्मफलत्यागं ततः कुरु यतात्मवान् ॥१२-११॥

**Simple English**

If you are unable to do even this, then, resorting to union with Me, renounce the fruits of all actions with the self controlled.

### 12.12

**Sanskrit**

श्रेयो हि ज्ञानमभ्यासाज्ज्ञानाद्ध्यानं विशिष्यते ।
ध्यानात्कर्मफलत्यागस्त्यागाच्छान्तिरनन्तरम् ॥१२-१२॥

**Simple English**

Better indeed is knowledge than practice; than knowledge meditation is better; than meditation the renunciation of the fruits of actions: peace immediately follows renunciation.

### 12.13

**Sanskrit**

अद्वेष्टा सर्वभूतानां मैत्रः करुण एव च ।
निर्ममो निरहङ्कारः समदुःखसुखः क्षमी ॥१२-१३॥

**Simple English**

A devotee who hates no being, is friendly and compassionate, free from possessiveness and ego, steady in joy and sorrow, and forgiving, is dear to me.

### 12.14

**Sanskrit**

सन्तुष्टः सततं योगी यतात्मा दृढनिश्चयः ।
मय्यर्पितमनोबुद्धिर्यो मद्भक्तः स मे प्रियः ॥१२-१४॥

**Simple English**

Ever content, steady in meditation, self-controlled, possessed of firm conviction, with the mind and intellect dedicated to Me, he, My devotee, is dear to Me.

### 12.15

**Sanskrit**

यस्मान्नोद्विजते लोको लोकान्नोद्विजते च यः ।
हर्षामर्षभयोद्वेगैर्मुक्तो यः स च मे प्रियः ॥१२-१५॥

**Simple English**

He by whom the world is not agitated and who cannot be agitated by the world, and who is freed from joy, anger, fear and anxiety he is dear to Me.

### 12.16

**Sanskrit**

अनपेक्षः शुचिर्दक्ष उदासीनो गतव्यथः ।
सर्वारम्भपरित्यागी यो मद्भक्तः स मे प्रियः ॥१२-१६॥

**Simple English**

He who is free from wants, pure, expert, unconcerned, and free from pain, renouncing all undertakings or commencements he who is (thus) devoted to Me, is dear to Me.

### 12.17

**Sanskrit**

यो न हृष्यति न द्वेष्टि न शोचति न काङ्क्षति ।
शुभाशुभपरित्यागी भक्तिमान्यः स मे प्रियः ॥१२-१७॥

**Simple English**

He who neither rejoices, nor hates, nor grieves, nor desires, renouncing good and evil, and who is full of devotion, is dear to Me.

### 12.18

**Sanskrit**

समः शत्रौ च मित्रे च तथा मानापमानयोः ।
शीतोष्णसुखदुःखेषु समः सङ्गविवर्जितः ॥१२-१८॥

**Simple English**

Swami Sivananda did not comment on this sloka.

### 12.19

**Sanskrit**

तुल्यनिन्दास्तुतिर्मौनी सन्तुष्टो येन केनचित् ।
अनिकेतः स्थिरमतिर्भक्तिमान्मे प्रियो नरः ॥१२-१९॥

**Simple English**

He to whom censure and praise are equal, who is silent, content with anything, homeless, of a steady mind, and full of devotion that man is dear to Me.

### 12.20

**Sanskrit**

ये तु धर्म्यामृतमिदं यथोक्तं पर्युपासते ।
श्रद्दधाना मत्परमा भक्तास्तेऽतीव मे प्रियाः ॥१२-२०॥

**Simple English**

They truly who follow this immortal Dharma (law or doctrine) as described above, endowed with faith, regarding Me as their supreme goal, they, the devotees, are exceedingly dear to Me.

## Chapter 13 - Ksetra Ksetrajna Vibhaaga Yoga

### 13.1

**Sanskrit**

अर्जुन उवाच ।
प्रकृतिं पुरुषं चैव क्षेत्रं क्षेत्रज्ञमेव च ।
एतद्वेदितुमिच्छामि ज्ञानं ज्ञेयं च केशव ॥१३-१॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: Keshava, I want to understand prakriti and purusha, the field and the knower of the field, knowledge and what is to be known.

### 13.2

**Sanskrit**

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
इदं शरीरं कौन्तेय क्षेत्रमित्यभिधीयते ।
एतद्यो वेत्ति तं प्राहुः क्षेत्रज्ञ इति तद्विदः ॥१३-२॥

**Simple English**

Sri Krishna said: This body, O Arjuna, is called the field; he who knows it is called the knower of the field, by those who know of them.

### 13.3

**Sanskrit**

क्षेत्रज्ञं चापि मां विद्धि सर्वक्षेत्रेषु भारत ।
क्षेत्रक्षेत्रज्ञयोर्ज्ञानं यत्तज्ज्ञानं मतं मम ॥१३-३॥

**Simple English**

Do you also know Me as the knower of the field in all fields, O Arjuna. Knowledge of both the field and the knower of the field is considered by Me to be the knowledge.

### 13.4

**Sanskrit**

तत्क्षेत्रं यच्च यादृक्च यद्विकारि यतश्च यत् ।
स च यो यत्प्रभावश्च तत्समासेन मे शृणु ॥१३-४॥

**Simple English**

What the field is and of what nature, what are its modifications and from where it is and also who He is and what His powers are hear all that from Me in brief.

### 13.5

**Sanskrit**

ऋषिभिर्बहुधा गीतं छन्दोभिर्विविधैः पृथक् ।
ब्रह्मसूत्रपदैश्चैव हेतुमद्भिर्विनिश्चितैः ॥१३-५॥

**Simple English**

Sages have sung in many ways, in various distinctive chants and also in the suggestive words indicative of the Absolute, full of reasoning and decisive.

### 13.6

**Sanskrit**

महाभूतान्यहंकारो बुद्धिरव्यक्तमेव च ।
इन्द्रियाणि दशैकं च पञ्च चेन्द्रियगोचराः ॥१३-६॥

**Simple English**

The great elements, ego, intellect, and also the Unseen Nature, the ten senses and one (mind), and the five objects of the senses.

### 13.7

**Sanskrit**

इच्छा द्वेषः सुखं दुःखं संघातश्चेतना धृतिः ।
एतत्क्षेत्रं समासेन सविकारमुदाहृतम् ॥१३-७॥

**Simple English**

Desire, hatred, pleasure, pain, the aggregate (the body), intelligence, fortitude the field has thus been briefly described with its modifications.

### 13.8

**Sanskrit**

अमानित्वमदम्भित्वमहिंसा क्षान्तिरार्जवम् ।
आचार्योपासनं शौचं स्थैर्यमात्मविनिग्रहः ॥१३-८॥

**Simple English**

Humility, unpretentiousness, non-injury, forgiveness, uprightness, service of the teacher, purity, steadfastness, self-control.

### 13.9

**Sanskrit**

इन्द्रियार्थेषु वैराग्यमनहंकार एव च ।
जन्ममृत्युजराव्याधिदुःखदोषानुदर्शनम् ॥१३-९॥

**Simple English**

Indifference to the objects of the senses and also absence of ego; perception of (or reflection on) the evil in birth, death, old age, sickness and pain.

### 13.10

**Sanskrit**

असक्तिरनभिष्वङ्गः पुत्रदारगृहादिषु ।
नित्यं च समचित्तत्वमिष्टानिष्टोपपत्तिषु ॥१३-१०॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: Keshava, I want to understand prakriti and purusha, the field and the knower of the field, knowledge and what is to be known.

### 13.11

**Sanskrit**

मयि चानन्ययोगेन भक्तिरव्यभिचारिणी ।
विविक्तदेशसेवित्वमरतिर्जनसंसदि ॥१३-११॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: Keshava, I want to understand prakriti and purusha, the field and the knower of the field, knowledge and what is to be known.

### 13.12

**Sanskrit**

अध्यात्मज्ञाननित्यत्वं तत्त्वज्ञानार्थदर्शनम् ।
एतज्ज्ञानमिति प्रोक्तमज्ञानं यदतोऽन्यथा ॥१३-१२॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: Keshava, I want to understand prakriti and purusha, the field and the knower of the field, knowledge and what is to be known.

### 13.13

**Sanskrit**

ज्ञेयं यत्तत्प्रवक्ष्यामि यज्ज्ञात्वामृतमश्नुते ।
अनादिमत्परं ब्रह्म न सत्तन्नासदुच्यते ॥१३-१३॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: Keshava, I want to understand prakriti and purusha, the field and the knower of the field, knowledge and what is to be known.

### 13.14

**Sanskrit**

सर्वतः पाणिपादं तत्सर्वतोऽक्षिशिरोमुखम् ।
सर्वतः श्रुतिमल्लोके सर्वमावृत्य तिष्ठति ॥१३-१४॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: Keshava, I want to understand prakriti and purusha, the field and the knower of the field, knowledge and what is to be known.

### 13.15

**Sanskrit**

सर्वेन्द्रियगुणाभासं सर्वेन्द्रियविवर्जितम् ।
असक्तं सर्वभृच्चैव निर्गुणं गुणभोक्तृ च ॥१३-१५॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: Keshava, I want to understand prakriti and purusha, the field and the knower of the field, knowledge and what is to be known.

### 13.16

**Sanskrit**

बहिरन्तश्च भूतानामचरं चरमेव च ।
सूक्ष्मत्वात्तदविज्ञेयं दूरस्थं चान्तिके च तत् ॥१३-१६॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: Keshava, I want to understand prakriti and purusha, the field and the knower of the field, knowledge and what is to be known.

### 13.17

**Sanskrit**

अविभक्तं च भूतेषु विभक्तमिव च स्थितम् ।
भूतभर्तृ च तज्ज्ञेयं ग्रसिष्णु प्रभविष्णु च ॥१३-१७॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: Keshava, I want to understand prakriti and purusha, the field and the knower of the field, knowledge and what is to be known.

### 13.18

**Sanskrit**

ज्योतिषामपि तज्ज्योतिस्तमसः परमुच्यते ।
ज्ञानं ज्ञेयं ज्ञानगम्यं हृदि सर्वस्य विष्ठितम् ॥१३-१८॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: Keshava, I want to understand prakriti and purusha, the field and the knower of the field, knowledge and what is to be known.

### 13.19

**Sanskrit**

इति क्षेत्रं तथा ज्ञानं ज्ञेयं चोक्तं समासतः ।
मद्भक्त एतद्विज्ञाय मद्भावायोपपद्यते ॥१३-१९॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: Keshava, I want to understand prakriti and purusha, the field and the knower of the field, knowledge and what is to be known.

### 13.20

**Sanskrit**

प्रकृतिं पुरुषं चैव विद्ध्यनादी उभावपि ।
विकारांश्च गुणांश्चैव विद्धि प्रकृतिसम्भवान् ॥१३-२०॥

**Simple English**

Know you that Nature (matter) and the Spirit are both beginningless; and know also that all modifications and qualities are born of Nature.

### 13.21

**Sanskrit**

कार्यकारणकर्तृत्वे हेतुः प्रकृतिरुच्यते ।
पुरुषः सुखदुःखानां भोक्तृत्वे हेतुरुच्यते ॥१३-२१॥

**Simple English**

In the production of the effect and the cause, Nature (matter) is said to be the cause; in the experience of pleasure and pain, the soul is said to be the cause.

### 13.22

**Sanskrit**

पुरुषः प्रकृतिस्थो हि भुङ्क्ते प्रकृतिजान्गुणान् ।
कारणं गुणसङ्गोऽस्य सदसद्योनिजन्मसु ॥१३-२२॥

**Simple English**

The soul seated in Nature experiences the qualities born of Nature; attachment to the qualities is the cause of its birth in good and evil wombs.

### 13.23

**Sanskrit**

उपद्रष्टानुमन्ता च भर्ता भोक्ता महेश्वरः ।
परमात्मेति चाप्युक्तो देहेऽस्मिन्पुरुषः परः ॥१३-२३॥

**Simple English**

The Supreme Soul in this body is also called the spectator, the permitter, the supporter, the enjoyer, the great Lord and the Supreme Self.

### 13.24

**Sanskrit**

य एवं वेत्ति पुरुषं प्रकृतिं च गुणैः सह ।
सर्वथा वर्तमानोऽपि न स भूयोऽभिजायते ॥१३-२४॥

**Simple English**

He who thus knows the Spirit and Matter together with the qualities, in whatever condition he may be, he is not born again.

### 13.25

**Sanskrit**

ध्यानेनात्मनि पश्यन्ति केचिदात्मानमात्मना ।
अन्ये साङ्ख्येन योगेन कर्मयोगेन चापरे ॥१३-२५॥

**Simple English**

Some by meditation see the Self in the self by the self, others by the Yoga of knowledge, and still others by the Yoga of action.

### 13.26

**Sanskrit**

अन्ये त्वेवमजानन्तः श्रुत्वान्येभ्य उपासते ।
तेऽपि चातितरन्त्येव मृत्युं श्रुतिपरायणाः ॥१३-२६॥

**Simple English**

Others also, not knowing thus, worship, having heard of It from others; they, too, cross beyond death, regarding what they have heard as the Supreme refuge.

### 13.27

**Sanskrit**

यावत्सञ्जायते किञ्चित्सत्त्वं स्थावरजङ्गमम् ।
क्षेत्रक्षेत्रज्ञसंयोगात्तद्विद्धि भरतर्षभ ॥१३-२७॥

**Simple English**

Wherever a being is born, whether unmoving or moving, know you, O best of the Bharatas (Arjuna), that it is from the union between the field and its knower.

### 13.28

**Sanskrit**

समं सर्वेषु भूतेषु तिष्ठन्तं परमेश्वरम् ।
विनश्यत्स्वविनश्यन्तं यः पश्यति स पश्यति ॥१३-२८॥

**Simple English**

He sees, who sees the Supreme Lord, existing equally in all beings, the unperishing within the perishing.

### 13.29

**Sanskrit**

समं पश्यन्हि सर्वत्र समवस्थितमीश्वरम् ।
न हिनस्त्यात्मनात्मानं ततो याति परां गतिम् ॥१३-२९॥

**Simple English**

Because he who sees the same Lord equally dwelling everywhere does not destroy the Self by the self; he goes to the highest goal.

### 13.30

**Sanskrit**

प्रकृत्यैव च कर्माणि क्रियमाणानि सर्वशः ।
यः पश्यति तथात्मानमकर्तारं स पश्यति ॥१३-३०॥

**Simple English**

He sees, who sees that all actions are performed by Nature alone and that the Self is actionless.

### 13.31

**Sanskrit**

यदा भूतपृथग्भावमेकस्थमनुपश्यति ।
तत एव च विस्तारं ब्रह्म सम्पद्यते तदा ॥१३-३१॥

**Simple English**

When a man sees the whole variety of beings as resting in the One, and spreading forth from That alone, he then becomes the Supreme Reality.

### 13.32

**Sanskrit**

अनादित्वान्निर्गुणत्वात्परमात्मायमव्ययः ।
शरीरस्थोऽपि कौन्तेय न करोति न लिप्यते ॥१३-३२॥

**Simple English**

Being without beginning and being devoid of (any) qualities, the Supreme Self, eternal, though dwelling in the body, O Arjuna, neither acts nor is tainted.

### 13.33

**Sanskrit**

यथा सर्वगतं सौक्ष्म्यादाकाशं नोपलिप्यते ।
सर्वत्रावस्थितो देहे तथात्मा नोपलिप्यते ॥१३-३३॥

**Simple English**

As the all-pervading ether is not tainted, because of its subtlety, so the Self seated everywhere in the body is not tainted.

### 13.34

**Sanskrit**

यथा प्रकाशयत्येकः कृत्स्नं लोकमिमं रविः ।
क्षेत्रं क्षेत्री तथा कृत्स्नं प्रकाशयति भारत ॥१३-३४॥

**Simple English**

Just as the one sun illumines the whole world, so also the Lord of the field (Supreme Self) illumines the whole field, O Arjuna.

### 13.35

**Sanskrit**

क्षेत्रक्षेत्रज्ञयोरेवमन्तरं ज्ञानचक्षुषा ।
भूतप्रकृतिमोक्षं च ये विदुर्यान्ति ते परम् ॥१३-३५॥

**Simple English**

They who, by the eye of knowledge, perceive the distinction between the field and its knower and also the freedom from the Nature of being, go to the Supreme.

## Chapter 14 - Gunatraya Vibhaga Yoga

### 14.1

**Sanskrit**

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
परं भूयः प्रवक्ष्यामि ज्ञानानां ज्ञानमुत्तमम् ।
यज्ज्ञात्वा मुनयः सर्वे परां सिद्धिमितो गताः ॥१४-१॥

**Simple English**

Sri Krishna said: I will again declare (to you) that supreme knowledge, the best of all knowledge, having known which all the sages have gone to the supreme perfection after this life.

### 14.2

**Sanskrit**

इदं ज्ञानमुपाश्रित्य मम साधर्म्यमागताः ।
सर्गेऽपि नोपजायन्ते प्रलये न व्यथन्ति च ॥१४-२॥

**Simple English**

They who, having taken refuge in this knowledge, have attained to unity with Me, are neither born at the time of creation nor are they disturbed at the time of dissolution.

### 14.3

**Sanskrit**

मम योनिर्महद् ब्रह्म तस्मिन्गर्भं दधाम्यहम् ।
सम्भवः सर्वभूतानां ततो भवति भारत ॥१४-३॥

**Simple English**

My womb is the great Brahma; in that I place the germ; thence, O Arjuna, is the birth of all beings.

### 14.4

**Sanskrit**

सर्वयोनिषु कौन्तेय मूर्तयः सम्भवन्ति याः ।
तासां ब्रह्म महद्योनिरहं बीजप्रदः पिता ॥१४-४॥

**Simple English**

Whatever forms are produced, O Arjuna, in any womb whatsoever, the great Brahma is their womb and I am the seed-giving father.

### 14.5

**Sanskrit**

सत्त्वं रजस्तम इति गुणाः प्रकृतिसम्भवाः ।
निबध्नन्ति महाबाहो देहे देहिनमव्ययम् ॥१४-५॥

**Simple English**

Clarity, passion, and darkness are the three qualities born from nature. They bind the eternal soul to the body.

### 14.6

**Sanskrit**

तत्र सत्त्वं निर्मलत्वात्प्रकाशकमनामयम् ।
सुखसङ्गेन बध्नाति ज्ञानसङ्गेन चानघ ॥१४-६॥

**Simple English**

Of these, clarity, which from its stainlessness is luminous and healthy, binds by attachment to happiness and by attachment to knowledge, O pure one.

### 14.7

**Sanskrit**

रजो रागात्मकं विद्धि तृष्णासङ्गसमुद्भवम् ।
तन्निबध्नाति कौन्तेय कर्मसङ्गेन देहिनम् ॥१४-७॥

**Simple English**

Know you passion to be of the nature of passion, the source of thirst (for sensual enjoyment) and attachment; it binds fast, O Arjuna, the embodied one by attachment to action.

### 14.8

**Sanskrit**

तमस्त्वज्ञानजं विद्धि मोहनं सर्वदेहिनाम् ।
प्रमादालस्यनिद्राभिस्तन्निबध्नाति भारत ॥१४-८॥

**Simple English**

But know you darkness to be born of ignorance, deluding all embodied beings; it binds fast, O Arjuna, by heedlessness, indolence and sleep.

### 14.9

**Sanskrit**

सत्त्वं सुखे सञ्जयति रजः कर्मणि भारत ।
ज्ञानमावृत्य तु तमः प्रमादे सञ्जयत्युत ॥१४-९॥

**Simple English**

clarity attaches to happiness, passion to action, O Arjuna, while darkness truly shrouding knowledge attaches to heedlessness.

### 14.10

**Sanskrit**

रजस्तमश्चाभिभूय सत्त्वं भवति भारत ।
रजः सत्त्वं तमश्चैव तमः सत्त्वं रजस्तथा ॥१४-१०॥

**Simple English**

Now clarity arises (prevails), O Arjuna, having overpowered passion and darkness; nor passion, having overpowered clarity and darkness; and now darkness, having overpowered clarity and passion.

### 14.11

**Sanskrit**

सर्वद्वारेषु देहेऽस्मिन्प्रकाश उपजायते ।
ज्ञानं यदा तदा विद्याद्विवृद्धं सत्त्वमित्युत ॥१४-११॥

**Simple English**

When through every gate (sense) in this body, the wisdom-light shines, then it may be known that clarity is predominant.

### 14.12

**Sanskrit**

लोभः प्रवृत्तिरारम्भः कर्मणामशमः स्पृहा ।
रजस्येतानि जायन्ते विवृद्धे भरतर्षभ ॥१४-१२॥

**Simple English**

Greed, activity, the undertaking of actions, restlessness, longing these arise when passion is predominant, O Arjuna.

### 14.13

**Sanskrit**

अप्रकाशोऽप्रवृत्तिश्च प्रमादो मोह एव च ।
तमस्येतानि जायन्ते विवृद्धे कुरुनन्दन ॥१४-१३॥

**Simple English**

Darkness, inertness, heedlessness and confusion these arise when darkness is predominant, O Arjuna.

### 14.14

**Sanskrit**

यदा सत्त्वे प्रवृद्धे तु प्रलयं याति देहभृत् ।
तदोत्तमविदां लोकानमलान्प्रतिपद्यते ॥१४-१४॥

**Simple English**

If the embodied one meets with death when clarity is predominant, then he attains to the spotless worlds of the knowers of the Highest.

### 14.15

**Sanskrit**

रजसि प्रलयं गत्वा कर्मसङ्गिषु जायते ।
तथा प्रलीनस्तमसि मूढयोनिषु जायते ॥१४-१५॥

**Simple English**

Meeting death in passion, he is born among those who are attached to action; and dying in darkness, he is born in the womb of the senseless.

### 14.16

**Sanskrit**

कर्मणः सुकृतस्याहुः सात्त्विकं निर्मलं फलम् ।
रजसस्तु फलं दुःखमज्ञानं तमसः फलम् ॥१४-१६॥

**Simple English**

The fruit of good action, they say, is pure and pure, truly the fruit of passion is pain, and ignorance is the fruit of darkness.

### 14.17

**Sanskrit**

सत्त्वात्सञ्जायते ज्ञानं रजसो लोभ एव च ।
प्रमादमोहौ तमसो भवतोऽज्ञानमेव च ॥१४-१७॥

**Simple English**

From clarity arises knowledge, and greed from passion; heedlessness and confusion arise from darkness, and also ignorance.

### 14.18

**Sanskrit**

ऊर्ध्वं गच्छन्ति सत्त्वस्था मध्ये तिष्ठन्ति राजसाः ।
जघन्यगुणवृत्तिस्था अधो गच्छन्ति तामसाः ॥१४-१८॥

**Simple English**

Those who are seated in clarity go upwards; the passionate dwell in the middle; and the dark, abiding in the function of the lowest Guna, go downwards.

### 14.19

**Sanskrit**

नान्यं गुणेभ्यः कर्तारं यदा द्रष्टानुपश्यति ।
गुणेभ्यश्च परं वेत्ति मद्भावं सोऽधिगच्छति ॥१४-१९॥

**Simple English**

When the seer beholds no agent other than the Gunas and knows That which is higher than they, he attains to My Being.

### 14.20

**Sanskrit**

गुणानेतानतीत्य त्रीन्देही देहसमुद्भवान् ।
जन्ममृत्युजरादुःखैर्विमुक्तोऽमृतमश्नुते ॥१४-२०॥

**Simple English**

The embodied one having crossed beyond these three Gunas out of which the body is evolved, is freed from birth, death, decay and pain, and attains to immortality.

### 14.21

**Sanskrit**

अर्जुन उवाच ।
कैर्लिङ्गैस्त्रीन्गुणानेतानतीतो भवति प्रभो ।
किमाचारः कथं चैतांस्त्रीन्गुणानतिवर्तते ॥१४-२१॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: What are the marks of him who has transcended the three qualities, O Lord? What is his conduct and how does he go beyond these three qualities?

### 14.22

**Sanskrit**

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
प्रकाशं च प्रवृत्तिं च मोहमेव च पाण्डव ।
न द्वेष्टि सम्प्रवृत्तानि न निवृत्तानि काङ्क्षति ॥१४-२२॥

**Simple English**

Sri Krishna said: When light, activity and confusion are present, he hates them not, nor does he long for them when they are absent.

### 14.23

**Sanskrit**

उदासीनवदासीनो गुणैर्यो न विचाल्यते ।
गुणा वर्तन्त इत्येवं योऽवतिष्ठति नेङ्गते ॥१४-२३॥

**Simple English**

He who, seated like one unconcerned, is not moved by the qualities, and who, knowing that the qualities are active, is self-centred and moves not.

### 14.24

**Sanskrit**

समदुःखसुखः स्वस्थः समलोष्टाश्मकाञ्चनः ।
तुल्यप्रियाप्रियो धीरस्तुल्यनिन्दात्मसंस्तुतिः ॥१४-२४॥

**Simple English**

Who is the same in pleasure and pain, who dwells in the Self, to whom a clod of earth, stone and gold are alike, who is the same to the dear and the unfriendly, who is firm, and to whom censure and praise are as one.

### 14.25

**Sanskrit**

मानापमानयोस्तुल्यस्तुल्यो मित्रारिपक्षयोः ।
सर्वारम्भपरित्यागी गुणातीतः स उच्यते ॥१४-२५॥

**Simple English**

Who is the same in honour and dishonour, the same to friend and foe, abandoning all undertakings he is said to have transcended the qualities.

### 14.26

**Sanskrit**

मां च योऽव्यभिचारेण भक्तियोगेन सेवते ।
स गुणान्समतीत्यैतान्ब्रह्मभूयाय कल्पते ॥१४-२६॥

**Simple English**

And he who serves Me with unswerving devotion, he, crossing beyond the qualities, is fit for becoming the Supreme Reality.

### 14.27

**Sanskrit**

ब्रह्मणो हि प्रतिष्ठाहममृतस्याव्ययस्य च ।
शाश्वतस्य च धर्मस्य सुखस्यैकान्तिकस्य च ॥१४-२७॥

**Simple English**

For I am the abode of the Supreme Reality, the immortal and the unchanging, of everlasting Dharma and of absolute bliss.

## Chapter 15 - Purushottama Yoga

### 15.1

**Sanskrit**

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
ऊर्ध्वमूलमधःशाखमश्वत्थं प्राहुरव्ययम् ।
छन्दांसि यस्य पर्णानि यस्तं वेद स वेदवित् ॥१५-१॥

**Simple English**

Sri Krishna said: They (the wise) speak of the indestructible peepul tree having its root above and branches below, whose leaves are the metres or hymns: he who knows it is a knower of the Vedas.

### 15.2

**Sanskrit**

अधश्चोर्ध्वं प्रसृतास्तस्य शाखा
गुणप्रवृद्धा विषयप्रवालाः ।
अधश्च मूलान्यनुसन्ततानि
कर्मानुबन्धीनि मनुष्यलोके ॥१५-२॥

**Simple English**

Below and above spread its branches, nourished by the Gunas; sense-objects are its buds; and below, in the world of men, stretch forth the roots, originating action.

### 15.3

**Sanskrit**

न रूपमस्येह तथोपलभ्यते
नान्तो न चादिर्न च सम्प्रतिष्ठा ।
अश्वत्थमेनं सुविरूढमूलं
असङ्गशस्त्रेण दृढेन छित्त्वा ॥१५-३॥

**Simple English**

Its form is not perceived here as such, neither its end nor its origin, nor its foundation nor resting place: having cut asunder this firmly rooted peepul tree with the strong axe of non-attachment.

### 15.4

**Sanskrit**

ततः पदं तत्परिमार्गितव्यं
यस्मिन्गता न निवर्तन्ति भूयः ।
तमेव चाद्यं पुरुषं प्रपद्ये ।
यतः प्रवृत्तिः प्रसृता पुराणी ॥१५-४॥

**Simple English**

Then That goal should be sought for, whither having gone none returns again. I seek refuge in that Primeval the conscious Self Where streamed forth the ancient activity or energy.

### 15.5

**Sanskrit**

निर्मानमोहा जितसङ्गदोषा
अध्यात्मनित्या विनिवृत्तकामाः ।
द्वन्द्वैर्विमुक्ताः सुखदुःखसंज्ञैर्-
गच्छन्त्यमूढाः पदमव्ययं तत् ॥१५-५॥

**Simple English**

Free from pride and confusion, victorious over the evil of attachment, dwelling constantly in the Self, their desires having completely turned away, freed from the pairs of opposites known as pleasure and pain, the undeluded reach the eternal goal.

### 15.6

**Sanskrit**

न तद्भासयते सूर्यो न शशाङ्को न पावकः ।
यद्गत्वा न निवर्तन्ते तद्धाम परमं मम ॥१५-६॥

**Simple English**

Neither doth the sun illumine there nor the moon, nor the fire; having gone thither they return not; that is My supreme abode.

### 15.7

**Sanskrit**

ममैवांशो जीवलोके जीवभूतः सनातनः ।
मनःषष्ठानीन्द्रियाणि प्रकृतिस्थानि कर्षति ॥१५-७॥

**Simple English**

An eternal portion of Myself having become a living soul in the world of life, draws to (itself) the (five) senses with the mind for the sixth, abiding in Nature.

### 15.8

**Sanskrit**

शरीरं यदवाप्नोति यच्चाप्युत्क्रामतीश्वरः ।
गृहीत्वैतानि संयाति वायुर्गन्धानिवाशयात् ॥१५-८॥

**Simple English**

When the Lord (as the individual soul) obtains a body and when He leaves it, He takes these and goes (with them) as the wind takes the scents from their seats (flowers, etc.).

### 15.9

**Sanskrit**

श्रोत्रं चक्षुः स्पर्शनं च रसनं घ्राणमेव च ।
अधिष्ठाय मनश्चायं विषयानुपसेवते ॥१५-९॥

**Simple English**

Presiding over the ear, the eye, touch, taste and smell, as well as the mind, it enjoys the objects of the senses.

### 15.10

**Sanskrit**

उत्क्रामन्तं स्थितं वापि भुञ्जानं वा गुणान्वितम् ।
विमूढा नानुपश्यन्ति पश्यन्ति ज्ञानचक्षुषः ॥१५-१०॥

**Simple English**

The confused do not see Him Who departs, stays and enjoys; but they who possess the eye of knowledge see Him.

### 15.11

**Sanskrit**

यतन्तो योगिनश्चैनं पश्यन्त्यात्मन्यवस्थितम् ।
यतन्तोऽप्यकृतात्मानो नैनं पश्यन्त्यचेतसः ॥१५-११॥

**Simple English**

The Yogis striving (for perfection) see Him dwelling in the Self; but, the unrefined and unintelligent, even though striving, see Him not.

### 15.12

**Sanskrit**

यदादित्यगतं तेजो जगद्भासयतेऽखिलम् ।
यच्चन्द्रमसि यच्चाग्नौ तत्तेजो विद्धि मामकम् ॥१५-१२॥

**Simple English**

That light which residing in the sun illumines the whole world, that which is in the moon and in the fire know that light to be Mine.

### 15.13

**Sanskrit**

गामाविश्य च भूतानि धारयाम्यहमोजसा ।
पुष्णामि चौषधीः सर्वाः सोमो भूत्वा रसात्मकः ॥१५-१३॥

**Simple English**

Permeating the earth I support all beings by (My) energy; and having become the watery moon I nourish all herbs.

### 15.14

**Sanskrit**

अहं वैश्वानरो भूत्वा प्राणिनां देहमाश्रितः ।
प्राणापानसमायुक्तः पचाम्यन्नं चतुर्विधम् ॥१५-१४॥

**Simple English**

Having become the fire Vaisvanara, I abide in the body of living beings and, associated with the Prana and the Apana, digest the fourfold food.

### 15.15

**Sanskrit**

सर्वस्य चाहं हृदि सन्निविष्टो
मत्तः स्मृतिर्ज्ञानमपोहनञ्च ।
वेदैश्च सर्वैरहमेव वेद्यो
वेदान्तकृद्वेदविदेव चाहम् ॥१५-१५॥

**Simple English**

And I am seated in the hearts of all; from Me are memory and knowledge, as well as their absence. I am truly That which has to be known by all the Vedas; I am indeed the author of the Vedanta and the knower of the Vedas am I.

### 15.16

**Sanskrit**

द्वाविमौ पुरुषौ लोके क्षरश्चाक्षर एव च ।
क्षरः सर्वाणि भूतानि कूटस्थोऽक्षर उच्यते ॥१५-१६॥

**Simple English**

Two Purushas there are in this world, the temporary and the eternal. All beings are the temporary and the Kutastha the unchanging is called the eternal.

### 15.17

**Sanskrit**

उत्तमः पुरुषस्त्वन्यः परमात्मेत्युधाहृतः ।
यो लोकत्रयमाविश्य बिभर्त्यव्यय ईश्वरः ॥१५-१७॥

**Simple English**

But distinct is the Supreme the conscious Self called the highest Self, the indestructible Lord Who, pervading the three worlds, sustains them.

### 15.18

**Sanskrit**

यस्मात्क्षरमतीतोऽहमक्षरादपि चोत्तमः ।
अतोऽस्मि लोके वेदे च प्रथितः पुरुषोत्तमः ॥१५-१८॥

**Simple English**

As I transcend the temporary and am even higher than the eternal, I am declared to be the highest the conscious Self in the world and in the Vedas.

### 15.19

**Sanskrit**

यो मामेवमसम्मूढो जानाति पुरुषोत्तमम् ।
स सर्वविद्भजति मां सर्वभावेन भारत ॥१५-१९॥

**Simple English**

He who, undeluded, knows Me thus as the highest the conscious Self, he, knowing all, worships Me with his whole being (heart), O Arjuna.

### 15.20

**Sanskrit**

इति गुह्यतमं शास्त्रमिदमुक्तं मयानघ ।
एतद्बुद्ध्वा बुद्धिमान्स्यात्कृतकृत्यश्च भारत ॥१५-२०॥

**Simple English**

Thus, this most secret science has been taught by Me, O pure one; on knowing this, a man becomes wise, and all his duties are accomplished, O Arjuna.

## Chapter 16 - Daivasura Sampad Vibhaga Yoga

### 16.1

**Sanskrit**

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
अभयं सत्त्वसंशुद्धिर्ज्ञानयोगव्यवस्थितिः ।
दानं दमश्च यज्ञश्च स्वाध्यायस्तप आर्जवम् ॥१६-१॥

**Simple English**

Sri Krishna said: Fearlessness, purity of heart, steadfastness in knowledge and Yoga, almsgiving, control of the senses, sacrifice, study of scriptures, self-discipline and straightforwardness.

### 16.2

**Sanskrit**

अहिंसा सत्यमक्रोधस्त्यागः शान्तिरपैशुनम् ।
दया भूतेष्वलोलुप्त्वं मार्दवं ह्रीरचापलम् ॥१६-२॥

**Simple English**

Harmlessness, truth, absence of anger, renunciation, peacefulness, absence of crookedness, compassion towards beings, non-greed, gentleness, modesty, absence of fickleness.

### 16.3

**Sanskrit**

तेजः क्षमा धृतिः शौचमद्रोहो नातिमानिता ।
भवन्ति सम्पदं दैवीमभिजातस्य भारत ॥१६-३॥

**Simple English**

Vigour, forgiveness, fortitude, purity, absence of hatred, absence of pride these belong to the one born for a divine state, O Arjuna.

### 16.4

**Sanskrit**

दम्भो दर्पोऽभिमानश्च क्रोधः पारुष्यमेव च ।
अज्ञानं चाभिजातस्य पार्थ सम्पदमासुरीम् ॥१६-४॥

**Simple English**

Hypocrisy, arrogance and self-conceit, anger and also harshness and ignorance, belong to one who is born for a demoniacal state, O Partha (Arjuna).

### 16.5

**Sanskrit**

दैवी सम्पद्विमोक्षाय निबन्धायासुरी मता ।
मा शुचः सम्पदं दैवीमभिजातोऽसि पाण्डव ॥१६-५॥

**Simple English**

The divine nature is deemed conducive to freedom, and the demoniacal to bondage. Grieve not, O Arjuna, you are born with divine endowments.

### 16.6

**Sanskrit**

द्वौ भूतसर्गौ लोकेऽस्मिन्दैव आसुर एव च ।
दैवो विस्तरशः प्रोक्त आसुरं पार्थ मे शृणु ॥१६-६॥

**Simple English**

There are two types of beings in this world, the divine and the demoniacal; the divine has been described at length; hear from Me, O Arjuna, of the demoniacal.

### 16.7

**Sanskrit**

प्रवृत्तिं च निवृत्तिं च जना न विदुरासुराः ।
न शौचं नापि चाचारो न सत्यं तेषु विद्यते ॥१६-७॥

**Simple English**

The demoniacal know not what to do and what to refrain from; neither purity, nor right conduct nor truth is found in them.

### 16.8

**Sanskrit**

असत्यमप्रतिष्ठं ते जगदाहुरनीश्वरम् ।
अपरस्परसम्भूतं किमन्यत्कामहैतुकम् ॥१६-८॥

**Simple English**

They say, "This universe is without truth, without (moral) basis, without a God, brought about by mutual union, with lust for its cause; what else?".

### 16.9

**Sanskrit**

एतां दृष्टिमवष्टभ्य नष्टात्मानोऽल्पबुद्धयः ।
प्रभवन्त्युग्रकर्माणः क्षयाय जगतोऽहिताः ॥१६-९॥

**Simple English**

Holding this view, these ruined souls of small intellect and fierce deeds, come forth as the enemies of the world for its destruction.

### 16.10

**Sanskrit**

काममाश्रित्य दुष्पूरं दम्भमानमदान्विताः ।
मोहाद्गृहीत्वासद्ग्राहान्प्रवर्तन्तेऽशुचिव्रताः ॥१६-१०॥

**Simple English**

Filled with insatiable desires, full of hypocrisy, pride and arrogance, holding evil ideas through confusion, they work with impure resolves.

### 16.11

**Sanskrit**

चिन्तामपरिमेयां च प्रलयान्तामुपाश्रिताः ।
कामोपभोगपरमा एतावदिति निश्चिताः ॥१६-११॥

**Simple English**

Giving themselves over to immeasurable cares ending only with death, regarding gratification of lust as their highest aim, and feeling sure that that is all.

### 16.12

**Sanskrit**

आशापाशशतैर्बद्धाः कामक्रोधपरायणाः ।
ईहन्ते कामभोगार्थमन्यायेनार्थसञ्चयान् ॥१६-१२॥

**Simple English**

Bound by a hundred ties of hope, given over to lust and anger, they strive to obtain by unlawful means hoards to wealth for sensual enjoyments.

### 16.13

**Sanskrit**

इदमद्य मया लब्धमिमं प्राप्स्ये मनोरथम् ।
इदमस्तीदमपि मे भविष्यति पुनर्धनम् ॥१६-१३॥

**Simple English**

"This has been gained by me today; this desire of mine I shall fuffil; this is mine and this wealth also shall be mine in future.".

### 16.14

**Sanskrit**

असौ मया हतः शत्रुर्हनिष्ये चापरानपि ।
ईश्वरोऽहमहं भोगी सिद्धोऽहं बलवान्सुखी ॥१६-१४॥

**Simple English**

"That enemy has been slain by me; and others also I shall kill. I am the lord. I enjoy. I am perfect, powerful and happy.".

### 16.15

**Sanskrit**

आढ्योऽभिजनवानस्मि कोऽन्योऽस्ति सदृशो मया ।
यक्ष्ये दास्यामि मोदिष्य इत्यज्ञानविमोहिताः ॥१६-१५॥

**Simple English**

"I am rich and born in a noble family. Who else is equal to me? I shall perform sacrifices. I shall give (charity). I shall rejoice," thus confused by ignorance.

### 16.16

**Sanskrit**

अनेकचित्तविभ्रान्ता मोहजालसमावृताः ।
प्रसक्ताः कामभोगेषु पतन्ति नरकेऽशुचौ ॥१६-१६॥

**Simple English**

Bewildered by many a fancy, entangled in the snare of confusion, addicted to the gratification of lust, they fall into a foul hell.

### 16.17

**Sanskrit**

आत्मसम्भाविताः स्तब्धा धनमानमदान्विताः ।
यजन्ते नामयज्ञैस्ते दम्भेनाविधिपूर्वकम् ॥१६-१७॥

**Simple English**

Self-conceited, stubborn, filled with the pride and intoxication of wealth, they perform sacrifices in name out of ostentation, contrary to scriptural ordinances.

### 16.18

**Sanskrit**

अहंकारं बलं दर्पं कामं क्रोधं च संश्रिताः ।
मामात्मपरदेहेषु प्रद्विषन्तोऽभ्यसूयकाः ॥१६-१८॥

**Simple English**

Given over to ego, power, pride, lust and anger, these malicious people hate Me in their own bodies and in those of others.

### 16.19

**Sanskrit**

तानहं द्विषतः क्रुरान्संसारेषु नराधमान् ।
क्षिपाम्यजस्रमशुभानासुरीष्वेव योनिषु ॥१६-१९॥

**Simple English**

Those cruel haters, worst among men in the world, I hurl those evil-doers into the wombs of demons only.

### 16.20

**Sanskrit**

आसुरीं योनिमापन्ना मूढा जन्मनि जन्मनि ।
मामप्राप्यैव कौन्तेय ततो यान्त्यधमां गतिम् ॥१६-२०॥

**Simple English**

Entering into demoniacal wombs and confused, birth after birth, not attaining Me, they thus fall, O Arjuna, into a condition still lower than that.

### 16.21

**Sanskrit**

त्रिविधं नरकस्येदं द्वारं नाशनमात्मनः ।
कामः क्रोधस्तथा लोभस्तस्मादेतत्त्रयं त्यजेत् ॥१६-२१॥

**Simple English**

Triple is the gate of this hell, destructive of the self lust, anger and greed; therefore one should abandon these three.

### 16.22

**Sanskrit**

एतैर्विमुक्तः कौन्तेय तमोद्वारैस्त्रिभिर्नरः ।
आचरत्यात्मनः श्रेयस्ततो याति परां गतिम् ॥१६-२२॥

**Simple English**

A man who is free from these three gates to darkness, O Arjuna, practises what is good for him and thus goes to the Supreme Goal.

### 16.23

**Sanskrit**

यः शास्त्रविधिमुत्सृज्य वर्तते कामकारतः ।
न स सिद्धिमवाप्नोति न सुखं न परां गतिम् ॥१६-२३॥

**Simple English**

He who, having cast aside the ordinances of the scriptures, acts under the impulse of desire, attains not perfection, nor happiness nor the Supreme Goal.

### 16.24

**Sanskrit**

तस्माच्छास्त्रं प्रमाणं ते कार्याकार्यव्यवस्थितौ ।
ज्ञात्वा शास्त्रविधानोक्तं कर्म कर्तुमिहार्हसि ॥१६-२४॥

**Simple English**

Therefore, let the scripture be your authority in determining what ought to be done and what ought not to be done. Having known what is said in the ordinance of the scriptures, you should act here in this world.

## Chapter 17 - Sraddhatraya Vibhaga Yoga

### 17.1

**Sanskrit**

अर्जुन उवाच ।
ये शास्त्रविधिमुत्सृज्य यजन्ते श्रद्धयान्विताः ।
तेषां निष्ठा तु का कृष्ण सत्त्वमाहो रजस्तमः ॥१७-१॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: Those who, setting aside the ordinances of the scriptures, perform sacrifice with faith, what is their condition, O Krishna? Is is clarity, passion or darkness?

### 17.2

**Sanskrit**

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
त्रिविधा भवति श्रद्धा देहिनां सा स्वभावजा ।
सात्त्विकी राजसी चैव तामसी चेति तां शृणु ॥१७-२॥

**Simple English**

Sri Krishna said: Threefold is the faith of the embodied, which is inherent in their nature the pure (pure), the passionate (passionate) and the dark (dark). Do you hear of it.

### 17.3

**Sanskrit**

सत्त्वानुरूपा सर्वस्य श्रद्धा भवति भारत ।
श्रद्धामयोऽयं पुरुषो यो यच्छ्रद्धः स एव सः ॥१७-३॥

**Simple English**

The faith of each is in accordance with his nature, O Arjuna. The man consists of his faith; as a man's faith is, so is he.

### 17.4

**Sanskrit**

यजन्ते सात्त्विका देवान्यक्षरक्षांसि राजसाः ।
प्रेतान्भूतगणांश्चान्ये यजन्ते तामसा जनाः ॥१७-४॥

**Simple English**

The pure or the pure men worship the gods; the passionate or the passionate worship the Yakshas and the Rakshasas; the others (the dark or the confused people) worship the ghosts and the hosts of the nature-spirits.

### 17.5

**Sanskrit**

अशास्त्रविहितं घोरं तप्यन्ते ये तपो जनाः ।
दम्भाहंकारसंयुक्ताः कामरागबलान्विताः ॥१७-५॥

**Simple English**

Those men who practise terrific self-discipline not enjoined by the scriptures, given to hypocrisy and ego, impelled by the force of lust and attachment.

### 17.6

**Sanskrit**

कर्षयन्तः शरीरस्थं भूतग्राममचेतसः ।
मां चैवान्तःशरीरस्थं तान्विद्ध्यासुरनिश्चयान् ॥१७-६॥

**Simple English**

Senseless, torturing all the elements in the body and Me also, Who dwell in the body, know you these to be of demonical resolves.

### 17.7

**Sanskrit**

आहारस्त्वपि सर्वस्य त्रिविधो भवति प्रियः ।
यज्ञस्तपस्तथा दानं तेषां भेदमिमं शृणु ॥१७-७॥

**Simple English**

The food also which is dear to each is threefold, as also sacrifice, self-discipline and almsgiving. Hear you the distinction of these.

### 17.8

**Sanskrit**

आयुःसत्त्वबलारोग्यसुखप्रीतिविवर्धनाः ।
रस्याः स्निग्धाः स्थिरा हृद्या आहाराः सात्त्विकप्रियाः ॥१७-८॥

**Simple English**

The foods which increase life, purity, strength, health, joy and cheerfulness (good appetite), which are savoury and oleaginous, substantial and agreeable, are dear to the pure (pure) people.

### 17.9

**Sanskrit**

कट्वम्ललवणात्युष्णतीक्ष्णरूक्षविदाहिनः ।
आहारा राजसस्येष्टा दुःखशोकामयप्रदाः ॥१७-९॥

**Simple English**

The foods that are bitter, sour, saline, excessively hot, pungent, dry and burning, are liked by the passionate and are productive of pain, grief and disease.

### 17.10

**Sanskrit**

यातयामं गतरसं पूति पर्युषितं च यत् ।
उच्छिष्टमपि चामेध्यं भोजनं तामसप्रियम् ॥१७-१०॥

**Simple English**

That which is state, tasteless, putrid, rotten, refuse and impure, is the food liked by the dark.

### 17.11

**Sanskrit**

अफलाङ्क्षिभिर्यज्ञो विधिदृष्टो य इज्यते ।
यष्टव्यमेवेति मनः समाधाय स सात्त्विकः ॥१७-११॥

**Simple English**

That sacrifice which is offered by men without desire for reward as enjoined by the ordinance (scripture), with a firm faith that to do so is a duty, is pure or pure.

### 17.12

**Sanskrit**

अभिसन्धाय तु फलं दम्भार्थमपि चैव यत् ।
इज्यते भरतश्रेष्ठ तं यज्ञं विद्धि राजसम् ॥१७-१२॥

**Simple English**

The sacrifice which is offered, O Arjuna, seeking a reward and for ostentation, know you that to be a passionate Yajna.

### 17.13

**Sanskrit**

विधिहीनमसृष्टान्नं मन्त्रहीनमदक्षिणम् ।
श्रद्धाविरहितं यज्ञं तामसं परिचक्षते ॥१७-१३॥

**Simple English**

They declare that sacrifice to be dark which is contrary to the ordinances of the scriptures, in which no food is distributed, which is devoid of Mantras, gifts and faith.

### 17.14

**Sanskrit**

देवद्विजगुरुप्राज्ञपूजनं शौचमार्जवम् ।
ब्रह्मचर्यमहिंसा च शारीरं तप उच्यते ॥१७-१४॥

**Simple English**

Worship of the gods, the twice-born, the teachers and the wise, purity, straightforwardness, celibacy and non-injury are called the self-discipline of the body.

### 17.15

**Sanskrit**

अनुद्वेगकरं वाक्यं सत्यं प्रियहितं च यत् ।
स्वाध्यायाभ्यसनं चैव वाङ्मयं तप उच्यते ॥१७-१५॥

**Simple English**

Speech which causes no excitement, truthful, pleasant and beneficial, the practice of the study of the Vedas, are called self-discipline of speech.

### 17.16

**Sanskrit**

मनः प्रसादः सौम्यत्वं मौनमात्मविनिग्रहः ।
भावसंशुद्धिरित्येतत्तपो मानसमुच्यते ॥१७-१६॥

**Simple English**

Serenity of mind, good-heartedness, self-control, purity of nature this is called mental self-discipline.

### 17.17

**Sanskrit**

श्रद्धया परया तप्तं तपस्तत्त्रिविधं नरैः ।
अफलाकाङ्क्षिभिर्युक्तैः सात्त्विकं परिचक्षते ॥१७-१७॥

**Simple English**

This threefold self-discipline, practised by steady men, with the utmost faith, desiring no reward, they call pure.

### 17.18

**Sanskrit**

सत्कारमानपूजार्थं तपो दम्भेन चैव यत् ।
क्रियते तदिह प्रोक्तं राजसं चलमध्रुवम् ॥१७-१८॥

**Simple English**

The self-discipline which is practised with the object of gaining good reception, honour and worship, and with hypocrisy, is here said to be passionate, unstable and transitory.

### 17.19

**Sanskrit**

मूढग्राहेणात्मनो यत्पीडया क्रियते तपः ।
परस्योत्सादनार्थं वा तत्तामसमुदाहृतम् ॥१७-१९॥

**Simple English**

That self-discipline which is practised out of a foolish notion, with self-torture, or for the purpose of destroying another, is declared to be dark.

### 17.20

**Sanskrit**

दातव्यमिति यद्दानं दीयतेऽनुपकारिणे ।
देशे काले च पात्रे च तद्दानं सात्त्विकं स्मृतम् ॥१७-२०॥

**Simple English**

That gift which is given to one who does nothing in return, knowing it to be a duty to give in a fit place and time to a worthy person, that gift is held to be pure.

### 17.21

**Sanskrit**

यत्तु प्रत्युपकारार्थं फलमुद्दिश्य वा पुनः ।
दीयते च परिक्लिष्टं तद्दानं राजसं स्मृतम् ॥१७-२१॥

**Simple English**

And, that gift which is given with a view to receive something in return, or looking for a reward, or reluctantly, is held to be passionate.

### 17.22

**Sanskrit**

अदेशकाले यद्दानमपात्रेभ्यश्च दीयते ।
असत्कृतमवज्ञातं तत्तामसमुदाहृतम् ॥१७-२२॥

**Simple English**

The gift that is given at a wrong place and time, to unworthy persons, without respect or with insult is declared to be dark.

### 17.23

**Sanskrit**

ॐतत्सदिति निर्देशो ब्रह्मणस्त्रिविधः स्मृतः ।
ब्राह्मणास्तेन वेदाश्च यज्ञाश्च विहिताः पुरा ॥१७-२३॥

**Simple English**

"Om Tat Sat": This has been declared to be the triple designation of the Supreme Reality. By that were created formerly, the Brahmanas, the Vedas and the sacrifices.

### 17.24

**Sanskrit**

तस्मादोमित्युदाहृत्य यज्ञदानतपःक्रियाः ।
प्रवर्तन्ते विधानोक्ताः सततं ब्रह्मवादिनाम् ॥१७-२४॥

**Simple English**

Therefore, with the utterance of "Om" are the acts of sacrifice, gift and self-discipline as enjoined in the scriptures, always begun by the students of the Supreme Reality.

### 17.25

**Sanskrit**

तदित्यनभिसन्धाय फलं यज्ञतपःक्रियाः ।
दानक्रियाश्च विविधाः क्रियन्ते मोक्षकाङ्क्षिभिः ॥१७-२५॥

**Simple English**

Uttering Tat, without aiming at the fruits, are the acts of sacrifice and self-discipline and the various acts of gifts performed by the seekers of freedom.

### 17.26

**Sanskrit**

सद्भावे साधुभावे च सदित्येतत्प्रयुज्यते ।
प्रशस्ते कर्मणि तथा सच्छब्दः पार्थ युज्यते ॥१७-२६॥

**Simple English**

The word Sat is used in the sense of reality and of goodness; and so also, O Arjuna, the word Sat is used in the sense of an auspicious act.

### 17.27

**Sanskrit**

यज्ञे तपसि दाने च स्थितिः सदिति चोच्यते ।
कर्म चैव तदर्थीयं सदित्येवाभिधीयते ॥१७-२७॥

**Simple English**

Steadfastness in sacrifice, self-discipline and gift, is also called 'Sat' and also action in connection with these (or for the sake of the Supreme) is called 'Sat'.

### 17.28

**Sanskrit**

अश्रद्धया हुतं दत्तं तपस्तप्तं कृतं च यत् ।
असदित्युच्यते पार्थ न च तत्प्रेत्य नो इह ॥१७-२८॥

**Simple English**

Whatever is sacrificed, given or performed, and whatever self-discipline is practised without faith, it is called 'Asat', O Arjuna; it is nothing here or hereafter (after death).

## Chapter 18 - spiritual freedom Sanyaas Yoga

### 18.1

**Sanskrit**

अर्जुन उवाच ।
संन्यासस्य महाबाहो तत्त्वमिच्छामि वेदितुम् ।
त्यागस्य च हृषीकेश पृथक्केशिनिषूदन ॥१८-१॥

**Simple English**

Arjuna said: I desire to know severally, O mighty-armed, the essence or truth of renunciation, O Hrishikesa, as also of abandonment, O slayer of Kesi.

### 18.2

**Sanskrit**

श्रीभगवानुवाच ।
काम्यानां कर्मणां न्यासं संन्यासं कवयो विदुः ।
सर्वकर्मफलत्यागं प्राहुस्त्यागं विचक्षणाः ॥१८-२॥

**Simple English**

Sri Krishna said: The sages understand Sannyasa to be the renunciation of action with desire; the wise declare the abandonment of the fruits of all actions as Tyaga.

### 18.3

**Sanskrit**

त्याज्यं दोषवदित्येके कर्म प्राहुर्मनीषिणः ।
यज्ञदानतपःकर्म न त्याज्यमिति चापरे ॥१८-३॥

**Simple English**

Some philosophers declare that actions should be abandoned as an evil; while others (declare) that acts of sacrifice, gift and self-discipline should not be relinished.

### 18.4

**Sanskrit**

निश्चयं शृणु मे तत्र त्यागे भरतसत्तम ।
त्यागो हि पुरुषव्याघ्र त्रिविधः सम्प्रकीर्तितः ॥१८-४॥

**Simple English**

Hear from Me the conclusion or the final truth about this abandonment, O best of the Bharatas; abandonment, truly, O best of men, has been declared to be of three kinds.

### 18.5

**Sanskrit**

यज्ञदानतपःकर्म न त्याज्यं कार्यमेव तत् ।
यज्ञो दानं तपश्चैव पावनानि मनीषिणाम् ॥१८-५॥

**Simple English**

Acts of sacrifice, gift and self-discipline should not be abandoned, but should be performed; sacrifice, gift and also self-discipline are the purifiers of the wise.

### 18.6

**Sanskrit**

एतान्यपि तु कर्माणि सङ्गं त्यक्त्वा फलानि च ।
कर्तव्यानीति मे पार्थ निश्चितं मतमुत्तमम् ॥१८-६॥

**Simple English**

But even these actions should be performed leaving aside attachment and the desire for rewards, O Arjuna; this is My certain and best conviction.

### 18.7

**Sanskrit**

नियतस्य तु संन्यासः कर्मणो नोपपद्यते ।
मोहात्तस्य परित्यागस्तामसः परिकीर्तितः ॥१८-७॥

**Simple English**

Truly the renunciation of obligatory action is not proper; the abandonment of the same from confusion is declared to be dark.

### 18.8

**Sanskrit**

दुःखमित्येव यत्कर्म कायक्लेशभयात्त्यजेत् ।
स कृत्वा राजसं त्यागं नैव त्यागफलं लभेत् ॥१८-८॥

**Simple English**

He who abandons action on account of the fear of bodily trouble (because it is painful), does not obtain the merit of renunciation by doing such passionate renunciation.

### 18.9

**Sanskrit**

कार्यमित्येव यत्कर्म नियतं क्रियतेऽर्जुन ।
सङ्गं त्यक्त्वा फलं चैव स त्यागः सात्त्विको मतः ॥१८-९॥

**Simple English**

Whatever obligatory action is done, O Arjuna, merely because it ought to be done, abandoning attachment and also the desire for reward, that renunciation is regarded as pure (pure).

### 18.10

**Sanskrit**

न द्वेष्ट्यकुशलं कर्म कुशले नानुषज्जते ।
त्यागी सत्त्वसमाविष्टो मेधावी छिन्नसंशयः ॥१८-१०॥

**Simple English**

The man of renunciation, pervaded by purity, intelligent, and with his doubts cut asunder, does not hate a disagreeable work nor is he attached to an agreeable one.

### 18.11

**Sanskrit**

न हि देहभृता शक्यं त्यक्तुं कर्माण्यशेषतः ।
यस्तु कर्मफलत्यागी स त्यागीत्यभिधीयते ॥१८-११॥

**Simple English**

Truly, it is not possible for an embodied being to abandon actions entirely; but he who relinishes the rewards of actions is truly called a man of renunciation.

### 18.12

**Sanskrit**

अनिष्टमिष्टं मिश्रं च त्रिविधं कर्मणः फलम् ।
भवत्यत्यागिनां प्रेत्य न तु संन्यासिनां क्वचित् ॥१८-१२॥

**Simple English**

The threefold result of action (evil, good and mixed) accrues after death to the non-abandoners, but never to the abandoners.

### 18.13

**Sanskrit**

पञ्चैतानि महाबाहो कारणानि निबोध मे ।
साङ्ख्ये कृतान्ते प्रोक्तानि सिद्धये सर्वकर्मणाम् ॥१८-१३॥

**Simple English**

Learn from Me, O mighty-armed Arjuna, these five causes as declared in the Sankhya system for the accomplishment of all actions.

### 18.14

**Sanskrit**

अधिष्ठानं तथा कर्ता करणं च पृथग्विधम् ।
विविधाश्च पृथक्चेष्टा दैवं चैवात्र पञ्चमम् ॥१८-१४॥

**Simple English**

There are five causes of any action: the body, the doer, the senses, the different efforts, and the divine factor.

### 18.15

**Sanskrit**

शरीरवाङ्मनोभिर्यत्कर्म प्रारभते नरः ।
न्याय्यं वा विपरीतं वा पञ्चैते तस्य हेतवः ॥१८-१५॥

**Simple English**

Whatever action a man performs with his body, speech and mind whether right or the reverse these five are its causes.

### 18.16

**Sanskrit**

तत्रैवं सति कर्तारमात्मानं केवलं तु यः ।
पश्यत्यकृतबुद्धित्वान्न स पश्यति दुर्मतिः ॥१८-१६॥

**Simple English**

Now, such being the case, truly he who owing to untrained understanding looks upon his Self, which is isolated, as the agent, he of perverted intelligence, sees not.

### 18.17

**Sanskrit**

यस्य नाहंकृतो भावो बुद्धिर्यस्य न लिप्यते ।
हत्वाऽपि स इमाँल्लोकान्न हन्ति न निबध्यते ॥१८-१७॥

**Simple English**

He who is free from the egoistic notion, whose intelligence is not tainted (by good or evil), though he slays these people, he kills not, nor is he bound (by the action).

### 18.18

**Sanskrit**

ज्ञानं ज्ञेयं परिज्ञाता त्रिविधा कर्मचोदना ।
करणं कर्म कर्तेति त्रिविधः कर्मसंग्रहः ॥१८-१८॥

**Simple English**

Knowledge, the knowable and the knower form the threefold impulse to action; the organ, the action and the agent form the threefold basis of action.

### 18.19

**Sanskrit**

ज्ञानं कर्म च कर्ताच त्रिधैव गुणभेदतः ।
प्रोच्यते गुणसङ्ख्याने यथावच्छृणु तान्यपि ॥१८-१९॥

**Simple English**

Knowledge, action and actor are declared in the science of the Gunas (Sankhya philosophy) to be of three kinds only, according to the distinction of the Gunas. Of these also, hear duly.

### 18.20

**Sanskrit**

सर्वभूतेषु येनैकं भावमव्ययमीक्षते ।
अविभक्तं विभक्तेषु तज्ज्ञानं विद्धि सात्त्विकम् ॥१८-२०॥

**Simple English**

That by which one sees the one indestructible Reality in all beings, not separate in all the separate beings know you that knowledge to be pure.

### 18.21

**Sanskrit**

पृथक्त्वेन तु यज्ज्ञानं नानाभावान्पृथग्विधान् ।
वेत्ति सर्वेषु भूतेषु तज्ज्ञानं विद्धि राजसम् ॥१८-२१॥

**Simple English**

But that knowledge which sees in all beings various entities of distinct kinds as different from one another know you that knowledge to be passionate.

### 18.22

**Sanskrit**

यत्तु कृत्स्नवदेकस्मिन्कार्ये सक्तमहैतुकम् ।
अतत्त्वार्थवदल्पं च तत्तामसमुदाहृतम् ॥१८-२२॥

**Simple English**

But that which clings to one single effect as if it were the whole, without reason, without foundation in Truth, and trivial that is declared to be dark.

### 18.23

**Sanskrit**

नियतं सङ्गरहितमरागद्वेषतः कृतम् ।
अफलप्रेप्सुना कर्म यत्तत्सात्त्विकमुच्यते ॥१८-२३॥

**Simple English**

An action which is ordained, which is free from attachment, and which is done without love or hatred by one who is not desirous of any reward that action is declared to be pure.

### 18.24

**Sanskrit**

यत्तु कामेप्सुना कर्म साहंकारेण वा पुनः ।
क्रियते बहुलायासं तद्राजसमुदाहृतम् ॥१८-२४॥

**Simple English**

But that action which is done by one longing for the fulfilment of desires or gain with ego or with much effort that is declared to be passionate (passionate).

### 18.25

**Sanskrit**

अनुबन्धं क्षयं हिंसामनपेक्ष्य च पौरुषम् ।
मोहादारभ्यते कर्म यत्तत्तामसमुच्यते ॥१८-२५॥

**Simple English**

That action which is undertaken from confusion, without a regard for the conseences, loss, injury and (one's own) ability that is declared to be dark (dark).

### 18.26

**Sanskrit**

मुक्तसङ्गोऽनहंवादी धृत्युत्साहसमन्वितः ।
सिद्ध्यसिद्ध्योर्निर्विकारः कर्ता सात्त्विक उच्यते ॥१८-२६॥

**Simple English**

An agent who is free from attachment, non-egoistic, endowed with firmness and enthusiasm, and unaffected by success or failure, is called pure (pure).

### 18.27

**Sanskrit**

रागी कर्मफलप्रेप्सुर्लुब्धो हिंसात्मकोऽशुचिः ।
हर्षशोकान्वितः कर्ता राजसः परिकीर्तितः ॥१८-२७॥

**Simple English**

Passionate, desiring to obtain the reward of actions, greedy, cruel, impure, moved by joy and sorrow, such an agent is said to be passionate (passionate).

### 18.28

**Sanskrit**

अयुक्तः प्राकृतः स्तब्धः शठो नैष्कृतिकोऽलसः ।
विषादी दीर्घसूत्री च कर्ता तामस उच्यते ॥१८-२८॥

**Simple English**

Unsteady, vulgar, unbending, cheating, malicious, lazy, desponding and procrastinating such an agent is called dark.

### 18.29

**Sanskrit**

बुद्धेर्भेदं धृतेश्चैव गुणतस्त्रिविधं शृणु ।
प्रोच्यमानमशेषेण पृथक्त्वेन धनञ्जय ॥१८-२९॥

**Simple English**

Hear you the threefold division of intellect and firmness according to the Gunas, as I declare them fully and distinctly, O Arjuna.

### 18.30

**Sanskrit**

प्रवृत्तिं च निवृत्तिं च कार्याकार्ये भयाभये ।
बन्धं मोक्षं च या वेत्ति बुद्धिः सा पार्थ सात्त्विकी ॥१८-३०॥

**Simple English**

The intellect which knows the path of work and renunciation, what ought to be done and what ought not to be done, fear and fearlessness, bondage and freedom that intellect is pure (pure), O Arjuna.

### 18.31

**Sanskrit**

यया धर्ममधर्मं च कार्यं चाकार्यमेव च ।
अयथावत्प्रजानाति बुद्धिः सा पार्थ राजसी ॥१८-३१॥

**Simple English**

That, by which one wrongly understands Dharma and Adharma and also what ought to be done and what ought not to be done that intellect, O Arjuna, is passionate (passionate).

### 18.32

**Sanskrit**

अधर्मं धर्ममिति या मन्यते तमसावृता ।
सर्वार्थान्विपरीतांश्च बुद्धिः सा पार्थ तामसी ॥१८-३२॥

**Simple English**

That, which, enveloped in darkness, sees Adharma as Dharma and all things perverted that intellect, O Arjuna, is dark (dark).

### 18.33

**Sanskrit**

धृत्या यया धारयते मनःप्राणेन्द्रियक्रियाः ।
योगेनाव्यभिचारिण्या धृतिः सा पार्थ सात्त्विकी ॥१८-३३॥

**Simple English**

The unwavering firmness by which, through Yoga, the functions of the mind, the life-force and the senses are restrained that firmness, O Arjuna, is pure (pure).

### 18.34

**Sanskrit**

यया तु धर्मकामार्थान्धृत्या धारयतेऽर्जुन ।
प्रसङ्गेन फलाकाङ्क्षी धृतिः सा पार्थ राजसी ॥१८-३४॥

**Simple English**

But that, O Arjuna, by which, on account of attachment and desire for reward, one holds fast to Dharma (duty), enjoyment of pleasures and earning of wealth that firmness, O Arjuna, is passionate (passionate).

### 18.35

**Sanskrit**

यया स्वप्नं भयं शोकं विषादं मदमेव च ।
न विमुञ्चति दुर्मेधा धृतिः सा पार्थ तामसी ॥१८-३५॥

**Simple English**

That, by which a stupid man does not abandon sleep, fear, grief, despair and also conceit that firmness, O Arjuna, is dark.

### 18.36

**Sanskrit**

सुखं त्विदानीं त्रिविधं शृणु मे भरतर्षभ ।
अभ्यासाद्रमते यत्र दुःखान्तं च निगच्छति ॥१८-३६॥

**Simple English**

And now hear from Me, O Arjuna, of the threefold pleasure, in which one rejoices by practice and surely comes to the end of pain.

### 18.37

**Sanskrit**

यत्तदग्रे विषमिव परिणामेऽमृतोपमम् ।
तत्सुखं सात्त्विकं प्रोक्तमात्मबुद्धिप्रसादजम् ॥१८-३७॥

**Simple English**

That which is like poison at first but in the end like nectar that happiness is declared to be pure, born of the purity of one's own mind due to Self-realisation.

### 18.38

**Sanskrit**

विषयेन्द्रियसंयोगाद्यत्तदग्रेऽमृतोपमम् ।
परिणामे विषमिव तत्सुखं राजसं स्मृतम् ॥१८-३८॥

**Simple English**

That happiness which arises from the contact of the sense-organs with the objects, which is at first like nectar, and in the end like poison that is declared to be passionate.

### 18.39

**Sanskrit**

यदग्रे चानुबन्धे च सुखं मोहनमात्मनः ।
निद्रालस्यप्रमादोत्थं तत्तामसमुदाहृतम् ॥१८-३९॥

**Simple English**

That happiness which at first as well as in the seel deludes the self, and which arises from sleep, indolence and heedlessness that is declared to be dark.

### 18.40

**Sanskrit**

न तदस्ति पृथिव्यां वा दिवि देवेषु वा पुनः ।
सत्त्वं प्रकृतिजैर्मुक्तं यदेभिः स्यात्त्रिभिर्गुणैः ॥१८-४०॥

**Simple English**

There is no being on earth or again in heaven among the gods, that is free from the three qualities born of Nature.

### 18.41

**Sanskrit**

ब्राह्मणक्षत्रियविशां शूद्राणां च परन्तप ।
कर्माणि प्रविभक्तानि स्वभावप्रभवैर्गुणैः ॥१८-४१॥

**Simple English**

Of Brahmanas, warriors and Vaisyas, as also of Sudras, O Arjuna, the duties are distributed according to the qualities born of their own nature.

### 18.42

**Sanskrit**

शमो दमस्तपः शौचं क्षान्तिरार्जवमेव च ।
ज्ञानं विज्ञानमास्तिक्यं ब्रह्मकर्म स्वभावजम् ॥१८-४२॥

**Simple English**

Serenity, self-restraint, self-discipline, purity, forgiveness and also uprightness, knowledge, realisation and belief in God are the duties of the Brahmanas, born of (their own) nature.

### 18.43

**Sanskrit**

शौर्यं तेजो धृतिर्दाक्ष्यं युद्धे चाप्यपलायनम् ।
दानमीश्वरभावश्च क्षात्रं कर्म स्वभावजम् ॥१८-४३॥

**Simple English**

Prowess, splendour, firmness, dexterity and also not fleeing from battle, generosity and lordliness are the duties of the warriors, born of (their own) nature.

### 18.44

**Sanskrit**

कृषिगौरक्ष्यवाणिज्यं वैश्यकर्म स्वभावजम् ।
परिचर्यात्मकं कर्म शूद्रस्यापि स्वभावजम् ॥१८-४४॥

**Simple English**

Agriculture, cattle-rearing and trade are the duties of the Vaisya (merchant), born of (their own) nature; and action consisting of service is the duty of the Sudra (servant-class), born of (their own) nature.

### 18.45

**Sanskrit**

स्वे स्वे कर्मण्यभिरतः संसिद्धिं लभते नरः ।
स्वकर्मनिरतः सिद्धिं यथा विन्दति तच्छृणु ॥१८-४५॥

**Simple English**

By being devoted to his own work, a person attains perfection. Now hear how one devoted to his own work reaches perfection.

### 18.46

**Sanskrit**

यतः प्रवृत्तिर्भूतानां येन सर्वमिदं ततम् ।
स्वकर्मणा तमभ्यर्च्य सिद्धिं विन्दति मानवः ॥१८-४६॥

**Simple English**

He from Whom all the beings have evolved and by Whom all this is pervaded worshipping Him with his own duty, man attains perfection.

### 18.47

**Sanskrit**

श्रेयान्स्वधर्मो विगुणः परधर्मात्स्वनुष्ठितात् ।
स्वभावनियतं कर्म कुर्वन्नाप्नोति किल्बिषम् ॥१८-४७॥

**Simple English**

Better is one's own duty (though) destitute of merits, than the duty of another well performed. He who does the duty ordained by his own nature incurs no wrongdoing.

### 18.48

**Sanskrit**

सहजं कर्म कौन्तेय सदोषमपि न त्यजेत् ।
सर्वारम्भा हि दोषेण धूमेनाग्निरिवावृताः ॥१८-४८॥

**Simple English**

One should not abandon, O Arjuna, the duty to which one is born, though faulty; for, all undertakings are enveloped by evil, as fire by smoke.

### 18.49

**Sanskrit**

असक्तबुद्धिः सर्वत्र जितात्मा विगतस्पृहः ।
नैष्कर्म्यसिद्धिं परमां संन्यासेनाधिगच्छति ॥१८-४९॥

**Simple English**

He whose intellect is unattached everywhere, who has subdued his self, from whom desire has fled, he by renunciation, attains the supreme state of freedom from action.

### 18.50

**Sanskrit**

सिद्धिं प्राप्तो यथा ब्रह्म तथाप्नोति निबोध मे ।
समासेनैव कौन्तेय निष्ठा ज्ञानस्य या परा ॥१८-५०॥

**Simple English**

Learn from Me in brief, O Arjuna, how he who has attained perfection reaches the Supreme Reality (the Eternal), that supreme state of knowledge.

### 18.51

**Sanskrit**

बुद्ध्या विशुद्धया युक्तो धृत्यात्मानं नियम्य च ।
शब्दादीन्विषयांस्त्यक्त्वा रागद्वेषौ व्युदस्य च ॥१८-५१॥

**Simple English**

Endowed with a pure intellect, controlling the self by firmness, relinishing sound and other objects and abandoning attraction and hatred.

### 18.52

**Sanskrit**

विविक्तसेवी लघ्वाशी यतवाक्कायमानसः ।
ध्यानयोगपरो नित्यं वैराग्यं समुपाश्रितः ॥१८-५२॥

**Simple English**

Dwelling in solitude, eating but little, with speech, body and mind subdued, always engaged in meditation and concentration, resorting to dispassion.

### 18.53

**Sanskrit**

अहंकारं बलं दर्पं कामं क्रोधं परिग्रहम् ।
विमुच्य निर्ममः शान्तो ब्रह्मभूयाय कल्पते ॥१८-५३॥

**Simple English**

Having abandoned ego, strength, arrogance, desire, anger and greed, and free from the notion of 'mine' and peaceful, he is fit for becoming the Supreme Reality.

### 18.54

**Sanskrit**

ब्रह्मभूतः प्रसन्नात्मा न शोचति न काङ्क्षति ।
समः सर्वेषु भूतेषु मद्भक्तिं लभते पराम् ॥१८-५४॥

**Simple English**

Becoming the Supreme Reality, serene in the Self, he neither grieves nor desires, the same to all beings, he obtains supreme devotion to Me.

### 18.55

**Sanskrit**

भक्त्या मामभिजानाति यावान्यश्चास्मि तत्त्वतः ।
ततो मां तत्त्वतो ज्ञात्वा विशते तदनन्तरम् ॥१८-५५॥

**Simple English**

By devotion he knows Me in truth, what and who I am; then having known Me in truth, he at once enters into the Supreme.

### 18.56

**Sanskrit**

सर्वकर्माण्यपि सदा कुर्वाणो मद्व्यपाश्रयः ।
मत्प्रसादादवाप्नोति शाश्वतं पदमव्ययम् ॥१८-५६॥

**Simple English**

Doing all actions always having taken refuge in Me, by My grace he obtains the eternal indestructible state of being.

### 18.57

**Sanskrit**

चेतसा सर्वकर्माणि मयि संन्यस्य मत्परः ।
बुद्धियोगमुपाश्रित्य मच्चित्तः सततं भव ॥१८-५७॥

**Simple English**

Mentally renouncing all actions in Me, having Me as the highest goal, resorting to the Yoga of clear understanding do you ever fix your mind on Me.

### 18.58

**Sanskrit**

मच्चित्तः सर्वदुर्गाणि मत्प्रसादात्तरिष्यसि ।
अथ चेत्त्वमहंकारान्न श्रोष्यसि विनङ्क्ष्यसि ॥१८-५८॥

**Simple English**

Fixing your mind on Me, you will by My grace overcome all obstacles; but if from ego you will not hear Me, you will perish.

### 18.59
